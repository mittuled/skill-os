## Executive Summary

[2-3 sentences summarizing the sprint goal, total capacity, utilisation, and key risks.
GUIDANCE: Lead with the sprint goal and the primary outcome the team is committing to.]

## Sprint Goal

[One sentence describing the sprint's primary outcome.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Users can create, assign, and enforce custom roles within their workspace."
- Bad: "Complete RBAC stories"
- Format: Outcome-oriented statement that is testable at sprint end]

## Capacity Table

[Per-person availability for the sprint.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table with Team Member, Available Days, Meeting Overhead, On-Call Days, Net Days, Estimated SP
- Bad: "Team has about 30 points"
- Format: Markdown table with one row per person, totals row at bottom]

## Sprint Backlog

[Selected stories in dependency-sequenced order.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table with Sequence, Story ID, Title, Estimate (SP), Owner, Dependencies, Risk Flag
- Bad: List of story titles
- Format: Markdown table ordered by sequence number, risk flags in last column]

## Risk Annotations

[Stories flagged for external dependencies, uncertain estimates, or high complexity.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "T-003 (Permission Catalogue Integration): External dependency on User Service team. Expected delivery: Sprint Day 3. Contingency: If delayed, work on T-005 (UI scaffolding) first and integrate when available."
- Bad: "Some stories are risky"
- Format: Per-story risk note with dependency, contingency plan]

## Capacity Utilisation

[Summary of how capacity is allocated.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Total capacity: 27 SP. Committed: 21 SP (78%). Buffer: 6 SP (22%). Buffer covers contingency for T-003 (dependency risk) and T-006 (uncertain estimate)."
- Bad: "We have room"
- Format: Labeled values with buffer justification]

## Recommendations

[Actions for sprint success.
GUIDANCE: Each recommendation should be:
- Specific (not "watch risks" but "check User Service team delivery status on Day 2 and escalate to EM if delayed")
- Actionable (assignable to a person/team)
- Prioritized (P1/P2/P3)]
