# Framework: User Research

## Core Model

Semi-structured qualitative research designed to answer a specific product decision question. Follows a plan-recruit-interview-analyse-synthesise cycle producing actionable insights ranked by frequency and impact.

## Research Objective Pattern

Every study must state:
- **Decision**: What product decision will this research inform?
- **Question**: What do we need to learn to make that decision?
- **Scope**: One objective per study (no omnibus research)

## Interview Guide Structure

| Section | Duration | Question Type | Example |
|---------|----------|--------------|---------|
| Warm-up | 5 min | Context-setting | "Tell me about your role and how you use [product area] today." |
| Behaviour | 15 min | Behavioural recall | "Walk me through the last time you [relevant activity]." |
| Pain points | 10 min | Problem exploration | "What was the hardest part of that experience?" |
| Needs | 5 min | Unmet need probing | "If you could change one thing about how you do this, what would it be?" |
| Wrap-up | 5 min | Open-ended | "Is there anything I should have asked about but didn't?" |

## Participant Criteria

- **Sample size**: 5-8 participants (saturation point for qualitative research)
- **Screening**: Short questionnaire confirming participant matches target profile
- **Diversity**: Mix of experience levels, company sizes, or use cases relevant to the research question
- **Anti-bias**: Avoid over-indexing on power users or internal colleagues

## Analysis Method: Affinity Mapping

1. Extract observations from each interview (one per sticky note / row)
2. Tag each observation: stated preference vs. revealed behaviour (weight behaviour higher)
3. Cluster into themes
4. Count frequency per theme across participants
5. Note divergence: where findings split by segment or role

## Insight Format

```
We observed [specific behaviour or pattern across N of M participants]
because [underlying motivation or mental model].
This means we should [specific product implication or recommendation].
```

## Quality Gates

- [ ] Research objective tied to a specific product decision
- [ ] No leading questions in the interview guide
- [ ] 5+ participants matching the target profile
- [ ] Stated preferences distinguished from revealed behaviours
- [ ] Disconfirming evidence included, not dropped
- [ ] Each insight supported by evidence from 2+ participants
