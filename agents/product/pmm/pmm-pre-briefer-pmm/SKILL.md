# pmm-pre-briefer-pmm

## Agent: PMM
L2 product marketing manager responsible for competitive intelligence, positioning, GTM planning, pricing strategy, launch narrative, and sales enablement.

Department ethos: [ideal-product.md](../../../../departments/product/ideal-product.md)

## Skill Description
Prepares and delivers the PMM briefing pack to sales and CS before a launch, ensuring field teams understand the narrative, positioning, objection handling, and competitive context before they face buyers.

## When to Use
- A product launch is one to two weeks away and sales and CS need a structured walkthrough.
- A major positioning or pricing change requires synchronous communication beyond an async brief.
- A Tier 1 launch where the enablement brief alone is insufficient and a live briefing session is warranted.
- New hires in sales or CS need a catch-up briefing on recent launches they missed.
- A competitive development requires urgent field preparation before upcoming deal meetings.

## Workflow
1. Assemble the briefing pack. Pull together the launch narrative, enablement brief, updated battle cards, pricing/packaging summary, and FAQ document.
2. Structure the briefing agenda: positioning overview (five minutes), demo or walkthrough (ten minutes), objection handling (ten minutes), competitive context (five minutes), Q&A (ten minutes).
3. Prepare a one-page cheat sheet that reps can reference during calls. Include the value prop one-liner, three key proof points, top three objections with responses, and competitive differentiators.
4. Schedule the briefing session. Coordinate with sales and CS leadership for a time that maximises attendance. Record the session for those who cannot attend live.
5. Deliver the briefing. Lead with what is new and why it matters to the buyer, not the product team. Emphasise the selling motion, not the feature spec.
6. Run a live objection-handling exercise. Present common buyer objections and have reps practise responses using the approved talk track.
7. Distribute the briefing pack and recording immediately after the session. Pin the cheat sheet in the sales channel.
8. Collect feedback from attendees: was the briefing useful, what was unclear, what objections are they still worried about. Use feedback to refine the enablement brief if needed.

## Anti-Patterns
- **Delivering the briefing after launch day.** *Why: Reps who have already talked to buyers with incorrect or missing information cannot undo those conversations.*
- **Reading the enablement brief aloud instead of briefing.** *Why: A briefing should add context, emphasis, and practice that a document alone cannot provide; reading it aloud wastes everyone's time.*
- **Skipping the objection-handling exercise.** *Why: Knowing the talk track intellectually is different from being able to deliver it under pressure; practice builds muscle memory.*
- **Not recording the session.** *Why: In a distributed team, not everyone can attend live; an unrecorded briefing creates an information asymmetry that hurts reps in different time zones.*

## Output

**Success:**
- Briefing session delivered at least five business days before launch with recorded session available within 24 hours.
- One-page cheat sheet distributed and pinned in the sales channel.
- Post-briefing feedback collected and incorporated into the enablement brief.

**Failure:**
- Briefing delivered after launch day or so close to launch that reps cannot absorb the material.
- No recording available for absent team members.
- Briefing focused on product features rather than selling motion and buyer outcomes.
