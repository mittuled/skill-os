# Framework: Go-to-Market Planning

Reference framework for structuring GTM plans with consistent methodology across launches.

## Launch Tier Classification

| Tier | Criteria | Resource Level | Timeline |
|------|----------|---------------|----------|
| Tier 1 | New product, major repositioning, new market entry | Full cross-functional: PMM, sales, CS, marketing, product, design, PR | 8-12 weeks pre-launch |
| Tier 2 | Major feature, pricing/packaging change, vertical expansion | PMM + sales + marketing | 4-6 weeks pre-launch |
| Tier 3 | Minor feature, iteration, incremental improvement | Product-led with lightweight PMM support | 1-2 weeks pre-launch |

## GTM Plan Structure

Every GTM plan covers these sections:

### 1. Objective & Success Metrics

| Metric Type | Examples | When to Measure |
|-------------|---------|-----------------|
| Leading (early signal) | Email sign-ups, waitlist size, demo requests, blog traffic | Launch week |
| Lagging (outcome) | Pipeline generated, activation rate, revenue, expansion | 30-90 days post-launch |

### 2. Audience Definition

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| ICP | Firmographic and technographic profile of the target company |
| Economic Buyer | Person who signs the contract; cares about ROI, risk, strategic fit |
| Technical Evaluator | Person who assesses product capability; cares about integration, performance |
| End User Champion | Person who will use the product daily; cares about UX, workflow fit |

### 3. Channel Map

Map channels to buyer journey stages:

| Stage | Channel | Purpose | Owner |
|-------|---------|---------|-------|
| Awareness | Blog, social, PR, paid | Generate attention and interest | Marketing |
| Consideration | Webinar, demo, case study, analyst brief | Build credibility and educate | PMM + Marketing |
| Decision | Sales deck, proposal, trial, pricing page | Convert interest to pipeline | Sales + PMM |
| Activation | In-app, email onboarding, CS outreach | Drive first-value moment | Product + CS |
| Expansion | CS playbook, usage triggers, upsell sequence | Grow account value | CS + Sales |

### 4. Launch Sequence Template

| Phase | Timing | Activities |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Pre-launch tease | T-2 weeks | Internal enablement, social teaser, waitlist/early access, analyst pre-brief |
| Launch day | T+0 | Blog, email, in-app, social, PR embargo lift, sales sequence activation |
| Launch week sustain | T+1 to T+5 days | Customer story drop, demo video, webinar, partner co-marketing |
| Post-launch nurture | T+2 to T+8 weeks | Nurture campaigns, retargeting, case study development, win/loss tracking |

### 5. Risk Register

| Risk Category | Example | Mitigation |
|---------------|---------|------------|
| Product readiness | Feature not deployed by launch date | Confirm deploy date T-5 days; have contingency launch date |
| Asset readiness | Design assets not complete | Stagger asset creation; prioritise hero visual and email first |
| Partner dependency | Co-marketing partner delays their post | Send assets T-7 days; have owned-channel backup plan |
| Competitive timing | Competitor launches same week | Monitor competitor signals; prepare rapid-response brief |
| Channel performance | Primary channel underperforms | Allocate 20% budget reserve for mid-launch channel shift |

## GTM Checklist (Pre-Launch Readiness)

- [ ] Positioning document finalised and approved
- [ ] Launch narrative brief distributed to all content creators
- [ ] Sales enablement brief delivered and training session scheduled
- [ ] Pricing/packaging finalised and approved by finance
- [ ] Marketing assets staged (blog, email, social, in-app)
- [ ] PR/comms queued (press release, analyst briefings)
- [ ] CRM fields updated for new product/feature tracking
- [ ] SDR sequences loaded and reviewed
- [ ] Success metrics defined with measurement plan
- [ ] Risk register reviewed and mitigations confirmed
