## Launch Context

[Background on what is launching and why.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "WorkflowPro 2.0 introduces real-time collaboration (Tier 1 launch, target: April 15). Positioning reference: /positioning/v3.md. Target audience: RevOps teams at mid-market SaaS companies (50-500 employees)."

## Headline & Sub-Headline

[The approved launch messaging.

GUIDANCE:
- Headline: Under 12 words, concrete, benefit-oriented, passes the "would a buyer forward this?" test
- Sub-headline: Expands the headline with specificity and persona relevance
- Good headline: "Close deals 40% faster with real-time pricing intelligence"
- Bad headline: "Introducing the next generation of our platform"]

**Headline**: [12 words max]

**Sub-headline**: [One sentence expanding with specificity]

## Three-Act Narrative

### Act 1: The Problem
[The status quo that this launch disrupts. Frame as a before/after.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "RevOps teams spend 6 hours per week manually reconciling pricing across spreadsheets, CRM, and billing systems. By the time a quote is approved, the deal momentum has stalled."
- Bad: "Pricing is hard."
- Format: 2-3 paragraphs establishing tension and urgency]

### Act 2: The Solution
[What the product does and how it works, in buyer-centric language.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "WorkflowPro 2.0 connects your CRM, billing, and pricing rules into a single live workspace. When a rep configures a deal, pricing is validated instantly — no spreadsheet, no approval bottleneck, no waiting."
- Bad: "We built a real-time WebSocket integration with bidirectional sync."
- Format: 2-3 paragraphs focusing on buyer experience, not implementation]

### Act 3: The Proof
[Evidence and momentum.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Customer metric, beta results, analyst validation, or compelling demo moment
- Bad: Internal assertions without external evidence
- Format: Proof points followed by CTA]
