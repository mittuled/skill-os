## Executive Summary

[2-3 sentences covering overall adoption status (green/amber/red), primary metric vs target, and top action item.
GUIDANCE: Lead with the status verdict and the single most important fact.]

## Adoption Status by Metric

[Status table for all tracked metrics.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table with Metric, Target, Actual, Variance %, Status (G/A/R), Trend (↑/→/↓)
- Bad: Narrative paragraphs without numbers
- Format: One row per metric; use Green/Amber/Red in Status column]

## Cohort Breakdown

[Adoption rates segmented by plan tier, geography, or activation cohort.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Separate rows for Free/Pro/Enterprise with activation rate and variance per tier
- Bad: Single aggregate number masking segment-level variation
- Format: Table with Segment, Activated Users, Eligible Users, Activation Rate, Status]

## Trend Analysis

[Direction and rate of change for the primary metric over the last 3+ reporting cycles.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Primary metric has declined 3 points week-over-week for 3 consecutive weeks, now at 34% vs 40% target"
- Bad: "Numbers are going down"
- Format: Trend table or chart description with numeric values per cycle]

## Underperformance Diagnoses

[Root-cause hypotheses for any Amber or Red metric.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Enterprise cohort activation at 18% (target 35%). Hypothesis: permission-gated onboarding step requires admin action; enterprise admins are not completing it. Evidence: funnel drop-off concentrated at step 3 (permission grant)."
- Bad: "Enterprise users are not adopting"
- Format: One hypothesis block per underperforming metric with evidence cited]

## Recommended Actions

[Prioritised interventions with owner and timeline.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "P1: Add contextual prompt for admin permission step (Engineering, Sprint 24). P2: Send targeted email to enterprise admins who have not completed step 3 (Growth, this week)."
- Bad: "We should look into this"
- Format: Ranked list with priority, action, owner, due date]
