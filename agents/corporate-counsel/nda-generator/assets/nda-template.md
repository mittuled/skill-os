## Preamble

This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [EFFECTIVE DATE] ("Effective Date") by and between:

**[PARTY A FULL LEGAL NAME]**, a [STATE/COUNTRY] [ENTITY TYPE] with its principal place of business at [ADDRESS] ("**[CUSTOMIZE: Disclosing Party / Party A / Company]**"),

and

**[PARTY B FULL LEGAL NAME]**, a [STATE/COUNTRY] [ENTITY TYPE] with its principal place of business at [ADDRESS] ("**[CUSTOMIZE: Receiving Party / Party B / Recipient]**").

[ANNOTATION: This section identifies who is bound by the agreement. The party names must match their official legal registration exactly — using a trade name instead of the legal entity name can make the agreement unenforceable.]

## Recitals

WHEREAS, the parties wish to explore [CUSTOMIZE: a potential business relationship / partnership / transaction / employment] (the "Purpose"); and

WHEREAS, in connection with the Purpose, [CUSTOMIZE: one or both parties] may disclose certain confidential and proprietary information to the other party;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

[ANNOTATION: Recitals explain why the agreement exists. The "Purpose" is important — it limits how the receiving party can use your confidential information. A narrow purpose provides stronger protection.]

## 1. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by [CUSTOMIZE: the Disclosing Party / either party] to [CUSTOMIZE: the Receiving Party / the other party], whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by inspection, including but not limited to:

(a) technical information, including research, inventions, designs, specifications, source code, algorithms, and product plans;
(b) business information, including financial data, business plans, customer lists, marketing strategies, and pricing;
(c) trade secrets as defined under applicable law; and
(d) any other information designated as confidential at the time of disclosure or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential.

[CUSTOMIZE: For written/electronic disclosures, information must be marked "Confidential" at the time of disclosure. For oral disclosures, the disclosing party must identify the information as confidential at the time of disclosure and confirm in writing within [30] days.]

[ANNOTATION: This defines what is protected. The broader the definition, the more protection for the disclosing party — but overly broad definitions can be hard to enforce. The marking requirement helps both parties track what is covered.]

## 2. Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) was publicly available at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;
(b) was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as documented by written records;
(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information;
(d) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure; or
(e) is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice (where legally permitted) and cooperates with efforts to obtain a protective order.

[ANNOTATION: These are standard carve-outs that every NDA includes. Without them, you could be liable for "disclosing" information you already knew or that anyone could find publicly. The legal compulsion exception (e) ensures you are not forced to choose between breaking the NDA and breaking the law.]

## 3. Obligations of Receiving Party

The Receiving Party shall:

(a) use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
(b) restrict disclosure to its employees, agents, and advisors who (i) have a need to know and (ii) are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than this Agreement;
(c) protect the Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;
(d) not reverse-engineer, decompile, or disassemble any products or materials containing Confidential Information; and
(e) promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any unauthorized disclosure or use.

[ANNOTATION: This is the core of the agreement — what the receiving party must and must not do. The "same degree of care" standard ties the obligation to the receiving party's own practices, but the "reasonable care" floor ensures a minimum standard regardless.]

## 4. Term and Termination

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and remain in effect for a period of [CUSTOMIZE: 1/2/3] year(s), unless terminated earlier by either party upon [CUSTOMIZE: 30] days' written notice.

The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination and continue for a period of [CUSTOMIZE: 2/3/5] years from the date of each disclosure, provided that obligations relating to trade secrets shall survive for as long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

[ANNOTATION: There are two time periods here — how long you can share information (the Agreement term) and how long it must be kept secret (the confidentiality term). The confidentiality term always outlasts the Agreement term. Trade secrets get indefinite protection because their value depends on secrecy.]

## 5. Return or Destruction of Materials

Upon termination of this Agreement or upon the Disclosing Party's written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly:

(a) return or destroy all Confidential Information and all copies, summaries, and extracts thereof; and
(b) certify in writing that it has complied with this obligation.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party may retain (i) copies required by applicable law or regulation and (ii) archival copies in routine electronic backup systems, in each case subject to the continuing confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

[ANNOTATION: When the relationship ends, confidential materials must be returned or destroyed. The exceptions are practical — you cannot selectively delete from backup tapes, and some regulations require record retention. But even retained copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations.]

## 6. Remedies

The parties acknowledge that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting any bond, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

[ANNOTATION: This clause allows the disclosing party to get a court order stopping a breach immediately, without first having to prove exactly how much money was lost. This is critical because once confidential information is disclosed, the damage cannot be undone by a later money judgment.]

## 7. General Provisions

**7.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of [CUSTOMIZE: STATE/COUNTRY], without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

**7.2 Dispute Resolution.** [CUSTOMIZE: Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved by (binding arbitration under the rules of [ARBITRATION BODY] / litigation in the courts of [JURISDICTION]).]

**7.3 Assignment.** This Agreement may not be assigned by either party without the prior written consent of the other party.

**7.4 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.

**7.5 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**7.6 No Waiver.** No failure to exercise, or delay in exercising, any right hereunder shall constitute a waiver thereof.

**7.7 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.

**7.8 No Obligation to Disclose.** Nothing in this Agreement obligates either party to disclose any Confidential Information.

**7.9 No License.** Nothing in this Agreement grants any license or right to any patent, copyright, trade secret, or other intellectual property right of either party.

[ANNOTATION: These are standard "boilerplate" provisions, but each serves a specific purpose. Governing law determines which state or country's rules apply if there is a dispute. Severability ensures that if one clause is struck down, the rest of the agreement survives. The "no license" clause prevents the receiving party from claiming that access to confidential information gives them any IP rights.]
