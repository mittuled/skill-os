# Scoring Rubric: freelancer-contract-reviewer

Evaluates freelancer and contractor agreements on two axes: worker classification risk and contractual risk.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Behavioral Control | 15% | Degree to which the contract grants the company control over how, when, and where work is performed |
| 2 | Financial Control | 15% | Degree to which the contract structures financial arrangements as employment-like (fixed salary, expense reimbursement, no profit/loss opportunity) |
| 3 | Relationship Type | 20% | Nature of the ongoing relationship including exclusivity, term, benefits, and integration into business |
| 4 | IP Protection | 15% | Strength of IP provisions including work product ownership, background IP preservation, and licensing scope |
| 5 | Liability Allocation | 15% | Fairness of indemnification, limitation of liability, and warranty provisions |
| 6 | Termination Provisions | 10% | Adequacy of termination rights, notice periods, kill fees, and payment for completed work |
| 7 | Payment Terms | 10% | Clarity and fairness of rate, schedule, invoicing, and late payment provisions |
| **Total** | | **100%** | |

> **Axis mapping**: Criteria 1-3 = Classification Risk (50% total). Criteria 4-7 = Contractual Risk (50% total).

## Scale

Each criterion is scored **0-10**:
- **0**: No evidence / completely absent
- **5**: Partially present with significant gaps
- **10**: Fully present, comprehensive, no gaps

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score x weight)

> **Note**: For Classification Risk criteria (1-3), a **higher** score means **lower** risk (i.e., the contract correctly structures the relationship as independent contractor). A score of 10 means the contract has zero classification risk indicators on that criterion.

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Low Risk | Contract clearly structures an independent contractor relationship with strong protections for both parties | Approve with minor edits; no classification concern |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Minimal Risk | Strong contractor structure with minor gaps in protections that do not create material risk | Approve with noted improvements; no classification concern |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Moderate Risk | Generally sound but several provisions need strengthening; some classification indicators present | Modify flagged provisions before signing; document business rationale for any E-like factors |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Elevated Risk | Multiple classification red flags or significant contractual gaps; relationship may not withstand IRS scrutiny | Substantial revisions required; legal review of actual working arrangement mandatory |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | High Risk | Contract describes an employment relationship using contractor language; significant contractual imbalance | Do not sign; restructure as employment or fundamentally redesign engagement terms |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Critical Risk | Contract creates near-certain misclassification liability and/or exposes company to severe contractual risk | Reject immediately; engage employment counsel before proceeding with any engagement |

## Signal Tables

### Behavioral Control

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | No schedule requirements, worker determines methods and tools, no training required, deliverable-based milestones only, no reporting beyond milestone delivery |
| 7-8 | Minimal coordination requirements (e.g., weekly check-in), worker chooses methods, no mandatory training, progress updates at milestones |
| 5-6 | Some schedule requirements justified by collaboration needs, light guidance on methods, occasional required meetings |
| 3-4 | Fixed core hours, company-directed methods, required attendance at team meetings, regular progress reports |
| 0-2 | Full-time fixed schedule, detailed instructions on how to perform work, mandatory training, daily standups, same supervision as employees |

### Financial Control

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Project-based or deliverable-based fee, worker invoices independently, no expense reimbursement, worker bears own business costs, genuine profit/loss opportunity |
| 7-8 | Milestone-based payments, limited expense reimbursement for pre-approved costs only, worker uses own equipment |
| 5-6 | Hourly rate with some project fee elements, moderate expense reimbursement, some company-provided tools |
| 3-4 | Regular hourly/weekly pay on fixed schedule, broad expense reimbursement, company provides primary tools and equipment |
| 0-2 | Salary-equivalent regular payments, full expense reimbursement, all tools and equipment provided, benefits offered, no profit/loss opportunity |

### Relationship Type

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Non-exclusive, fixed project term, worker serves multiple clients, no integration into company org structure, no company email/title, no benefits |
| 7-8 | Non-exclusive, defined term with single renewal option, worker has other clients, minimal integration, no company benefits |
| 5-6 | Soft exclusivity (priority to company), longer term (6-12 months), limited other clients, some integration (team Slack channel, project meetings) |
| 3-4 | Practical exclusivity (full-time hours), indefinite or auto-renewing term, company email provided, included in team meetings and org chart |
| 0-2 | Contractual exclusivity, indefinite term, full integration into company (email, title, org chart, all-hands), benefits or benefit-equivalents provided |

### IP Protection

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Clear work product assignment with background IP carve-out, pre-existing IP listed, license-back for worker's portfolio use, moral rights addressed |
| 7-8 | Work product assignment with general background IP preservation, no pre-existing IP inventory but carve-out clause exists |
| 5-6 | Work product assignment but background IP carve-out vague, no pre-existing IP identification, no portfolio use rights |
| 3-4 | Overly broad IP assignment covering pre-existing materials, no background IP protection, "all work" assignment language |
| 0-2 | No IP clause, or IP clause assigns all worker's IP (including pre-existing) to company, no background IP preservation |

### Liability Allocation

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Mutual indemnification with reasonable scope, liability capped at fees paid, mutual warranty disclaimers, insurance requirements proportionate to engagement |
| 7-8 | Mostly mutual indemnification, liability cap at 2x fees, reasonable warranty provisions |
| 5-6 | Indemnification tilted toward company, liability cap at elevated level or missing for some obligations, limited warranty provisions |
| 3-4 | One-sided indemnification favoring company, no liability cap for worker, broad worker warranties |
| 0-2 | Unlimited worker liability, fully one-sided indemnification, worker warrants outcomes company controls, no company warranties |

### Termination Provisions

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Mutual termination for convenience with 14-30 day notice, kill fee for early termination, payment for all completed work, clear deliverable handover process |
| 7-8 | Mutual termination with 30-day notice, payment for completed work, no kill fee but reasonable notice |
| 5-6 | Company-favorable termination (shorter company notice, longer worker notice), partial payment for completed work |
| 3-4 | Company can terminate at will, worker has limited termination rights, no payment for work-in-progress |
| 0-2 | Company can terminate immediately without payment, worker cannot terminate, no provision for completed work |

### Payment Terms

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Clear rate/fee, Net-30 or better, late payment penalty, detailed invoicing process, no contingent payment, milestone definitions clear |
| 7-8 | Clear rate, Net-30, invoicing process defined, minor ambiguity in milestone triggers |
| 5-6 | Rate clear but Net-45-60, no late payment penalty, invoicing process informal |
| 3-4 | Rate ambiguous or subject to change, Net-60+, payment contingent on client's customer paying, no invoicing process |
| 0-2 | No defined rate, payment at company discretion, no invoicing terms, payment contingent on undefined conditions |
