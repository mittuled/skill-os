# Review Framework: freelancer-contract-reviewer

Comprehensive framework for analyzing freelancer and contractor agreements including classification tests and analysis lenses.

## IRS 20-Factor Test

Each factor is scored as **Employee-like (E)**, **Neutral (N)**, or **Contractor-like (C)**.

| # | Factor | Employee-like | Neutral | Contractor-like |
|---|--------|--------------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | Instructions | Worker must comply with detailed instructions about when, where, and how to work | Some guidance provided but worker has discretion on methods | Worker determines own methods with minimal instruction |
| 2 | Training | Company provides or requires specific training | Orientation provided but no ongoing training | No training required; worker brings own expertise |
| 3 | Integration | Worker's services integrated into business operations as core function | Services support operations but are not core | Services are supplementary or project-specific |
| 4 | Personal services | Company requires the specific individual to perform the work | Preference for individual but substitution discussed | Worker can delegate or subcontract freely |
| 5 | Hiring assistants | Company hires, supervises, and pays worker's assistants | Shared responsibility for assistants | Worker hires, supervises, and pays own assistants |
| 6 | Continuing relationship | Ongoing relationship with expectation of indefinite continuation | Fixed-term engagement with potential renewal | Single project or defined deliverable with clear end |
| 7 | Set hours | Company sets specific work hours or schedule | Flexible hours with some required availability | Worker sets own schedule entirely |
| 8 | Full-time required | Worker must devote full-time to company's business | Majority of time but not exclusively | Worker serves multiple clients simultaneously |
| 9 | Work on premises | Work performed at company's location or designated site | Hybrid arrangement | Worker chooses location; primarily off-site |
| 10 | Order of work | Company directs sequence and priority of tasks | General milestones set but sequencing flexible | Worker determines own task sequence and priorities |
| 11 | Reports | Regular verbal or written reports required on progress | Milestone check-ins only | Deliverable-based; no progress reporting required |
| 12 | Payment method | Regular salary or hourly wage on fixed schedule | Milestone payments with time component | Project fee, per-deliverable, or commission only |
| 13 | Expenses | Company reimburses business and travel expenses | Some expenses reimbursed, others borne by worker | Worker bears all business expenses |
| 14 | Tools and materials | Company provides tools, equipment, and materials | Some tools provided, worker supplies others | Worker provides all tools and equipment |
| 15 | Investment | Worker has no significant investment in facilities | Minor investment in home office or basic tools | Significant investment in own facilities and equipment |
| 16 | Profit or loss | No opportunity for profit or loss; guaranteed pay | Limited profit/loss exposure through bonuses/penalties | Genuine opportunity for profit or loss based on performance |
| 17 | Multiple clients | Works exclusively or primarily for one company | Primarily one client but some others | Serves multiple clients regularly |
| 18 | Public availability | Services not offered to the general public | Limited marketing of services | Actively markets services to the general public |
| 19 | Right to discharge | Company can fire worker at will without cause | Termination with notice period and defined triggers | Termination only for breach of contract terms |
| 20 | Right to quit | Worker can quit at any time without liability | Worker can quit with notice period | Worker obligated to complete project; early departure triggers liability |

**Scoring**: Count E, N, and C factors. 12+ Employee-like factors = High classification risk. 8-11 = Moderate risk. 7 or fewer = Low risk.

## ABC Test (California AB5)

A worker is an employee unless the hiring entity demonstrates all three conditions:

| Prong | Test | Employee If | Contractor If |
|-------|------|-------------|---------------|
| A | Control | Worker is under the control and direction of the hiring entity in performing the work | Worker is free from control and direction in performing the work, both contractually and in fact |
| B | Business scope | Work is within the usual course of the hiring entity's business | Work is outside the usual course of the hiring entity's business |
| C | Independent trade | Worker does not customarily engage in an independently established trade or business of the same nature | Worker is customarily engaged in an independently established trade, occupation, or business of the same nature |

**Scoring**: Failure on any single prong = Employee classification under California law.

## 14 Freelancer Analysis Lenses

| # | Lens | What to Evaluate | Red Flags |
|---|------|-------------------|-----------|
| 1 | IP Ownership | Who owns work product? Background IP preserved? | "All work product belongs to Company" without carve-out for pre-existing IP |
| 2 | Work Product | Deliverable definitions, acceptance criteria, revision limits | Undefined deliverables, unlimited revision obligations |
| 3 | Payment Terms | Rate, schedule, net terms, late payment penalties | Net-90+, no late payment penalty, payment contingent on client's customer paying |
| 4 | Expense Reimbursement | Which expenses covered, approval process, caps | No expense reimbursement for required travel; worker bears all costs (E-like) |
| 5 | Insurance Requirements | Professional liability, general liability, workers' comp | Company-provided insurance (E-like); no insurance requirements (risk exposure) |
| 6 | Non-Compete | Scope, duration, geography, industry | Broad non-compete exceeding 12 months or covering worker's primary industry |
| 7 | Termination | Notice period, cause triggers, kill fee, completed work payment | No kill fee for early termination; no payment for completed-but-undelivered work |
| 8 | Confidentiality | Scope, duration, exceptions, return of materials | Perpetual confidentiality with no exceptions; scope broader than engagement |
| 9 | Liability | Indemnification, limitation of liability, warranty disclaimers | Unlimited worker liability; one-sided indemnification |
| 10 | Tax Obligations | 1099 classification, tax withholding, reporting | Company withholds taxes (E-like); no tax classification stated |
| 11 | Exclusivity | Can worker serve other clients during engagement? | Full exclusivity + full-time hours = high misclassification risk |
| 12 | Work Schedule | Who controls when work is performed? | Fixed 9-5 schedule, mandatory meetings, required on-site presence (all E-like) |
| 13 | Tool Provision | Who provides equipment, software, and workspace? | Company provides laptop, software licenses, office space (E-like) |
| 14 | Business Integration | Is worker integrated into company's organizational structure? | Company email address, org chart inclusion, title within company, team meetings (E-like) |

## Worker Classification Risk Matrix

| Risk Level | Indicator Combination | Recommended Action |
|------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Critical | Exclusivity + fixed schedule + company tools + 6+ month term | Restructure as employment or fundamentally change engagement terms |
| High | 12+ Employee-like IRS factors OR ABC test prong failure | Legal review required; modify contract to reduce E-like factors |
| Moderate | 8-11 Employee-like IRS factors, some red flag lenses triggered | Modify specific provisions; document business justification |
| Low | 7 or fewer E-like factors, no red flag lenses triggered | Standard review; proceed with minor adjustments |

## Common Misclassification Patterns

### The Exclusivity Paradox
Requiring exclusivity while calling the worker "independent" directly contradicts the independence element of contractor status. If the worker cannot serve other clients, they are economically dependent — a hallmark of employment.

### The Tool Provision Trap
Providing company laptop, software licenses, email address, and office access creates the infrastructure of employment. Each tool provided shifts one more IRS factor toward Employee-like classification.

### The Schedule Control Creep
Starts with "core hours for meetings" and gradually expands to a full work schedule. Document any schedule requirements and ensure they are minimal, justified by coordination needs, and do not resemble an employee's fixed schedule.
