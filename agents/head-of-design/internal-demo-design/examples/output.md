## Executive Summary

The internal demo for the onboarding redesign concept direction was completed with four stakeholders. Two concept directions were presented — stepped onboarding and conversational onboarding — along with user research synthesis from five usability test sessions. Directional feedback favored Concept A (stepped) with scope reduction. Two action items were identified: reducing the stepped flow from 7 to 4 steps, and an engineering feasibility assessment for the conversational component. Three open questions remain for product to resolve before the definition phase can be locked.

## Feedback by Category

### Usability Concerns
- Stepped approach felt too long in user tests — 7 steps is too many

### Engineering Feasibility Flags
- Conversational format requires new chat-style component not in the design system

### Positive Validation
- Both concepts successfully surface the core value prop within the first 2 screens

## Action Items

| Action | Owner | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Reduce stepped flow to 4 steps maximum | Visual Interaction Designer | Open |
| Engineering to assess effort for conversational component | Engineering Lead | Open |

## Open Questions for Resolution

1. Should the onboarding flow collect company size and role upfront or defer to in-product prompts?
2. Is the conversational format feasible within the current front-end framework?
3. What is the minimum viable onboarding for the enterprise self-serve tier?

**Owner for resolution:** CPO to confirm data collection requirements; Engineering Lead to confirm feasibility by end of week.

## Next Steps

Design will proceed with Concept A (stepped, reduced to 4 steps) as the primary direction, pending engineering feasibility confirmation. If conversational format is assessed as low-effort, a hybrid approach will be evaluated in the next exploration round.
