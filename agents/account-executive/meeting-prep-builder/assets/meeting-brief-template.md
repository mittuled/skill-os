## 1. Company Snapshot

[Company overview in 5-7 bullet points.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Series B SaaS company, 200 employees, $15M ARR, selling into mid-market financial services"
- Bad: "Founded in 2019 in San Francisco, California, the company has grown to..."
- Format: Bullet list, facts only, no narrative]

## 2. Attendee Profiles

| Name | Title | Role in Deal | Previous Interactions | Key Fact |
|------|-------|-------------|----------------------|----------|
| [Name] | [Title] | [Champion / Economic Buyer / Influencer / Blocker / End User] | [Last meeting date, topic] | [One relevant fact] |

[GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Maria Chen — VP Engineering — Economic Buyer — Met 2024-01-15 (demo) — Concerned about migration timeline"
- Bad: "Maria Chen works at the company as VP Engineering"
- Format: Table with one row per attendee, ordered by influence on the deal]

## 3. Deal History

[Timeline of key deal milestones and current status.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "First touch 2024-01-03 (inbound). Demo 2024-01-15. Proposal sent 2024-02-01 ($85K ACV). Stalled 2024-02-15 — budget review. Re-engaged 2024-03-01 after Q1 budget approval."
- Bad: "We have been in touch with this account for a few months."
- Format: Chronological bullet list with dates. Flag stalls, changes, or open items.]

## 4. Competitive Landscape

[Known competitors and positioning.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Prospect evaluated CompetitorX in Q4 — liked their reporting but found pricing opaque. Our advantage: transparent pricing and faster implementation."
- Bad: "There are several competitors in this space."
- Format: Per-competitor bullet with strength, weakness, and your counter-position. Reference battle cards if available.]

## 5. Recent News

[Relevant company and industry news from last 30 days.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "2024-03-10: Announced $20M Series C. 2024-03-05: New CTO hired from CompetitorY. Industry: FINRA released new compliance guidance affecting mid-market firms."
- Bad: Listing every press release the company has ever published.
- Format: Date-stamped bullets. Only include news that could affect the conversation.]

## 6. Pain Points

| Pain Point | Status | Evidence Source | Priority |
|-----------|--------|----------------|----------|
| [Specific pain] | [Confirmed / Hypothesized] | [Meeting notes date / Email / Industry pattern] | [P1 / P2 / P3] |

[GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Manual compliance reporting taking 40 hours/month (confirmed — discovery call 2024-01-15)"
- Bad: "They probably have problems with efficiency"
- Format: Table. Always distinguish confirmed from hypothesized. Never present a hypothesis as fact.]

## 7. Value Propositions

[Mapped to each pain point with quantification.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Pain: 40hrs/month manual compliance → Our solution: automated reporting reduces to 4hrs/month → Proof: CustomerX achieved 90% reduction in first quarter"
- Bad: "Our product is great at compliance"
- Format: Pain → Solution → Proof structure for each proposition]

## 8. Objection Prep

| Objection | Category | Response | Evidence |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|
| [Expected objection] | [Product / Price / Timeline / Risk / Competition] | [Prepared response] | [Case study, data point, or proof] |

[GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Objection: 'Your price is 30% higher than CompetitorX' → Response: 'When you factor in implementation cost and time-to-value, total 18-month cost is 15% lower. Here is the TCO comparison we built for CustomerY.'"
- Bad: "They might object to pricing. We should say we are worth it."
- Format: Table with specific objections, not vague categories.]
