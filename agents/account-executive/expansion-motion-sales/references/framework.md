# Framework: Expansion Sales Motion

## Purpose

Provides the structured methodology for identifying, qualifying, and executing expansion opportunities within existing accounts.

## Expansion Signal Identification

### Signal Categories

| Signal Type | Source | Threshold | Expansion Type |
|-------------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Usage ceiling | Product analytics | >80% of tier limit for 30+ days | Tier upgrade |
| Seat utilization | License management | >90% seats consumed | Seat expansion |
| New department request | CS notes, support tickets | Named department + use case | Cross-sell |
| Feature request pattern | Product feedback, SE notes | 3+ requests for premium feature | Tier upgrade |
| Contract milestone | CRM | Renewal within 90 days | Renewal + expand |
| Executive engagement | Meeting logs | New C-level contact engaged | Platform expansion |
| Organic growth | Product analytics | 20%+ usage increase QoQ | Usage-based expansion |

### Signal Strength Assessment

| Strength | Criteria | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Strong | 2+ signals from different categories; champion confirmed interested | Initiate expansion motion immediately |
| Moderate | 1 signal at threshold; champion not yet engaged on expansion | Engage champion to validate interest |
| Weak | Signal below threshold or single data point | Monitor; revisit in 30 days |

## Stakeholder Mapping for Expansion

### Role Identification

For expansion, the buying committee may differ from the original deal:

| Role | Original Deal | Expansion | How to Identify |
|------|--------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Economic Buyer | VP who signed | May be same or higher (CxO if cross-department) | Ask champion: "Who approves budget for the new team?" |
| Champion | Original user/manager | May be original champion or new department head | Ask: "Who's most excited about bringing this to [new team]?" |
| Technical Evaluator | Original IT contact | May be new team's tech lead | Ask: "Does [new team] have their own technical requirements?" |
| End User | Current users | New team's users | Ask: "Who will be the day-to-day users in [new team]?" |
| Blocker | N/A | Existing stakeholder who opposes expansion spend | Ask champion: "Is anyone hesitant about expanding?" |

## Value Case Construction Method

### Data Sources for Value Proof

| Data Type | Where to Find | How to Frame |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Usage metrics | Product analytics dashboard | "Your team processed X transactions last quarter, up Y% from launch" |
| Time savings | User surveys, workflow analysis | "Your team saves Z hours per week compared to the previous process" |
| Cost avoidance | Customer ROI model | "You avoided $X in [specific cost category] since deployment" |
| Revenue impact | Customer reporting, case study | "Teams using [product] grew [metric] by X% vs. non-users" |
| Satisfaction | NPS, CSAT, support tickets | "Your team's satisfaction score is X, with zero critical issues" |

### Value Case Structure

1. **Current state**: What the customer has achieved with existing deployment (specific metrics)
2. **Expansion opportunity**: What additional value is available (new teams, use cases, capabilities)
3. **Projected impact**: Expected ROI of expansion (modeled from current usage data)
4. **Risk of inaction**: What they miss by not expanding (competitor adoption, internal inconsistency)
5. **Investment**: Clear pricing for the expansion with comparison to value delivered

## Negotiation Guidelines for Expansion

### Expansion-Specific Principles

| Principle | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| Co-term to existing contract | Simplifies procurement; one renewal date |
| Volume discount for commitment | Reward expansion with better per-unit economics |
| Multi-year lock for significant expansion | Reduces churn risk; justifies discount |
| No discount without commitment | Expansion discounts require multi-year or volume commitment |

### Discount Authority for Expansion

| Expansion Type | Standard Discount | Approval |
|---------------|-------------------|----------|
| Seat add (same tier) | 0-5% | AE authority |
| Tier upgrade | 0-10% | AE authority (upgrade offset) |
| Cross-sell (new department) | 5-15% | Sales Manager (new budget holder) |
| Platform expansion (multi-department) | 10-20% | VP Sales (strategic account) |

## CS Handoff Requirements

### Expansion Handoff Document Contents

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Expansion scope | What was purchased (tier, seats, modules, use cases) |
| New stakeholders | Names, roles, contact info for new team |
| Success criteria | What the customer expects from the expansion (specific, measurable) |
| Onboarding requirements | Training needs, data migration, integration setup |
| Timeline | Expected go-live date, milestones |
| Risk flags | Known concerns, political dynamics, technical dependencies |
| Original champion status | Still engaged? Role in expansion? |
