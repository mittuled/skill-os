# Framework: Account Management Playbook

Defines the structural methodology for building a repeatable, scalable account management playbook covering QBRs, expansion conversations, renewal strategy, and escalation procedures.

## QBR Framework

### Required QBR Sections

| Section | Purpose | Time Allocation |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Account Health Review | Usage data, support tickets, NPS/CSAT | 10 min |
| Value Delivered | ROI realised since last QBR | 15 min |
| Progress vs. Goals | Customer's stated objectives vs. actual progress | 10 min |
| Product Roadmap Preview | Upcoming features relevant to customer | 10 min |
| Expansion Discussion | Opportunities identified by AM Lead | 15 min |
| Next-Quarter Planning | Mutual success plan with milestones | 10 min |

### QBR Health Scoring

| Signal | Green | Amber | Red |
|--------|-------|-------|-----|
| Product Usage | ≥ 70% of licensed capacity | 40–69% | < 40% |
| Support Tickets | < 3 P1/P2 per quarter | 3–5 P1/P2 | > 5 P1/P2 |
| NPS/CSAT | ≥ 8.0 / ≥ 85% | 6.0–7.9 / 70–84% | < 6.0 / < 70% |
| Exec Engagement | ≥ 1 exec touchpoint/quarter | < 1 exec touchpoint | No exec relationship |
| Renewal Date | ≥ 90 days away | 60–89 days | < 60 days |

## Expansion Conversation Framework

### EXPAND Model

1. **Explore**: Understand the customer's current and future business objectives
2. **X-ref**: Cross-reference objectives with unused or unavailable product capabilities
3. **Pain-surface**: Surface the cost of the gap (lost revenue, wasted time, risk exposure)
4. **Anchor value**: Tie the expansion to a specific business outcome with quantified impact
5. **Navigate objections**: Address hesitations using playbook response frameworks
6. **Drive to decision**: Agree on next steps with a specific follow-up date

### Expansion Scenario Playbooks

| Scenario | Trigger Signal | Primary Frame | Objection Response |
|----------|---------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Seat upsell | Usage > 80% of licensed seats | "Growing teams compound value" | "Show ROI per seat" |
| Tier upgrade | Using 3+ advanced features in lower tier | "Unlock what you're already relying on" | "Compare annualised cost vs. unlock value" |
| Cross-sell | New initiative signals in account | "One platform, not multiple tools" | "Integration ROI + reduced vendor overhead" |
| Multi-year renewal | 12 months before renewal | "Lock in today's price, fund tomorrow's goals" | "Price protection and budget predictability" |

## Renewal Strategy Framework

### Renewal Timeline

| Days Before Renewal | Action | Owner |
|--------------------|--------|-------|
| 180 | Flag renewal in CRM; assess health signals | AM |
| 120 | Executive Business Review (for strategic accounts) | AM + AML |
| 90 | Send renewal intent discussion | AM |
| 60 | Deliver formal renewal proposal | AM |
| 30 | Final negotiation and multi-year upsell attempt | AM + AML |
| 14 | Final push; legal review if needed | AM |

### Renewal Risk Tiers

| Risk Tier | Signals | Response |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Low | Health Green, exec engaged, usage growing | Standard renewal; attempt multi-year |
| Medium | Health Amber, limited exec access | Schedule EBR; resolve open issues before renewal |
| High | Health Red, open support escalations | Escalate to AM Lead; CS recovery plan before renewal |
| Critical | Customer has expressed intent to cancel | Executive escalation; emergency QBR; concession authority |

## Escalation Procedure

### Escalation Matrix

| Situation | Escalate To | Timeline |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Customer threatens to cancel | AM Lead + CBO | Same day |
| Competitor actively in the account | AM Lead | Within 48 hours |
| P1 support issue open > 5 days | AM Lead + Support Manager | Immediate |
| Stalled expansion deal > 30 days | AM Lead | Weekly |
| Executive relationship lost | AM Lead + CBO | Within 1 week |
