# Framework: Opportunity Framing for Account Management

Defines the methodology for identifying, qualifying, and framing expansion opportunities within existing accounts so account managers can pursue them through structured, value-led conversations.

## Opportunity Identification Triggers

| Trigger Type | Signal | Threshold | Action |
|-------------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Usage ceiling | Capacity utilisation ≥ 80% | Active | Frame tier upgrade or seat upsell |
| Feature underuse | < 30% adoption of purchased features | Active | Frame enablement + success path |
| Whitespace | Account lacks product capabilities now needed | On discovery | Frame cross-sell |
| Business event | Funding, acquisition, headcount growth | On detection | Frame strategic expansion |
| Contract anniversary | 3+ months before renewal with multi-year opportunity | Scheduled | Frame multi-year renewal + upgrade |

## Business Case Framework (VALUE Model)

Build every expansion business case using the VALUE model:

1. **V — Validate the pain**: Confirm the specific business problem the expansion solves, in the customer's language
2. **A — Anchor the status quo cost**: Quantify what the customer loses by NOT expanding (time, revenue, risk)
3. **L — Link to their strategic goal**: Connect the expansion to the customer's publicly stated or discovered business objective
4. **U — Unpack the ROI**: Calculate the expected return — net benefit ÷ expansion cost over 12 months
5. **E — Evidence**: Include 1–2 comparable customer case studies or metrics that validate the ROI claim

## Fit Assessment Matrix

Rate each expansion opportunity on two axes before building the full business case:

| Dimension | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Strategic fit | Minor convenience | Addresses a stated priority | Critical to a stated business goal |
| Implementation effort | Significant change required | Moderate configuration | Near-zero onboarding needed |
| Customer health | Red (at-risk) | Amber (some friction) | Green (satisfied and engaged) |
| Decision-maker access | No exec relationship | Director-level access | C-suite champion available |
| Budget signal | Budget contraction mentioned | Budget stable | Budget growth or new budget event |

**Scoring**: Sum of scores. Pursue at ≥ 12. Qualify further at 9–11. Hold at < 9.

## Prioritisation Framework

Rank framed opportunities by expected value-weighted probability:

**Priority Score** = (Revenue Potential × Likelihood of Close) ÷ Implementation Complexity

| Factor | Measure |
|--------|---------|
| Revenue Potential | Estimated ACV of expansion ($) |
| Likelihood of Close | Based on fit score + customer health (0–1) |
| Implementation Complexity | Low=1, Medium=2, High=3 |

Top priority opportunities: Priority Score ≥ $25K equivalent.

## Talking Points Architecture

Every opportunity brief handed to an AM must include:

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Opening frame | The business problem in 1 sentence (not the product) |
| Evidence of fit | 1–2 specific data points from their usage or business context |
| Business case headline | ROI claim in customer terms |
| Proof point | Comparable customer outcome |
| Objection map | Top 2–3 objections with response for each |
| Recommended timing | When to surface the conversation and why |
| Next step | Specific ask for AM to make in the next customer interaction |
