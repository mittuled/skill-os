# peer-review-platform-manager

## Agent: Analyst Relations Manager

L2 analyst relations manager responsible for analyst briefings, inquiry responses, Magic Quadrant strategy, and peer review platform management.

Department ethos: [ideal-marketing.md](../../../../departments/marketing/ideal-marketing.md)

## Skill Description

Manages the company's presence on G2, Capterra, and other peer review platforms, building the social proof that enterprise buyers consult during vendor evaluation.

## When to Use

- The company's review volume or ratings on key platforms are below competitive benchmarks.
- A new product or category listing needs establishing on peer review platforms.
- Quarterly review generation campaigns are due to maintain fresh, recent reviews.
- Negative reviews appear that require response and internal feedback routing.

## Workflow

1. Audit the company's current presence across peer review platforms using [`references/checklist.md`](references/checklist.md) Checklist 1: profile completeness, review volume, average rating, competitive ranking, and recency of reviews. Use the platform prioritisation framework in [`references/framework.md`](references/framework.md) to identify which platforms deserve the most investment.
2. Optimise platform profiles against the profile optimisation standards in [`references/framework.md`](references/framework.md): update product descriptions, feature lists, screenshots, integrations, and pricing information to reflect current offerings.
3. Design the review generation programme: identify target reviewers from high-satisfaction triggers (NPS Promoters, renewals, QBRs) using the solicitation trigger table in [`references/framework.md`](references/framework.md). Apply all solicitation rules — no quid pro quo incentives. Complete [`references/checklist.md`](references/checklist.md) Checklist 2 before launching.
4. Launch review solicitation campaigns timed to positive customer milestones. Set velocity targets per platform using the targets in [`references/framework.md`](references/framework.md). Time campaigns to align with seasonal badge cycles.
5. Monitor new reviews daily. Respond to all reviews within SLAs using the response protocol by sentiment type in [`references/framework.md`](references/framework.md). All 1–2 star reviews responded within 24 hours; all reviews responded within 72 hours.
6. Route feedback from negative reviews to the relevant internal team using the negative theme classification table in [`references/framework.md`](references/framework.md): product, support, CS, or security depending on theme.
7. Track competitive review trends: monitor competitor ratings, review volume, and competitive rating gap using the sentiment tracking metrics from [`references/framework.md`](references/framework.md). Alert if competitive rating gap exceeds 0.5 stars.
8. Run monthly platform health review using [`references/checklist.md`](references/checklist.md) Checklist 3. Report on review volume trend, average rating, competitive positioning, badge status, and sentiment themes to VP Marketing.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Soliciting reviews only from the happiest customers.** *Why*: Platforms detect and penalise review manipulation; a mix of genuine reviews including constructive criticism builds more credible social proof.
- **Ignoring negative reviews instead of responding.** *Why*: Unaddressed negative reviews signal to buyers that the company does not care about customer feedback; public responses demonstrate accountability.
- **Treating review platforms as a set-and-forget profile.** *Why*: Stale profiles with outdated screenshots and descriptions undermine credibility; platforms reward active, updated listings with better visibility.
- **Offering incentives that violate platform terms of service.** *Why*: Platforms penalise or delist companies caught incentivising reviews improperly, destroying the entire review investment.

## Output

**Success artifacts:**
- Optimised platform profiles with current product information
- Review generation campaign plan with target reviewers and outreach cadence
- Monthly platform health report with ratings, volume, and competitive benchmarks
- Review response log with internal feedback routing records

**Failure reporting:**
- Flag sudden rating drops or clusters of negative reviews within 24 hours
- Escalate platform policy violations or delisting risks to marketing leadership immediately
