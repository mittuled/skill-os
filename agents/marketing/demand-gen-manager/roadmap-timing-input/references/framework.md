# Framework: roadmap-timing-input

Defines marketing lead time requirements, launch conflict categories, blackout periods, and timing recommendation standards for providing input to the product roadmap.

## Marketing Lead Time Requirements by Launch Type

| Launch Type | Minimum Lead Time | Recommended Lead Time | Key Drivers |
|------------|------------------|----------------------|-------------|
| Major product launch (new product / tier) | 8 weeks | 12 weeks | Full asset suite, PR campaign, sales training, paid campaigns |
| Feature launch (significant capability) | 4 weeks | 6 weeks | Landing page, email, social, sales enablement update |
| Minor feature release | 2 weeks | 3 weeks | Release notes, in-app messaging, social post |
| Pricing change | 6 weeks | 8 weeks | FAQs, sales training, customer communications, legal review |
| Rebranding or positioning shift | 10 weeks | 16 weeks | Full asset rebuild, design system update, comms cascade |
| Market expansion (new geo / segment) | 8 weeks | 12 weeks | Localization, new ICP content, regional media outreach |

## Launch Conflict Categories

| Conflict Type | Severity | Recommended Action |
|--------------|----------|-------------------|
| Overlap with competitor's annual conference | High | Shift launch by 1–2 weeks before or 2 weeks after |
| Overlap with existing major campaign in same segment | High | Sequence: complete campaign, then launch; or consolidate |
| Holiday blackout period (see below) | Medium–High | Move launch out of blackout; allocate extra lead time |
| Launch clustering (3+ launches within 4 weeks) | Medium | Sequence by priority; defer lowest-impact launch |
| Overlap with industry trade show | Low–Medium | Align launch to trade show pre-buzz window (1 week before) |
| Q4 budget freeze (many enterprise buyers) | Medium | Shift to Q3 close-push or Q1 fresh-budget timing |

## Marketing Blackout Periods

Avoid launching during these windows unless strategically justified:

| Period | Dates (Approximate) | Reason |
|--------|---------------------|--------|
| US Thanksgiving week | Last week of November | Media blackout; low business attention |
| Christmas–New Year | Dec 20 – Jan 2 | Minimal B2B audience engagement |
| Major US holidays | Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day | Reduced open and click rates |
| Q-end close weeks | Last 2 weeks of March, June, September, December | Sales team distracted by quota; low buyer decision-making |
| Direct competitor's keynote | Check annually | Narrative competition; media unavailable |

## Timing Recommendation Memo Structure

A timing recommendation memo must include:

1. **Proposed launch date**: What product is requesting
2. **Conflict analysis**: Any conflicts from the categories above, with severity rating
3. **Lead time assessment**: Whether the proposed date gives marketing sufficient lead time (pass / at-risk / fail)
4. **Alternative date(s)**: One or two specific alternative dates with rationale
5. **Marketing commitment**: What marketing can deliver by the proposed or recommended date
6. **Escalation required**: Flag if the conflict requires CBO or product leadership decision

## Optimal Launch Timing Signals

When multiple dates are feasible, prefer dates with these positive signals:

| Signal | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| 2–3 weeks before major industry conference | Pre-conference buzz; journalists writing preview pieces |
| Q1 first month (January–February) | Fresh budgets; high buyer attention to new tools |
| 6–8 weeks after a competitor's major launch | Capture buyers disappointed by competitor; news cycle ready |
| Mid-month (10th–20th) | Higher email open rates; not compressed by month-end noise |
| Tuesday–Thursday launch day | Peak B2B media coverage; journalists not in weekend or Monday backlog |
