## Executive Summary

Landing page copy scores 4.8/10 overall — the headline and subheadline are functional but the body copy is riddled with passive voice, lacks persuasion mechanics, and the CTA is the weakest possible option. Replacing the generic "Learn More" with "Start your free trial — deploy in 5 minutes" and adding one named customer result would lift conversion potential significantly.

## Headline Analysis

### Headline Scorecard

| Headline | 4U Score | PAS Elements | AIDA Coverage | Recommendation |
|----------|----------|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| "Project Management That Actually Works" | 8/16 (U:3, Ur:1, Un:2, Us:2) | P: Yes (tools don't work), A: No, S: Implied | A: Moderate, I: Weak, D: No, A: No | Refine — add specificity and urgency |

**4U breakdown**: Useful (3) — the benefit "actually works" is clear but generic. Urgent (1) — zero time pressure. Unique (2) — "actually works" implies differentiation but could be said by any competitor. Ultra-specific (2) — no numbers, metrics, or concrete outcomes.

**PAS analysis**: The headline names the problem (tools that don't work) but skips Agitate entirely — there is no amplification of the cost of broken tools. The solution is implied but not stated.

**Recommended rewrite**: "Ship 37% faster: project management built for engineering teams" — adds specificity (37%), identifies the audience (engineering teams), and leads with the outcome (shipping faster).
