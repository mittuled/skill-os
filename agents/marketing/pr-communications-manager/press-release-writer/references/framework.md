# Framework: Press Release Writer

Defines the structural, stylistic, and distribution framework for writing and distributing press releases that achieve media pickup.

## Inverted Pyramid Structure

The inverted pyramid places the most critical information first, because journalists scan the first paragraph to determine if a release is worth reading.

```
[MOST NEWSWORTHY]
    Headline: What happened + why it matters (max 10 words)
    Subheadline: Context or differentiator (optional, max 15 words)
    Lead paragraph: Who, What, When, Where, Why — in 2-3 sentences
         |
    Supporting detail: Proof points, metrics, product details
         |
    Executive quote: One quote from a named executive
         |
    Customer / partner quote (if applicable)
         |
    Additional context: Market backdrop, company growth
         |
    Boilerplate: Standard company description
[LEAST CRITICAL]
    Contact information
```

### Lead Paragraph Rules

| Rule | Correct | Incorrect |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Lead with the news, not the company | "XYZ today announced a $50M Series B..." | "XYZ, a leader in enterprise software, today..." |
| State the news value in the first sentence | "...enabling 300 enterprise customers to automate..." | "...as it continues to execute on its vision..." |
| Avoid superlatives | "The platform supports 99.9% uptime" | "The industry's most powerful platform" |
| Use concrete numbers | "3x faster than the previous version" | "significantly faster" |

## AP Style Key Rules

AP Style is the standard for press releases distributed to U.S. and international media.

| Category | AP Rule |
|----------|---------|
| Numbers | Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above |
| Percentages | Use numerals + "percent" (not %) in text; use % in data tables |
| Dollar amounts | $1 million (not $1M); $500,000 (not $500K) in body text |
| Dates | Month DD, YYYY — no st/nd/rd/th suffixes; e.g., "January 15, 2025" |
| Time | 10 a.m., 2:30 p.m. (not 10AM or 14:30) |
| Company names | Use exact legal name on first reference; abbreviate after |
| Job titles | Lowercase unless immediately preceding a name: "Chief Executive Jane Smith" vs "Jane Smith, chief executive" |
| State abbreviations | Use AP abbreviations, not postal codes: Calif. not CA |
| Quotation marks | Double quotes for direct quotes; single quotes for quotes within quotes |
| Acronyms | Spell out on first use with acronym in parentheses |

## Wire Service Selection

| Service | Best For | Reach | Price Tier |
|---------|---------|-------|-----------|
| PR Newswire | U.S. enterprise, financial, and business press; SEC-compliant distribution | 4,000+ outlets, 170 countries | Premium |
| Business Wire | Public companies, investor relations, regulatory filings | 89,000+ media + financial terminals | Premium |
| Globe Newswire | Mid-market, investor relations, North America focus | 10,000+ outlets | Mid |
| PRWeb | SMB, SEO-focused distribution, lower pickup rate at Tier 1 | 30,000+ outlets (lower authority) | Budget |
| GlobeNewswire / Accesswire | Cost-effective alternative for non-public companies | Moderate reach | Mid-budget |

### Selection Criteria

| Announcement Type | Recommended Wire | Rationale |
|------------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Funding round ($50M+) | PR Newswire or Business Wire | Maximum financial press reach; Bloomberg/Reuters terminal pickup |
| Product launch | PR Newswire regional or Globe Newswire | Technology vertical distribution |
| Partnership announcement | Globe Newswire or PRWeb | Cost-effective; SEO value; industry distribution |
| Crisis / public statement | PR Newswire | Broadest distribution; credibility signal |
| Award / milestone | PRWeb or Accesswire | Lower cost appropriate for lower-news-value announcement |

## SEO Optimisation for Press Releases

Wire-distributed releases are indexed by search engines. Optimise without compromising readability.

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| Headline | Include primary keyword naturally; front-load key term |
| Subheadline | Include secondary keyword if natural |
| Lead paragraph | Include company name and primary keyword within first 50 words |
| Anchor text | Link to product page or landing page using keyword-rich anchor text (max 2 links) |
| Image alt text | Descriptive, keyword-aware alt text for embedded images |
| Boilerplate | Include company URL and category keywords |
| Avoid keyword stuffing | Natural language only; one target keyword phrase per release |

## Embargo and Distribution Timing

| Scenario | Best Practice |
|----------|--------------|
| Standard announcement | Distribute 9–11 a.m. Tuesday–Thursday for maximum journalist attention |
| Funding announcement | Distribute at market open (9:30 a.m. ET) for financial press |
| Crisis / reactive statement | Distribute immediately; timing is secondary to speed |
| Embargo timing | Set lift for 6 a.m. ET to allow overnight pickup |
| Friday releases | Avoid unless reactive; weekend releases get minimal pickup |
| Holiday proximity | Avoid 1 week before and after major holidays for proactive releases |

## Word Count and Format Standards

| Standard | Target |
|----------|--------|
| Total length | 400–600 words |
| Headline | Max 10 words |
| Lead paragraph | Max 3 sentences |
| Executive quote | 1 quote, 2–3 sentences, named with title |
| Customer / partner quote | Optional; max 2–3 sentences |
| Boilerplate | 3–5 sentences; same text used consistently across all releases |
| Contact block | PR contact name, email, phone number at the end |
| ### formatting | End with "###" to signal end of release to wire services |
