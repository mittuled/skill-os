# Checklist: Thought Leadership Programme Runner

Operational checklists for programme health audits, byline production, and speaker programme management.

## Checklist 1: Programme Health Audit

Use quarterly to assess the health of the executive thought leadership programme.

### Executive Engagement
- [ ] All participating executives have confirmed their themes for the current quarter (see framework.md Expertise Depth)
- [ ] Executive time commitments reviewed and confirmed: review sessions, interviews, speaking prep
- [ ] Any executives who have not published in 60+ days flagged for re-engagement conversation
- [ ] New executives identified who should be added to the programme (e.g., new CPO, CTO hires)
- [ ] Thought leadership themes reviewed for alignment with current positioning and market narrative

### Content Pipeline
- [ ] Owned content (company blog / LinkedIn articles) published at least 2x per month across all active executives
- [ ] Minimum 2 Tier B or C byline pitches active per executive per quarter
- [ ] At least 1 Tier A byline pitch in progress for at least 1 executive per half-year
- [ ] Byline topic pipeline is 8+ weeks ahead (pitches prepared before editorial deadlines)
- [ ] No byline submitted to multiple publications simultaneously (exclusivity tracked)

### Speaking Pipeline
- [ ] Open CFP (Call for Papers) list reviewed — at least 3 applications in progress per quarter
- [ ] Conference calendar covers all 4 quarters with at least 1 confirmed slot per executive per quarter
- [ ] Flagship conference applications submitted at least 6 months in advance
- [ ] Speaker bios and abstracts up to date for all active executives
- [ ] Post-event content repurposing actioned within 2 weeks of each event

### Distribution and Amplification
- [ ] All published placements shared via company LinkedIn and Twitter/X within 24 hours
- [ ] Executive LinkedIn posts scheduled for each placement
- [ ] Top placements promoted via paid LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads (per PESO model)
- [ ] Owned content cross-linked to relevant earned placements

### Metrics Review
- [ ] Placement rate calculated: pitches submitted vs. secured (target 30%+ for Tier B/C)
- [ ] Social engagement rate tracked for published placements
- [ ] Thought leadership coverage tracked via earned-media-monitor
- [ ] Any pipeline attribution data pulled from CRM (deals influenced by thought leadership content)

## Checklist 2: Byline Production

Use for each byline article from briefing to publication.

### Pre-Writing
- [ ] Topic approved by executive and aligned to confirmed theme brief
- [ ] Target publication identified and tier confirmed (A/B/C/D)
- [ ] Publication's editorial guidelines reviewed (word count, format, exclusivity policy)
- [ ] Concept pitch drafted (200 words: topic + argument + author credibility) for Tier A/B
- [ ] Editor's name confirmed and personalised pitch sent (not the generic submission form for Tier A/B)
- [ ] Pitch response received before writing begins (Tier A/B) OR full piece drafted (Tier C/D)
- [ ] Executive interview or briefing session scheduled to capture authentic voice and examples

### Writing and Review
- [ ] First draft written by PR Manager or ghostwriter
- [ ] Executive review completed — voice, accuracy, examples confirmed
- [ ] Draft does not read as a product pitch; genuine industry insight is the lead
- [ ] Concrete data, proprietary research, or original anecdotes included
- [ ] No simultaneous submission to competing publications in the same tier
- [ ] Draft reviewed for legal sensitivities (competitive claims, forward-looking statements)

### Submission and Publication
- [ ] Submission formatted per publication's requirements
- [ ] Author bio and headshot provided per publication specs
- [ ] Submission confirmed received by editor
- [ ] Publish date confirmed
- [ ] Amplification plan prepared before publish date (social posts, newsletter, paid boost budget)

### Post-Publication
- [ ] Placed article archived in placement tracker with publication, date, URL, and metrics
- [ ] Social posts published on day of publication
- [ ] Article linked from company website (news or press page)
- [ ] Reach and engagement metrics recorded 7 days post-publication
- [ ] Any follow-up from editors (requests for future contributions) logged in CRM

## Checklist 3: Speaker Opportunity Management

Use for each speaking opportunity from application to post-event.

### Application Stage
- [ ] Conference tier confirmed and placed in pipeline at correct category (see framework.md Speaker Programme)
- [ ] CFP deadline calendared with 2-week prep buffer
- [ ] Abstract drafted with topic aligned to executive's authority theme
- [ ] Speaker bio tailored to conference audience focus
- [ ] Previous year's speaker list reviewed for competitive context
- [ ] Application submitted on time

### Confirmation Stage
- [ ] Slot confirmed in writing from conference organiser
- [ ] Speaking terms reviewed: session length, format (keynote/panel/workshop), promotion rights
- [ ] Company promoted as speaker on conference website/social (request from organiser)
- [ ] Internal announcement made: conference, date, session topic
- [ ] Presentation development timeline set with executive review scheduled

### Preparation Stage
- [ ] Presentation deck drafted with narrative structure aligned to audience profile
- [ ] Key messages rehearsed with executive (at minimum 1 dry run for Tier A/B events)
- [ ] Q&A preparation: anticipated questions and approved responses
- [ ] Logistics confirmed: travel, AV requirements, slide submission to organisers

### Post-Event
- [ ] Post-event social content published (quotes, key takeaways, session summary)
- [ ] Video or audio recording obtained (if available) for repurposing
- [ ] Content repurposed: blog post, LinkedIn article, podcast clip within 2 weeks
- [ ] New contacts made at event recorded in CRM
- [ ] Event effectiveness rated: audience quality, leads generated, coverage secured
