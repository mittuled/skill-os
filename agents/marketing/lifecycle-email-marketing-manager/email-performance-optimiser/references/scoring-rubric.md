# Scoring Rubric: Email Performance Optimiser

Evaluates the rigour and effectiveness of email A/B testing programmes across hypothesis quality, statistical discipline, and business impact.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Hypothesis Quality | 20% | Clarity, specificity, and data-grounding of test hypotheses |
| 2 | Test Design Rigour | 25% | Isolation of variables, sample sizing, and randomisation methodology |
| 3 | Statistical Discipline | 25% | Proper significance thresholds, sample adequacy, and result interpretation |
| 4 | Business Impact Measurement | 20% | Connection of test results to downstream revenue and conversion metrics |
| 5 | Knowledge Compounding | 10% | Documentation, pattern recognition, and reuse of learnings across programmes |
| **Total** | | **100%** | |

## Scale

Each criterion is scored **0-10**:
- **0**: No evidence / completely absent
- **5**: Partially present with significant gaps
- **10**: Fully present, comprehensive, no gaps

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score × weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Exceptional | Hypothesis-driven testing with proven revenue lift and a compounding knowledge base | Continue cadence; share methodology as a best practice across marketing |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Strong | Consistent testing discipline with clear business impact; minor gaps in documentation | Approve programme; schedule quarterly knowledge base review |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Good | Solid test execution but business impact measurement or knowledge reuse needs strengthening | Approve with follow-up on metric attribution within 30 days |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Adequate | Tests are running but hypotheses are vague, significance standards inconsistent, or impact unclear | Pause new tests; retrain on statistical methodology and hypothesis framing |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | Weak | Ad-hoc testing with no hypothesis framework; results are anecdotal | Redesign programme from scratch with documented testing playbook |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Failing | No structured testing; performance changes are unexplained | Commission an email programme audit before any further send volume |

## Signal Tables

### Hypothesis Quality
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Every test has a written hypothesis linking a specific variable change to a predicted metric lift with a stated rationale grounded in prior test data or audience research |
| 7-8 | Hypotheses are documented for most tests with clear variable isolation; occasional tests lack quantified expected lift |
| 5-6 | Tests have informal hypotheses but predictions are vague ("this should improve engagement") without specific metrics or rationale |
| 3-4 | Tests are run on gut feeling; hypothesis is reconstructed after results are observed |
| 0-2 | No hypothesis documentation; tests are random changes with no stated expectation |

### Test Design Rigour
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Single-variable isolation in every test; sample sizes pre-calculated for statistical power; randomisation verified; holdout groups maintained |
| 7-8 | Variable isolation and randomisation are standard practice; sample sizes are reasonable but not always pre-calculated |
| 5-6 | Most tests isolate variables but occasional multi-variable tests occur; sample sizes are estimated informally |
| 3-4 | Multiple variables changed simultaneously in most tests; no sample size calculation; segments chosen by convenience |
| 0-2 | No structured test design; changes are deployed to the full list without control groups |

### Statistical Discipline
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Pre-defined significance threshold (p < 0.05); tests run to calculated sample size; confidence intervals reported; no early stopping without correction |
| 7-8 | Significance is checked before declaring winners; most tests reach adequate sample size; occasional early calls on clear winners |
| 5-6 | Winners declared based on percentage lifts without significance testing; sample sizes are sometimes adequate by coincidence |
| 3-4 | Results reported as raw percentages with no statistical validation; small samples treated as conclusive |
| 0-2 | No statistical analysis; winner selected by visual inspection of incomplete data |

### Business Impact Measurement
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Every test result is traced through the funnel to revenue or activation; cumulative programme impact reported monthly with attributed revenue |
| 7-8 | Most tests track downstream conversion; monthly reports show aggregate lift but attribution to individual tests is sometimes approximate |
| 5-6 | Open and click rates are tracked but downstream conversion or revenue impact is not consistently measured |
| 3-4 | Only email-level metrics (opens, clicks) are reported; no funnel connection |
| 0-2 | No performance measurement beyond send confirmation |

### Knowledge Compounding
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | All test results logged in a searchable knowledge base with tags; prior learnings referenced in new test hypotheses; patterns identified across programmes |
| 7-8 | Test results documented in shared reports; some cross-referencing of prior learnings when designing new tests |
| 5-6 | Results shared in meetings but not archived systematically; institutional knowledge depends on individual memory |
| 3-4 | Results recorded inconsistently; same tests are repeated without reference to prior outcomes |
| 0-2 | No documentation of test results; learnings are lost between cycles |
