## Target Segment

[Definition of the audience entering this nurture track.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Mid-market CFOs (100-500 employees) who downloaded the 2024 FinOps Benchmark Report. Estimated monthly volume: 200 leads. Current MQL-to-SQL rate for this segment: 4.2%. Target: 8%."
- Bad: "Marketing qualified leads"
- Format: Table with ICP attributes, entry trigger, estimated volume, and baseline metrics]

## Buyer Journey Map

[Stage-by-stage journey with questions, objections, and content needs.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table with stage name, lead's key question at this stage, common objection, content type that addresses it, and exit signal to the next stage
- Bad: "Awareness → Consideration → Decision"
- Format: Three-column table minimum with stage, question/objection, and content response]

## Email Sequence

[Detailed sequence with timing, subject lines, and content.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Numbered sequence with email name, send trigger (time or behaviour), subject line, preview text, body summary, CTA, and content asset linked
- Bad: "Email 1: Welcome. Email 2: Follow up."
- Format: Table with one row per email, columns for sequence position, trigger, timing, subject, CTA, linked asset]

## Branch Logic

[Conditional paths based on engagement signals.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Decision tree or table showing signal (e.g., "opens 3+ emails, zero clicks"), condition evaluation, and resulting action (e.g., "move to re-engagement track")
- Bad: "If they engage, send more emails"
- Format: Signal-action table with at least 4 branching conditions]

## Recommendations

[Prioritized actions for launching and optimising the sequence.

GUIDANCE: Each recommendation should be:
- Specific (not "improve nurture" but "add pricing-page visit trigger to skip leads to decision stage by March 15")
- Actionable (assignable to email ops, content, or sales)
- Prioritized (P1/P2/P3)]
