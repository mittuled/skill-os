# Scoring Rubric: Marketing Audit Orchestrator

Evaluates the overall health and maturity of a marketing function across five dimensions that collectively determine marketing's contribution to revenue.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Brand Health | 20% | Strength and consistency of brand perception, awareness, and positioning in the target market |
| 2 | Channel Performance | 25% | Efficiency and ROI of paid, owned, and earned channels measured by pipeline contribution |
| 3 | Content Quality | 20% | Relevance, engagement, and conversion impact of content assets across the buyer journey |
| 4 | Funnel Efficiency | 20% | Stage-by-stage conversion rates, velocity, and leak identification from top-of-funnel to closed-won |
| 5 | MarTech Utilisation | 15% | Adoption depth, integration health, and data hygiene across the marketing technology stack |
| **Total** | | **100%** | |

## Scale

Each criterion is scored **0-10**:
- **0**: No evidence / completely absent
- **5**: Partially present with significant gaps
- **10**: Fully present, comprehensive, no gaps

**Composite Score** = Sum (criterion score x weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 - 10.0 | Exceptional | Marketing function operates as a predictable revenue engine with full attribution, optimised channels, and consistent brand | Maintain cadence; shift focus to experimentation and market expansion |
| A | 8.0 - 8.9 | Strong | Most dimensions performing well with minor optimisation opportunities in one or two areas | Address flagged gaps in next quarterly planning cycle |
| B | 7.0 - 7.9 | Good | Solid foundation with clear improvement opportunities in channel mix or funnel conversion | Prioritise top two dimension gaps; allocate budget to remediation |
| C | 5.0 - 6.9 | Adequate | Marketing generates pipeline but with inefficiencies, attribution gaps, or inconsistent execution | Commission deep-dives on weakest dimensions; consider structural changes |
| D | 3.0 - 4.9 | Weak | Significant gaps in multiple dimensions; marketing contribution to revenue unclear or minimal | Restructure marketing priorities; invest in foundational capabilities before scaling spend |
| F | 0.0 - 2.9 | Failing | No systematic marketing function; activities are ad-hoc with no measurement or attribution | Build marketing foundations from scratch: positioning, measurement, basic funnel |

## Signal Tables

### Brand Health

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Unaided brand awareness above 40% in ICP; NPS above 50; consistent visual and verbal identity across all touchpoints; share of voice exceeds nearest competitor |
| 7-8 | Aided awareness above 60% in ICP; NPS above 30; brand guidelines exist and are followed in 80%+ of materials; share of voice within 10% of top competitor |
| 5-6 | Brand guidelines exist but inconsistently applied; awareness measured but below industry benchmarks; NPS between 10-30 |
| 3-4 | No formal brand tracking; guidelines exist on paper but not followed; visual identity inconsistent across channels |
| 0-2 | No brand guidelines, no awareness measurement, no positioning documentation; brand is whatever each team member says it is |

### Channel Performance

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Multi-touch attribution model validated against CRM data; CAC by channel tracked monthly; ROAS above 5x on primary channels; channel mix optimised quarterly based on pipeline data |
| 7-8 | Attribution model in place (first/last touch minimum); CAC tracked by channel; ROAS above 3x on most channels; quarterly channel reviews conducted |
| 5-6 | Some channel-level reporting exists but attribution is incomplete; CAC known at aggregate level only; ROAS tracked for paid channels but not organic |
| 3-4 | Channel spending tracked but not tied to pipeline; no attribution model; performance judged by vanity metrics (impressions, clicks) |
| 0-2 | No channel-level performance data; budget allocated by inertia or gut feel; no connection between marketing spend and pipeline |

### Content Quality

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Content mapped to every buyer journey stage; top-performing content drives measurable pipeline; content refresh cadence established; SEO-optimised with ranking data tracked |
| 7-8 | Content covers most journey stages; engagement metrics (time on page, scroll depth) tracked; some content directly attributed to pipeline; editorial calendar maintained |
| 5-6 | Content exists for awareness stage but thin for consideration/decision; engagement tracked but not tied to conversion; publishing is reactive not planned |
| 3-4 | Content is sporadic; no editorial calendar; topics chosen by internal preference not buyer research; no conversion tracking on content |
| 0-2 | No content programme; occasional blog posts with no strategy, measurement, or buyer alignment |

### Funnel Efficiency

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Stage-by-stage conversion rates tracked weekly; funnel velocity measured; leak points identified and under active remediation; MQL-to-SQL conversion above 30%; SQL-to-close above 20% |
| 7-8 | Conversion rates tracked monthly at major stages; some leak analysis conducted; MQL-to-SQL above 20%; funnel velocity measured but not optimised |
| 5-6 | Top-of-funnel and bottom-of-funnel metrics tracked but mid-funnel is a black box; conversion rates below industry benchmarks; no systematic leak analysis |
| 3-4 | Only lead volume and closed-won tracked; no stage definitions or conversion measurement; sales and marketing disagree on funnel stages |
| 0-2 | No funnel model; leads go directly from form fill to sales with no nurture, scoring, or stage tracking |

### MarTech Utilisation

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | All MarTech tools integrated bidirectionally with CRM; feature adoption above 70% across platforms; data hygiene audit conducted quarterly; automation covers lead scoring, nurture, and reporting |
| 7-8 | Core tools integrated with CRM; feature adoption above 50%; some automation in place; data quality issues flagged but not systematically resolved |
| 5-6 | Tools purchased but partially adopted; integrations exist but break frequently; manual workarounds common; no data hygiene process |
| 3-4 | Multiple tools with overlapping functionality; poor integration; less than 30% feature adoption; significant data silos between platforms |
| 0-2 | No MarTech stack or tools purchased but unused; email sent from personal accounts; no CRM integration; no automation |
