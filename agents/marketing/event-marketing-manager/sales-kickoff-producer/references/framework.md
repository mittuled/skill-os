# Framework: sales-kickoff-producer

This framework defines the design principles, agenda frameworks, content delivery standards, and motivation science for producing a Sales Kickoff (SKO) event.

## SKO Design Principles

| Principle | Description | Application |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Skills over information | SKO should change selling behaviour, not just inform | ≥ 40% of agenda time in interactive/practice formats |
| Field-informed content | Content must address actual objections and competitive scenarios reps face | Pre-SKO field survey required; PM/PMM content validated by top AEs |
| Energy management | Cognitive load peaks in the morning; use afternoons for practice and social | Schedule dense content (strategy, roadmap) AM; role-plays and workshops PM |
| Recognition as culture | Public recognition is among the highest-impact culture moments of the year | Dedicate minimum 1 formal recognition session; do not rush it |
| Enablement handoff | SKO training decays without reinforcement — build the handoff into the event | Every session owner delivers 30-day follow-up plan at content review |
| Theme as anchor | A unifying theme gives reps a shared mental model for the year | Theme announced at leadership kickoff; woven through all materials |

## Agenda Framework: 3-Day SKO

| Day | Morning (9–12) | Afternoon (1–5) | Evening |
|-----|---------------|----------------|---------|
| Day 1 — Inspire | Vision keynote (CEO) → Year in review → Go-to-market strategy (CRO) | Territory and quota briefings (by team) → Pipeline expectations | Welcome reception + recognition preview |
| Day 2 — Enable | Product roadmap (CPO/PMM) → Competitive positioning deep-dive | Role-play workshops (objection handling + competitive) → Breakout by segment (ENT/MM/SMB) | Awards ceremony + team dinner |
| Day 3 — Activate | Sales methodology refresher → New playbook walkthrough | Peer deal reviews → Manager enablement → Action planning | Optional celebration / departure |

**Agenda time allocation targets:**
- Strategy and vision: 15–20%
- Product and competitive: 20–25%
- Skills practice (role-plays, workshops): 25–35%
- Recognition and culture: 10–15%
- Manager and team sessions: 10–15%

## SKO Theme Development

A strong theme has three properties:
1. **Aspirational**: Connects the work to something larger than quota
2. **Memorable**: Short enough to repeat (3–5 words); evokes the year's challenge or opportunity
3. **Visual**: Can be represented as a visual motif on all materials

**Theme development process:**
1. CRO drafts 3 candidate themes reflecting the year's strategic narrative
2. SKO producer reviews for differentiation from prior years
3. Creative team designs visual treatment for top choice
4. CEO approves final theme ≥ 8 weeks before event

**Examples of effective theme structures:**
- Challenge framing: "Win the Competitive Market"
- Aspiration framing: "Build the Category"
- Unity framing: "One Team, One Number"

## Motivation Science: Recognition Design

Recognition at SKO leverages three motivation principles:

| Principle | Application at SKO |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Social recognition (public praise) | Award ceremony with named categories and criteria announced in advance; all hands present |
| Specific attribution | Awards cite specific deals, behaviours, or metrics — not generic "great job" |
| Peer nomination | Include peer-nominated categories (e.g., "Teammate of the Year") to reinforce culture values |
| Tiered recognition | Top performer tier (President's Club eligible), most improved, and rising star — avoid single winner domination |

**Recognition anti-pattern**: Rushed awards at the end of a long agenda signal that recognition is an afterthought. Allocate dedicated time on the primary evening when energy is still high.

## SKO Production Timeline

| Weeks Before SKO | Milestone |
|-----------------|-----------|
| 12–14 weeks | Dates, format, and venue locked; SKO brief approved |
| 10–12 weeks | Agenda framework approved by CRO |
| 8–10 weeks | Content owners assigned; pre-SKO field survey launched |
| 6–8 weeks | Content outlines reviewed; gaps identified |
| 4–5 weeks | First draft decks delivered by all content owners |
| 3–4 weeks | Rehearsals scheduled; playbook and materials in production |
| 2–3 weeks | Pre-reads distributed; role-play scenarios finalised |
| 1–2 weeks | All decks final; run-of-show published |
| 3–5 days | Speaker rehearsals complete; AV confirmed |
| Day before | Setup complete; AV test done; speaker check-in |
| Day of | Event execution; session recordings active |
| 24–48 hrs after | Feedback survey deployed; recordings available |
| 1–2 weeks after | Post-SKO package delivered to CRO (recordings, playbooks, action items) |
