# Framework: user-conference-producer

This framework defines the design methodology, session programming, sponsor management, NPS measurement, and ROI model for producing a flagship user conference or customer summit.

## Conference Design Model

| Design Dimension | Decision | Options |
|-----------------|----------|---------|
| Primary audience | Who is the conference for? | Customers only; customers + prospects; customers + partners + prospects |
| Business goal | What must the conference deliver? | Retention, expansion pipeline, new logo pipeline, brand authority, community building |
| Scale | How many attendees? | Intimate summit (50–200), mid-size conference (200–1,000), flagship (1,000+) |
| Format | In-person / virtual / hybrid | In-person preferred for relationship-building; hybrid for global reach |
| Programming style | Curated vs. open call | Curated (invitation-only speakers): quality control; Open call for papers: community ownership |

## Audience Mix Model

| Segment | Target Mix | Rationale | Revenue Treatment |
|---------|-----------|-----------|------------------|
| Existing customers | 50–60% | Core community; retention and expansion signals | Registration discounted or free for strategic accounts |
| Prospects (opportunity stage) | 15–20% | High-intent pipeline acceleration opportunity | Registration at standard rate; pipeline tagged in CRM |
| Partners and ecosystem | 10–15% | Amplification, co-selling opportunities | Sponsor or partner registration package |
| Press and analysts | 5% | Coverage and category positioning | Complimentary press pass |
| Internal (company staff) | 5–10% | Execution support + customer engagement | Internal registration |

## Session Programming Framework

### Keynote Design

| Keynote | Length | Owner | Purpose |
|---------|--------|-------|---------|
| Opening keynote | 45–60 min | CEO + Customer co-presenter | Vision + product roadmap reveal + customer validation |
| Day 2 keynote | 30–45 min | CPO or Head of Product | Deep product deep-dive + roadmap priorities |
| Closing keynote | 20–30 min | CEO or CRO | Call to action + preview of next year + close |

**Keynote principle**: Every keynote must include at least one live customer story or endorsement. Vendor-only keynotes signal a sales pitch, not a community event.

### Breakout Track Design

| Track Type | Programming Principle | Session Length | Ratio of Total Sessions |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------|------------------------|
| Customer success stories | 2–3 customer speakers per session; event team facilitates | 30–45 min | 30–40% |
| Product deep-dives | PM or SE presents + live demo | 30–45 min | 20–25% |
| Hands-on workshops | Max 30 attendees; pre-registration required | 60–90 min | 15–20% |
| Thought leadership (industry) | External analyst or practitioner; no product pitch | 30–45 min | 10–15% |
| Partner sessions (if applicable) | Partner-presented with editorial review | 30–45 min | 5–10% |

### Networking Programming

| Format | Best For | Capacity | Planning Lead Time |
|--------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| Welcome reception | First-evening icebreaker; all attendees | Any | 4 weeks |
| Hosted roundtables | Peer-to-peer by role or industry | 8–12 per table | 3 weeks |
| Lunch seating by persona | Structured networking at meals | Any | 2 weeks (seating chart) |
| Evening dinner (customer-only) | Deep relationship building with strategic accounts | 20–60 | 6 weeks |
| Expo networking hours | Sponsor and partner interactions | Any | Built into expo schedule |

## Sponsor Management

| Tier | Typical Benefits | Revenue Target | Onboarding Deadline |
|------|-----------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Diamond | Keynote stage time, premier booth, hosted dinner, attendee list | 25–35% of event cost | 5 months before |
| Platinum | Breakout session, standard booth, logo on main stage | 15–20% of event cost | 4 months before |
| Gold | Booth in expo hall, logo on materials, 1 workshop | 5–10% of event cost | 3 months before |
| Silver | Table-top booth, logo in programme | 2–5% of event cost | 2 months before |

**Sponsor management rules:**
- All sponsor session content must be reviewed and approved by the event team (no product pitches masquerading as thought leadership)
- Sponsors receive lead list only for attendees who opt-in to sponsor contact
- Sponsor commitments from prior year: outreach begins 6 months in advance with early-bird discount

## NPS Measurement Model

**Target NPS: ≥ 50 for established conference; ≥ 40 for first-time event**

| Stage | Survey Timing | Key Questions | Distribution |
|-------|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| Real-time session feedback | Immediately after each session | Session rating (1–5), one improvement suggestion | Mobile app or QR code in room |
| Overall event NPS | 24 hours after event close | "How likely are you to recommend [Conference] to a colleague?" (0–10) + open comment | Email survey |
| Customer health check | 30 days after event | Expansion intention, product adoption change, overall relationship health | CS-led; not an event survey |

**NPS calculation:**
- Promoters: score 9–10
- Passives: score 7–8
- Detractors: score 0–6
- NPS = % Promoters − % Detractors

## ROI Measurement Model

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Attendance rate | Attendees / Registrants | ≥ 75% |
| Sponsor revenue | Total sponsor packages sold | ≥ 30% of event budget offset |
| Pipeline sourced (prospects) | Opportunity value from prospect attendees (90-day) | ≥ 3× sponsorship investment in pipeline |
| Expansion pipeline (customers) | Upsell/cross-sell opportunities opened with conference-attending customers (90-day) | Tracked separately from new logo pipeline |
| Retention correlation | Conference attendee retention rate vs. non-attendee retention rate (12-month) | Conference attendees retain at ≥ 10% higher rate |
| Event NPS | Composite NPS score | ≥ 50 |
| Session average rating | Average session rating across all sessions | ≥ 4.0 / 5.0 |

## Production Timeline

| Months Before | Milestone |
|--------------|-----------|
| 9–12 months | Conference brief approved; theme selected; venue contracted |
| 7–8 months | Call for papers opened; sponsor outreach started |
| 5–6 months | Speaker roster confirmed (60%+); sponsor Diamond/Platinum committed |
| 4–5 months | Registration opened; promotional campaign launched |
| 3–4 months | Full agenda published; session materials in production |
| 2–3 months | All speakers confirmed; all Gold/Silver sponsors committed |
| 1–2 months | All materials finalised; attendee communications at full cadence |
| 2 weeks | Run-of-show finalised; on-site staff briefed |
| Day before | Setup complete; AV tested; registration desk staffed |
| During event | Real-time NPS and session feedback active |
| 48 hours after | Post-event survey deployed; follow-up campaign launched |
| 2 weeks after | Post-conference report delivered |
