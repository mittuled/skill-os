# Framework: influencer-coordination-manager

This framework defines the end-to-end methodology for identifying, vetting, briefing, and measuring influencer partnerships in a B2B/B2C technology context.

## Influencer Tier Model

| Tier | Follower Count | Engagement Rate Benchmark | Best Use Case | Typical CPE Range |
|------|---------------|--------------------------|---------------|------------------|
| Nano | < 10K | 5–10% | Niche communities, hyper-targeted ICP reach | $0.05–$0.20 |
| Micro | 10K–100K | 3–7% | Segment-specific authority, high trust | $0.10–$0.50 |
| Macro | 100K–1M | 1–4% | Brand awareness at scale, mid-funnel support | $0.20–$2.00 |
| Mega | > 1M | 0.5–2% | Broad awareness, PR amplification | $0.50–$5.00+ |

**B2B Default:** Prioritise micro and nano influencers who are practitioners (analysts, operators, founders) over celebrities or generalist creators.

## Candidate Vetting Criteria

Score each candidate 1–5 on the following dimensions. Require minimum composite score of 3.5/5 to advance.

| Criterion | Weight | 1 (Fail) | 3 (Pass) | 5 (Strong) |
|-----------|--------|----------|----------|------------|
| ICP audience overlap | 30% | < 20% audience match | 40–60% audience match | > 75% audience match |
| Engagement authenticity | 25% | Follower spikes, generic comments | Some automation signals | Organic growth, substantive comments |
| Brand safety | 20% | Controversial content history | Minor concerns | Clean record, aligned values |
| Content quality | 15% | Low production, unclear messaging | Consistent but generic | High-quality, distinctive POV |
| Category authority | 10% | No category expertise | Peripheral knowledge | Recognised practitioner or analyst |

## Creative Brief Structure

Every influencer receives a brief containing:

1. **Campaign context**: What the brand is announcing and why it matters to their audience
2. **Key message (1 sentence)**: The single takeaway the audience should retain
3. **Proof points (3 max)**: Data, customer outcomes, or product capabilities that support the key message
4. **Format guidance**: Suggested content type (e.g., post, carousel, short video) and platform
5. **Mandatory disclosures**: FTC-required language (see FTC Compliance section below)
6. **Guardrails**: Topics, claims, or competitor names to avoid
7. **Deliverable schedule**: Draft review deadline, revision window, publish date
8. **Performance tracking**: UTM link or promo code assigned to this influencer

**Principle**: Briefs set the guardrails, not the script. Influencers who receive scripts produce content that reads like paid posts and destroys audience trust.

## Performance Tracking Model

Track per influencer per campaign:

| Metric | Formula | Target Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Engagement Rate | (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Impressions | ≥ tier benchmark |
| Click-Through Rate | Clicks / Impressions | ≥ 1.5% |
| UTM Conversions | Attributed conversions from UTM-tagged link | Defined per campaign |
| Pipeline Contribution | Opportunities sourced from influencer UTMs | ≥ 10% of campaign budget |
| Earned Media Value (EMV) | Engagements × average CPE for channel | > spend on influencer |
| Cost Per Engagement (CPE) | Total influencer cost / total engagements | Within tier benchmark |

## Influencer Renewal Decision Matrix

Evaluate after each campaign:

| Pipeline Contribution | Engagement Quality | Decision |
|----------------------|--------------------|----------|
| Above target | High | Renew, expand deliverables |
| Above target | Low | Renew, require content format change |
| On target | High | Renew at same terms |
| On target | Low | Renew with content coaching |
| Below target | High | Pause, reassess ICP alignment |
| Below target | Low | Terminate, replace |

## FTC Compliance Requirements

All influencer content promoting the brand must comply with FTC guidelines:

- **Material connection disclosure**: Any paid or gifted relationship must be disclosed clearly (e.g., "#ad", "#sponsored", "Paid partnership with [Brand]")
- **Placement**: Disclosure must appear before the fold — not buried in hashtag lists or caption tails
- **Platform tools**: Use platform-native disclosure tools (Instagram "Paid Partnership" label, LinkedIn "Paid" tag) in addition to in-caption disclosure
- **Gifted products**: Free product constitutes material connection and requires disclosure even without a fee
- **Contract clause**: Include a disclosure compliance clause in all influencer agreements with takedown rights for non-compliant posts

## Programme Review Cadence

| Review | Frequency | Participants | Output |
|--------|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Performance check | Monthly | Social Manager, Demand Gen | Per-influencer performance summary |
| Programme health | Quarterly | Marketing Lead, Social Manager | Renewal decisions, ICP alignment audit |
| Annual programme review | Annual | Marketing Lead, CMO | Budget allocation, tier mix, ROI vs. channel alternatives |
