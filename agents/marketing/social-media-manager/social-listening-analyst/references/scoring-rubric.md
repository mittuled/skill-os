# Scoring Rubric: social-listening-analyst

This rubric evaluates the brand health signal quality surfaced by a social listening programme across five dimensions.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Share of Voice (SOV) | 25% | Brand's portion of total category conversation volume vs. key competitors |
| 2 | Sentiment Score | 25% | Ratio of positive and neutral mentions to negative mentions across monitored channels |
| 3 | Mention Velocity | 20% | Rate of brand mention growth or decline over the measurement period |
| 4 | Topic Coverage | 20% | Breadth of category conversations monitored; ability to surface emerging themes before they peak |
| 5 | Influencer Amplification | 10% | Share of brand mentions originating from accounts with > 10K followers or domain authority ≥ 40 |
| **Total** | | **100%** | |

## Scale
Each criterion is scored **0–10**: 0 = no evidence or data, 5 = partially present with gaps, 10 = fully present and exceeding benchmark

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score × weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Exceptional | Category-leading brand health with strong amplification | Maintain programme; study and replicate what's working |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Strong | Above-benchmark SOV, positive sentiment majority, fast-growing mentions | Optimise topic coverage and influencer identification |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Good | Competitive SOV, mostly positive sentiment with isolated negatives | Investigate negative sentiment clusters; expand monitoring queries |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Adequate | SOV near parity with competitors, mixed sentiment | Prioritise sentiment improvement; activate response workflows |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | Weak | Below-benchmark SOV, trending negative, slow detection | Audit monitoring query gaps; increase response resourcing |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Failing | Brand health crisis signals; unable to detect or respond at scale | Escalate to marketing leadership; implement crisis protocol |

## Signal Tables

### Share of Voice (SOV)

| Score Range | Observable Evidence |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 9–10 | Brand holds ≥ 40% category SOV; consistently exceeds nearest competitor by ≥ 10 percentage points |
| 7–8 | Brand holds 25–39% SOV; leads or ties nearest competitor |
| 5–6 | Brand holds 15–24% SOV; competitive but not leading |
| 3–4 | Brand holds 8–14% SOV; clearly outpaced by 1–2 competitors |
| 0–2 | Brand holds < 8% SOV; effectively invisible in category conversation |

### Sentiment Score

| Score Range | Observable Evidence |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 9–10 | > 70% positive mentions; < 5% negative; no sustained negative thread in past 30 days |
| 7–8 | 50–70% positive; < 15% negative; negative mentions isolated and resolved |
| 5–6 | 35–49% positive; 15–25% negative; mixed narrative without clear crisis |
| 3–4 | < 35% positive; 25–40% negative; recurring themes in negative mentions indicating systemic issue |
| 0–2 | < 20% positive; > 40% negative; active brand crisis or sustained negative campaign |

### Mention Velocity

| Score Range | Observable Evidence |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 9–10 | Month-over-month mention growth ≥ 20%; growth sustained over 3+ months |
| 7–8 | Month-over-month mention growth 10–19%; no sustained decline in prior 3 months |
| 5–6 | Mention volume flat (+/- 5%); no growth but no decline |
| 3–4 | Month-over-month decline of 5–15%; declining trend over 2+ months |
| 0–2 | Month-over-month decline > 15%; brand losing relevance in category conversation |

### Topic Coverage

| Score Range | Observable Evidence |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 9–10 | Monitoring queries cover brand, all products, key competitors, 10+ category keywords; emerging topics detected ≥ 72 hours before peak |
| 7–8 | Queries cover brand and products, 2–3 competitors, 6–9 category keywords; most emerging topics detected before peak |
| 5–6 | Queries cover brand only or brand + 1 competitor; limited category keyword coverage |
| 3–4 | Basic brand name monitoring only; blind to competitor and category conversations |
| 0–2 | No structured monitoring; mentions discovered reactively |

### Influencer Amplification

| Score Range | Observable Evidence |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 9–10 | ≥ 25% of brand mentions originate from accounts with > 10K followers or DA ≥ 40; consistent tier-1 amplification |
| 7–8 | 15–24% of mentions from high-authority accounts; notable influencers actively engaging the brand |
| 5–6 | 8–14% of mentions from high-authority accounts; occasional amplification without systematic programme |
| 3–4 | 3–7% of mentions from high-authority accounts; brand largely invisible to influential voices |
| 0–2 | < 3% of mentions from high-authority accounts; brand conversation confined to low-reach users |
