# ugc-programme-designer

## Agent

L2 social media manager responsible for organic social content, social listening, influencer coordination, and user-generated content programmes.

Department ethos: [ideal-marketing.md](../../../../departments/marketing/ideal-marketing.md)

## Skill Description

Designs programmes that encourage and amplify user-generated content, turning customer enthusiasm into authentic social proof that supports brand credibility and pipeline.

## When to Use

- The brand needs authentic social proof but lacks budget for large-scale influencer campaigns.
- Customer success reports high satisfaction scores and the marketing team wants to convert that sentiment into visible advocacy.
- A product launch or feature release benefits from real user stories to build credibility with prospects.
- Competitors are winning share of voice through community-driven content and the company has no structured UGC programme.

## Workflow

1. Define the UGC programme objectives: target content volume, content types, platforms, and how UGC will be used across marketing and sales channels.
2. Identify the target contributor segments: power users, community members, recent promoters from NPS surveys, and customers with public-facing success stories.
3. Design the participation mechanic using the programme mechanics guide in `references/framework.md`: hashtag campaigns, contests, template prompts, or ambassador tiers with clear incentives and recognition structures.
4. Draft programme guidelines covering brand usage rules, content standards, legal permissions, and the rights framework for repurposing UGC. Apply the rights acquisition process in `references/framework.md` to define approval per use case.
5. Verify launch readiness using `references/checklist.md` before going live. Launch the programme with seed content from internal advocates or early participants to demonstrate the expected format and quality.
6. Monitor submissions for quality, brand safety, and adherence to guidelines. Curate top content for amplification on owned channels.
7. Repurpose high-performing UGC into sales enablement assets, website testimonials, ad creative, and case study leads.
8. Report monthly on programme health: submission volume, engagement on amplified UGC, conversion lift from UGC-featuring pages, and contributor retention.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Launching without clear usage rights agreements.** *Why*: Repurposing user content in ads or on the website without explicit permission creates legal liability and damages contributor trust.
- **Over-editing UGC to match brand voice.** *Why*: The value of user-generated content is its authenticity; polishing it into corporate copy destroys the credibility that makes it effective.
- **Relying solely on intrinsic motivation.** *Why*: Even enthusiastic customers need a reason to create content consistently; programmes without recognition or incentives see submission rates collapse after launch.
- **Treating UGC as a one-time campaign instead of an ongoing programme.** *Why*: Sporadic UGC efforts produce a brief burst of content that goes stale; sustained programmes build a compounding library of social proof.

## Output

**Success artifacts:**
- UGC programme brief with mechanics, guidelines, and incentive structure
- Legal permissions template for content repurposing rights
- Curated UGC library organized by theme, platform, and use case
- Monthly programme performance report with submission and engagement metrics

**Failure reporting:**
- Flag brand-unsafe or off-guidelines submissions for removal within 12 hours
- Escalate programme participation drops below target thresholds to the marketing lead
