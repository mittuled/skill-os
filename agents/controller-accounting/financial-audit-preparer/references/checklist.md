# Checklist: financial-audit-preparer

Step-by-step checklist for preparing the company for a financial audit, from initial planning through finding remediation.

## Audit Timeline Planning (T−90 days before fieldwork)

- [ ] Confirm the audit firm engagement letter is signed
- [ ] Schedule the planning meeting with the audit team (partner, manager, staff)
- [ ] Agree on the audit timeline: planning meeting, interim procedures, fieldwork start/end, draft report, final report
- [ ] Set internal close deadlines for each month of the audit period to ensure clean books
- [ ] Build the internal prep calendar: assign PBC items to owners with deadlines
- [ ] Brief the CFO and department heads on the audit timeline and their responsibilities
- [ ] Review prior-year management letter comments; confirm remediation status of each

## PBC List Management (T−60 days)

- [ ] Receive the initial PBC (Prepared by Client) list from the audit firm
- [ ] Assign each PBC item to a specific internal owner with a delivery deadline
- [ ] Set up a shared PBC tracker (status: not started / in progress / ready for review / submitted)
- [ ] Quality-review each deliverable before submitting to auditors (no placeholders, no formulas missing)
- [ ] Respond to all auditor requests within the agreed SLA (typically 24–48 hours during fieldwork)
- [ ] Track auditor queries in a request log; never let a query go unanswered for more than 24 hours

## Audit Schedule Preparation (T−45 days)

### Revenue and Deferred Revenue
- [ ] Prepare revenue recognition detail by contract (ASC 606 five-step analysis for each material contract)
- [ ] Prepare deferred revenue rollforward: beginning balance + billings − recognized = ending balance
- [ ] Reconcile deferred revenue per GL to deferred revenue per billing system
- [ ] Document revenue recognition policy memo with accounting policy elections

### Equity and Capital
- [ ] Prepare equity rollforward (common, preferred, APIC, retained earnings/accumulated deficit)
- [ ] Confirm cap table agrees to equity per the GL and legal records
- [ ] Document all SAFE/convertible note conversions that occurred in the period
- [ ] Prepare 409A valuation timeline; confirm all option grants used a current 409A price

### Balance Sheet Schedules
- [ ] Prepare fixed asset rollforward (cost and accumulated depreciation by asset class)
- [ ] Prepare intangible asset rollforward including capitalized software development costs
- [ ] Prepare debt schedule: beginning balance + draws − payments + accrued interest = ending
- [ ] Prepare lease schedule under ASC 842: right-of-use asset and lease liability rollforward
- [ ] Prepare prepaid expense rollforward and confirm all prepaid items will benefit future periods

### Compliance and Governance
- [ ] Prepare related-party transaction schedule: identify all transactions with founders, board members, affiliates
- [ ] Identify and document subsequent events (material events after year-end through report date)
- [ ] Prepare management representation letter (draft for CFO/CEO signature at report issuance)

## ASC 606 Documentation (T−30 days)

- [ ] For each material revenue contract, document:
  - [ ] Step 1: Identify the contract (executed, commercial substance, collectible)
  - [ ] Step 2: Identify performance obligations (distinct goods/services, series provisions)
  - [ ] Step 3: Determine the transaction price (variable consideration, constraint applied)
  - [ ] Step 4: Allocate transaction price to performance obligations (SSP analysis)
  - [ ] Step 5: Recognition pattern (over time or point in time; method used)
- [ ] Confirm revenue recognition workpapers are prepared contemporaneously with transactions

## Internal Control Documentation (T−21 days)

- [ ] Document key financial controls by process area:
  - [ ] Financial close (reconciliation procedures, GL lock dates, review sign-offs)
  - [ ] Revenue recognition (contract review, deferred revenue reconciliation)
  - [ ] Accounts payable (three-way match, DOA limits, payment approval)
  - [ ] Payroll (input review, register approval, GL reconciliation)
  - [ ] Financial reporting (variance analysis, executive review)
- [ ] Review segregation of duties: confirm no single individual can initiate, approve, AND record transactions
- [ ] Review system access controls: confirm terminated employees removed, rights match roles
- [ ] Identify any control gaps; remediate before fieldwork begins

## Auditor Coordination During Fieldwork

- [ ] Designate a single internal point of contact for day-to-day auditor interaction
- [ ] Respond to all requests within agreed SLA; escalate blockers immediately
- [ ] Track open items in a shared log; review daily during fieldwork
- [ ] Host weekly status meetings with audit manager; no surprises
- [ ] Confirm that no auditor question goes unanswered for more than 48 hours

## Finding Remediation (Post-Audit)

- [ ] Receive the draft management letter comments and audit findings
- [ ] Assign each finding to an internal owner with a remediation deadline
- [ ] Develop written remediation plans with specific actions and testing criteria
- [ ] Confirm remediation is complete before the next audit cycle
- [ ] Document control improvements in the internal control matrix for future reference
