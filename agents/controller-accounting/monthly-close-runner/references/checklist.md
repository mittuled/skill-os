# Checklist: monthly-close-runner

Step-by-step checklist for executing the GAAP month-end close process within a 10-day close cycle.

## Pre-Close Readiness (Day 0–1)

- [ ] Confirm all bank feeds are current and reconciled through month-end
- [ ] Verify AP sub-ledger is complete: all invoices received through month-end are coded and posted
- [ ] Verify AR sub-ledger is current: all invoices issued through month-end are recorded
- [ ] Send close kickoff email to department leads requesting accrual inputs by Day 2
- [ ] Review close checklist from prior month for any carry-over items or new items
- [ ] Confirm payroll has been processed and GL entries posted for the final pay period

## Revenue Recognition (Day 1–3)

- [ ] Pull subscription data from billing system; reconcile new bookings, expansions, and churns against CRM
- [ ] Apply ASC 606 five-step model to all revenue transactions:
  - [ ] Identify contracts with customers
  - [ ] Identify performance obligations
  - [ ] Determine transaction price (variable consideration, financing component)
  - [ ] Allocate transaction price to performance obligations
  - [ ] Recognize revenue when/as performance obligations satisfied
- [ ] Recognize subscription revenue ratably over the service period
- [ ] Recognize professional services revenue upon delivery milestones
- [ ] Recognize usage-based revenue based on consumption data
- [ ] Reconcile deferred revenue schedule (beginning balance + new bookings − recognized = ending balance)
- [ ] Prepare ASC 606 revenue recognition workpaper with supporting documentation

## Expense Accruals (Day 2–3)

- [ ] Identify expenses incurred but not yet invoiced (vendor accruals, SaaS tools billed in arrears)
- [ ] Confirm payroll accrual for any days in the month not covered by submitted payroll
- [ ] Record benefits and employer payroll tax accruals
- [ ] Reverse prior-month accruals that are no longer applicable
- [ ] Confirm all accrual journal entries have supporting documentation
- [ ] Obtain approval for accruals above materiality threshold

## Bank and Account Reconciliations (Day 3–5)

- [ ] Reconcile all operating bank accounts to bank statements
- [ ] Reconcile all corporate credit cards to statements
- [ ] Reconcile intercompany accounts to zero or agreed balances
- [ ] Investigate all reconciling items; resolve any item older than 30 days
- [ ] Reconcile prepaid expense schedule (beginning + additions − amortization = ending)
- [ ] Reconcile fixed asset schedule (beginning + additions − disposals − depreciation = ending)
- [ ] Reconcile security deposits and other balance sheet accounts

## Financial Statement Preparation (Day 5–7)

- [ ] Run trial balance; verify debits equal credits
- [ ] Prepare income statement (revenue, COGS, gross profit, OpEx by category, EBITDA, net income)
- [ ] Prepare balance sheet (assets = liabilities + equity); confirm balance
- [ ] Prepare cash flow statement (operating, investing, financing); reconcile ending cash to bank
- [ ] Confirm balance sheet reconciliation: every line ties to a supporting workpaper
- [ ] Confirm intercompany eliminations are complete for multi-entity consolidations

## Analytical Review (Day 7–8)

- [ ] Run flux analysis: current month vs. prior month and vs. budget for all P&L lines
- [ ] Investigate all variances greater than 10% AND greater than $25K
- [ ] Prepare written explanations for all material variances
- [ ] Confirm explanations are grounded in operational data (not pure accounting)
- [ ] Flag any variance that may affect the rolling forecast

## Close Certification (Day 8–10)

- [ ] Confirm all checklist items above are complete
- [ ] Confirm all reconciling items are resolved or have approved carry-forward explanations
- [ ] Obtain CFO or designated approver sign-off on financial statements
- [ ] Lock the accounting period in the GL to prevent further posting
- [ ] Deliver financial package to FP&A for variance analysis and rolling forecast update
- [ ] File close certification with locked period confirmation in the workpaper archive
