# Copywriting Analysis Framework

Reference framework for evaluating marketing copy quality across headline effectiveness, voice consistency, and persuasion mechanics.

## Headline Formulas

### 4U Framework

Evaluate headlines on four dimensions, each scored 1-4:

| Dimension | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 | Score 4 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Useful** | No clear benefit to reader | Implied benefit but vague | Clear benefit stated | Specific, quantified benefit |
| **Urgent** | No time pressure | Mild implication of timeliness | Clear reason to act now | Compelling deadline or scarcity |
| **Unique** | Generic, could be any company | Some differentiation | Clear differentiator | Only this company could say this |
| **Ultra-specific** | Completely vague | Some specificity | Concrete details included | Exact numbers, names, or outcomes |

**Score interpretation**: 12-16 = strong headline; 8-11 = needs refinement; 4-7 = rewrite required.

### PAS Framework (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

Evaluate whether copy follows the PAS sequence:

| Element | Present | Evidence Required |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **Problem** | Yes/No | Copy names a specific pain the reader recognises |
| **Agitate** | Yes/No | Copy amplifies the emotional or financial cost of the problem |
| **Solve** | Yes/No | Copy presents a clear solution tied to the product |

**Scoring**: All three present = strong PAS execution. Missing Agitate = most common failure (jumps from problem to solution without building tension). Missing Problem = copy starts with solution (assumes reader already knows they have a problem).

### AIDA Framework (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

| Element | Diagnostic Question | Common Failure |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Attention** | Does the first line stop the scroll? | Headline is informational, not attention-grabbing |
| **Interest** | Does the next section build curiosity? | Jumps to features before establishing relevance |
| **Desire** | Does the copy create want (not just understanding)? | Lists features without connecting to outcomes |
| **Action** | Is the CTA clear, specific, and low-friction? | Vague CTA ("Learn more") or missing entirely |

## Voice Dimensions

Map copy on four spectrums to define and measure voice consistency:

| Dimension | Left Pole | Right Pole | Measurement |
|-----------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Formality | Formal | Casual | Sentence structure, contractions, jargon usage |
| Tone | Serious | Playful | Metaphor usage, humour, emotional register |
| Authority | Respectful | Irreverent | Treatment of industry norms, competitive references |
| Energy | Enthusiastic | Matter-of-fact | Exclamation usage, superlatives, emotional language |

**How to use**: Plot the brand's target position on each spectrum (e.g., "70% formal, 30% casual"). Then plot each copy sample. Drift is the distance between target and actual on any dimension.

### Voice Drift Detection

| Drift Level | Distance from Target | Action |
|-------------|---------------------|--------|
| None | Within 10% | Copy is on-brand |
| Minor | 10-25% | Flag for awareness; may be intentional audience adaptation |
| Significant | 25-50% | Requires revision; likely unintentional drift |
| Critical | 50%+ | Copy reads as a different brand; full rewrite needed |

## Readability Benchmarks

| Metric | B2B SaaS Target | B2C Target | Technical Audience Target |
|--------|-----------------|------------|--------------------------|
| Flesch-Kincaid Grade | 8-10 | 6-8 | 10-12 |
| Average Sentence Length | 15-20 words | 12-16 words | 18-22 words |
| Passive Voice | Below 10% | Below 5% | Below 15% |
| Paragraph Length | 3-4 sentences | 2-3 sentences | 4-5 sentences |

## Persuasion Markers

Score the presence and quality of these conversion elements:

| Marker | What to Look For | Impact |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| **Social Proof** | Customer names, logos, testimonials, case study references, user counts | Reduces anxiety, builds credibility |
| **Specificity** | Exact numbers ("37% faster"), named outcomes, concrete timelines | Increases believability |
| **Power Words** | Proven, guaranteed, instant, exclusive, free, new, results | Triggers emotional response |
| **Objection Handling** | Addresses "what if it doesn't work", "is it worth the price", "why now" | Removes purchase barriers |
| **Risk Reversal** | Money-back guarantee, free trial, no credit card required | Lowers commitment threshold |
