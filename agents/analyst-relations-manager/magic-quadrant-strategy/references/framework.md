# Framework: Magic Quadrant Strategy

Defines the Gartner Magic Quadrant and Forrester Wave evaluation frameworks, scoring methodology, positioning strategy, and year-round AR investment model for achieving and improving evaluation placement.

## Gartner Magic Quadrant Framework

### The Two Axes

| Axis | Definition | Key Sub-Criteria |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Completeness of Vision** (horizontal) | How well the vendor understands market direction and has a strategy to capitalise on it | Market understanding, sales strategy, offering strategy, innovation, go-to-market, geographic strategy, vertical/industry strategy |
| **Ability to Execute** (vertical) | How well the vendor delivers on its strategy through products, operations, and customer success | Product/service quality, overall viability (financial health), sales execution, market responsiveness, customer experience, operations, marketing execution |

### Quadrant Positions

| Quadrant | Vision | Execution | Buyer Implication |
|----------|--------|-----------|------------------|
| **Leaders** | High | High | Safe shortlist choice; analyst recommends for most buyers |
| **Challengers** | Low | High | Strong execution but limited vision; safe for conservative buyers |
| **Visionaries** | High | Low | Innovative approach; risk of execution concerns for enterprise |
| **Niche Players** | Low | Low | Focused market; evaluate only if strong fit with specific use case |

### Magic Quadrant Scoring Methodology

Gartner does not publish exact weights, but industry research and AR community consensus indicates approximate weighting:

| Criterion Group | Estimated Weight | Key Evidence Types |
|----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Product / Service (Execution) | 20–25% | Feature completeness, product demos, analyst hands-on |
| Customer Experience (Execution) | 15–20% | Reference customer interviews, support quality, NPS/retention data |
| Market Responsiveness (Execution) | 10–15% | Speed to market, responsiveness to customer requests |
| Sales Execution (Execution) | 10–15% | Win rates, deal size, geographic reach, channel coverage |
| Offering Strategy (Vision) | 15–20% | Roadmap ambition, differentiation clarity, ecosystem integrations |
| Market Understanding (Vision) | 10–15% | Vendor's stated view of where the market is going; analyst alignment |
| Innovation (Vision) | 5–10% | R&D investment, patent activity, novel approaches |

## Forrester Wave Framework

### Evaluation Structure

| Dimension | Weight Range | Key Criteria |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Current Offering** | 50% | Features present today; integration capability; security and compliance; usability |
| **Strategy** | 25% | Product vision; roadmap investment; partner ecosystem; market approach |
| **Market Presence** | 25% | Revenue/growth; customer base; geographic presence; brand recognition |

### Wave Positioning Categories

| Category | Criteria | Strategy Focus |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| **Leaders** | Strong current offering + strong strategy + significant market presence | Maintain by continuously improving on cited weaknesses |
| **Strong Performers** | Good offering + reasonable strategy; may lack scale | Investment in market presence and roadmap breadth |
| **Contenders** | Good market presence but weaker offering/strategy | Product investment to close feature gaps |
| **Challengers** | Good market presence; limited strategy | Vision articulation and roadmap investment |

## Reference Customer Management

### Reference Tier Model

| Tier | Criteria | Usage |
|------|---------|-------|
| Tier A | Large enterprise; measurable outcome; willing to speak freely; long tenure | Gartner MQ and Forrester Wave evaluations; highest-stakes deals |
| Tier B | Mid-market; good outcome; available but may have qualification questions | Secondary evaluations; supplemental references |
| Tier C | SMB or early-stage; limited metrics; available | Market Guides; lower-stakes inquiries |

### Reference Preparation Protocol

1. **Select**: Choose references whose outcomes map directly to evaluation criteria being scored
2. **Brief**: 30-minute call covering evaluation context, likely analyst questions, and the company's key messages
3. **Confirm**: Get explicit confirmation of availability for the evaluation window
4. **Do not coach**: Brief on context and messages — never instruct on what scores to give or how to answer specific questions; analysts flag coached references
5. **Track**: Record each reference's availability, briefing date, and evaluation usage to avoid reference fatigue

### Reference Fatigue Prevention

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Same reference used 3+ times in 18 months | Rotate to a fresh reference |
| Reference has been through >2 evaluations with analysts | Give a 12-month rest period |
| Reference relationship has soured or CS health score declined | Remove from active reference list immediately |

## Submission Consistency Requirements

All submitted claims must be verifiable and consistent with:

| Consistency Check | Source |
|------------------|--------|
| Public press releases and blog posts | Marketing archive |
| Investor / board materials | Finance team |
| Other simultaneous evaluation submissions | AR tracker |
| Customer-facing documentation | Product / CS team |
| Previous year's submission | AR tracker archive |

**Contradiction rule**: If any submitted claim contradicts a verifiable public source, revise the submission or update the public source before filing. Analysts detect contradictions.

## Year-Round AR Investment Model

A single submission cannot overcome poor year-round AR. The evaluation is influenced by every touchpoint.

| Quarter | Recommended Activities | Evaluation Impact |
|---------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Q1 (post-evaluation) | Debrief with analyst team; gap-closure plan actioned; product roadmap briefing | Sets expectations for next cycle |
| Q2 | Strategy briefing with primary evaluator; customer reference development | Shapes analyst's evolving opinion |
| Q3 | Product briefing with proof points; competitive positioning update | Reinforces execution capability |
| Q4 (pre-evaluation) | Evaluation prep briefing; submission strategy finalised; reference customers briefed | Direct evaluation preparation |

## Post-Publication Gap-Closure Planning

After each evaluation result, build a gap-closure plan covering:

| Gap Type | Short-Term Response | Long-Term Response |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Analyst perception gap (inaccurate caution) | Schedule correction briefing within 60 days | Provide ongoing evidence stream |
| Product capability gap (accurate caution) | Flag to Product for roadmap prioritisation | Feature delivery + analyst proof point briefing |
| Market presence gap (scale / geographic) | GTM expansion evidence; channel partner data | Revenue growth + geographic expansion plan |
| Customer experience gap (retention / support) | CS program improvements; NRR data | Long-term retention and satisfaction program |
