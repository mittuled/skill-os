# magic-quadrant-strategy

## Agent

L2 analyst relations manager responsible for analyst briefings, inquiry responses, Magic Quadrant strategy, and peer review platform management.

Department ethos: [ideal-marketing.md](../../../departments/marketing/ideal-marketing.md)

## Skill Description

Develops the strategy and submission for Gartner Magic Quadrant, Forrester Wave, and similar analyst evaluations that directly shape how enterprise buyers perceive and shortlist vendors.

## When to Use

- An analyst firm announces an upcoming evaluation cycle for the company's category and submissions open.
- The company's positioning in a previous evaluation was weaker than desired and a strategic push is needed for the next cycle.
- A new category definition emerges that the company should be included in to reach a different buyer segment.
- Product or market changes create an opportunity to move the company's position in an evaluation framework.

## Workflow

1. Identify the evaluation: confirm the analyst firm, evaluation type, category definition, timeline, and submission requirements.
2. Assess the company's current position: review the previous evaluation results, analyst feedback, and the gap between current placement and target placement.
3. Map the evaluation criteria to company strengths and gaps. Prioritise the criteria with the highest weighting and identify where the company can credibly improve its score.
4. Develop the positioning narrative: the strategic story that connects the company's vision, product capabilities, market traction, and customer outcomes to the evaluation criteria.
5. Coordinate the submission: gather product capability responses, customer reference lists, financial data, and roadmap details from across the organisation. Ensure consistency with other public statements.
6. Prepare customer references: brief reference customers on the evaluation context, likely questions, and the company's key messages. Confirm their availability and willingness.
7. Submit the completed questionnaire and supporting materials by the deadline. Log all submitted claims for future reference.
8. Prepare for the analyst demo and follow-up briefing: rehearse the demo flow, anticipate tough questions, and assign roles to internal participants.
9. Conduct the demo and briefing. Note analyst reactions, follow-up questions, and any concerns raised.
10. After publication, analyse the results: compare placement against target, review strengths and cautions, and develop a gap-closure plan for the next cycle.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Submitting inflated claims that cannot be verified by customer references.** *Why*: Analysts cross-reference vendor claims with customer feedback; inconsistencies destroy credibility and result in harsher evaluations.
- **Treating the evaluation as a one-time submission instead of a year-round effort.** *Why*: Analyst perceptions are shaped by every briefing and inquiry throughout the year; last-minute submissions cannot overcome months of neglect.
- **Focusing only on product capabilities and ignoring market presence criteria.** *Why*: Evaluations weight market traction, customer satisfaction, and sales execution alongside product features; a complete product with weak go-to-market still places poorly.
- **Selecting customer references without briefing them.** *Why*: Unprepared references may share negative experiences or miss opportunities to reinforce key themes, undermining the submission narrative.
- **Ignoring cautions from the previous evaluation.** *Why*: Analysts expect vendors to address cited weaknesses; repeated cautions signal a lack of self-awareness or execution ability.

## Output

**Success artifacts:**
- Evaluation strategy document with positioning narrative and criteria mapping
- Completed submission questionnaire with supporting materials
- Customer reference briefing packets
- Post-publication analysis with gap-closure plan for the next cycle

**Failure reporting:**
- Flag submission deadline risks at least 3 weeks in advance
- Escalate customer reference availability gaps or internal content blockers to marketing leadership immediately
