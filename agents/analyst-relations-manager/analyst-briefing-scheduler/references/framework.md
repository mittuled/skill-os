# Framework: Analyst Briefing Scheduler

Defines briefing types, frequency cadences, preparation methodology, and post-briefing protocols for managing analyst interactions with Gartner, Forrester, IDC, and peer firms.

## Briefing Types

| Type | Purpose | Duration | Frequency | Attendees |
|------|---------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Introductory Briefing | Establish relationship with a new analyst covering the category | 45–60 min | Once; repeat if analyst changes firms | AR Manager + CEO or CPO |
| Strategy Briefing | Share company direction, vision, and market thesis | 60–90 min | 1–2x per year | AR Manager + CEO + CPO |
| Product Briefing | Update on new capabilities, roadmap milestones, and differentiation | 45–60 min | Quarterly or post-major release | AR Manager + CPO + Product Lead |
| Evaluation Briefing | Structured interaction as part of a Gartner MQ, Forrester Wave, or peer evaluation | 60–90 min | As required by evaluation timeline | AR Manager + CEO + CPO + Sales Lead |
| Reference Customer Briefing | Prepare customer reference for analyst conversation | 30 min | Before any evaluation cycle | AR Manager + Customer (prep) |
| Emergency / Reactive Briefing | Respond to negative coverage, market shift, or analyst misconception | 30–45 min | As needed | AR Manager + CEO or CPO |

## Briefing Frequency Cadence

### Tier 1 Analysts (Primary Evaluators — Gartner MQ, Forrester Wave authors)

| Period | Recommended Touchpoints |
|--------|------------------------|
| Q1 | Strategy briefing: annual company direction and product roadmap |
| Q2 | Product briefing: major spring release or capability update |
| Q3 | Evaluation prep briefing: if evaluation cycle opens in H2 |
| Q4 | Product briefing: year-end update; relationship check-in |
| Between cycles | 1–2 informal inquiries (analyst-initiated) responded to within SLA |

### Tier 2 Analysts (Category Coverage — Market Guides, Research Notes)

| Period | Recommended Touchpoints |
|--------|------------------------|
| H1 | 1 strategy or product briefing |
| H2 | 1 product briefing or reactive briefing if relevant coverage published |
| Annual | Minimum 2 touchpoints per year to maintain relationship |

### Tier 3 Analysts (Adjacent Coverage — Occasional mentions)

| Period | Recommended Touchpoints |
|--------|------------------------|
| Annual | 1 introductory or update briefing if they publish on the category |
| Reactive | Respond to analyst-initiated inquiries within inquiry response SLA |

## Briefing Preparation Methodology

### Step 1: Analyst Research (2 weeks before briefing)

| Research Area | Sources | Output |
|--------------|---------|--------|
| Recent publications | Analyst firm portal, Google Scholar, LinkedIn | List of analyst's last 3–5 relevant publications; key themes and stated positions |
| Public statements | Webinars, podcasts, conference talks | Noted market views and definitions; known biases or focus areas |
| Competitor coverage | Analyst reports mentioning direct competitors | How analyst positions competitors; their stated strengths |
| Inquiry history | Internal AR tracker | Previous interactions; open commitments; questions raised last time |
| Evaluation criteria (if evaluation briefing) | Published methodology document | Criteria weighting; where company previously scored well/poorly |

### Step 2: Briefing Deck Construction

| Section | Content | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Company Overview | One-page: founded, HQ, funding, customer count, revenue tier (if shareable) | 1 slide |
| Market Thesis | The company's stated view of where the market is going and why | 1–2 slides |
| Product Differentiation | 3–5 key differentiators vs. category alternatives; supported by customer evidence | 2–3 slides |
| Customer Proof Points | 2–3 named or anonymised customer outcomes with specific metrics | 1–2 slides |
| Roadmap Highlights | 2–3 upcoming capabilities; no unannounced specifics without NDA | 1 slide |
| Key Messages | The 3 things the analyst should remember after the briefing | 1 slide |

**Deck Design Rules:**
- No more than 12 slides total for a 60-minute briefing
- Each slide should support a spoken narrative, not replace it
- No marketing superlatives — analysts are trained to dismiss unsupported claims
- Every claim must have a source (customer quote, data point, or verifiable reference)

### Step 3: Speaker Preparation (1 week before briefing)

| Prep Activity | Owner | Timing |
|--------------|-------|--------|
| Speaker briefing document delivered | AR Manager | 5 days before |
| Analyst background reviewed by speakers | All speakers | 3 days before |
| Message rehearsal / dry run | AR Manager facilitates | 2 days before; mandatory for Tier 1 eval briefings |
| Q&A prep: anticipated questions + approved answers | AR Manager | Completed before dry run |
| Logistics confirmed: platform, dial-in, NDA, materials | AR Manager | 2 days before |

## Post-Briefing Protocol

### Immediate Actions (Within 48 Hours)

| Action | Owner | Timing |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Thank-you note sent to analyst | AR Manager | Within 24 hours |
| All promised follow-up materials delivered | AR Manager | Within 48 hours |
| Key discussion points summary sent to analyst | AR Manager | Within 48 hours |
| Internal debrief notes recorded in AR tracker | AR Manager | Within 48 hours |

### AR Tracker Record Fields

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Date | YYYY-MM-DD |
| Analyst name + firm | Full name and firm |
| Briefing type | From briefing types table |
| Attendees | Names and titles |
| Key messages delivered | 3–5 bullet summary |
| Analyst reactions | Positive signals, concerns raised, questions asked |
| Open commitments | Action items made during briefing |
| Follow-up materials sent | What was sent and when |
| Next scheduled interaction | Date and type |
| Relationship temperature | Warm / Neutral / Cool |

## Common Analyst Firm Booking Systems

| Firm | Scheduling Method | Notes |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| Gartner | Gartner Connect portal or direct analyst email | Portal preferred; confirm analyst availability directly for eval briefings |
| Forrester | Forrester's analyst inquiry/briefing portal | Submit briefing request with agenda; approval required |
| IDC | Direct email to analyst or account manager | Less formal process; respond promptly to their scheduling requests |
| S&P Global Market Intelligence | Direct analyst contact | Smaller firm; personal relationship matters more |
| 451 Research / S&P | Direct analyst contact | Tech-focused; appreciate technical depth |
