# peer-review-platform-manager

## Agent

L2 analyst relations manager responsible for analyst briefings, inquiry responses, Magic Quadrant strategy, and peer review platform management.

Department ethos: [ideal-marketing.md](../../../departments/marketing/ideal-marketing.md)

## Skill Description

Manages the company's presence on G2, Capterra, and other peer review platforms, building the social proof that enterprise buyers consult during vendor evaluation.

## When to Use

- The company's review volume or ratings on key platforms are below competitive benchmarks.
- A new product or category listing needs establishing on peer review platforms.
- Quarterly review generation campaigns are due to maintain fresh, recent reviews.
- Negative reviews appear that require response and internal feedback routing.

## Workflow

1. Audit the company's current presence across peer review platforms: profile completeness, review volume, average rating, competitive ranking, and recency of reviews.
2. Optimise platform profiles: update product descriptions, feature lists, screenshots, integrations, and pricing information to reflect current offerings.
3. Design the review generation programme: identify target reviewers from happy customers, create outreach templates, and set up incentive structures that comply with platform policies.
4. Launch review solicitation campaigns timed to follow positive customer milestones: successful onboarding, renewal, expansion, or support resolution.
5. Monitor new reviews daily. Respond to all reviews within platform guidelines: thank positive reviewers and address negative reviews with empathy and resolution steps.
6. Route feedback from negative reviews to the relevant internal team: product for feature gaps, support for service issues, and CS for relationship risks.
7. Track competitive review trends: monitor competitor ratings, review volume, and sentiment shifts that could affect buyer perception.
8. Report monthly on platform health: review volume trend, average rating, competitive positioning, and review-driven website traffic and lead attribution.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Soliciting reviews only from the happiest customers.** *Why*: Platforms detect and penalise review manipulation; a mix of genuine reviews including constructive criticism builds more credible social proof.
- **Ignoring negative reviews instead of responding.** *Why*: Unaddressed negative reviews signal to buyers that the company does not care about customer feedback; public responses demonstrate accountability.
- **Treating review platforms as a set-and-forget profile.** *Why*: Stale profiles with outdated screenshots and descriptions undermine credibility; platforms reward active, updated listings with better visibility.
- **Offering incentives that violate platform terms of service.** *Why*: Platforms penalise or delist companies caught incentivising reviews improperly, destroying the entire review investment.

## Output

**Success artifacts:**
- Optimised platform profiles with current product information
- Review generation campaign plan with target reviewers and outreach cadence
- Monthly platform health report with ratings, volume, and competitive benchmarks
- Review response log with internal feedback routing records

**Failure reporting:**
- Flag sudden rating drops or clusters of negative reviews within 24 hours
- Escalate platform policy violations or delisting risks to marketing leadership immediately
