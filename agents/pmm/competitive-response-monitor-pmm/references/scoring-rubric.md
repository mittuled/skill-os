# Scoring Rubric: Competitive Response Monitor

Evaluates the quality and timeliness of competitive response activities, from signal detection through battle card distribution and follow-up tracking.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Signal Detection Accuracy | 20% | Quality of signal sourcing, verification, and threat-level classification |
| 2 | Response Timeliness | 20% | Speed from verified signal to distributed response within SLA window |
| 3 | Counter-Positioning Quality | 25% | Strength of differentiation arguments, proof points, and objection handlers in the response |
| 4 | Cross-Functional Validation | 15% | Evidence of product accuracy review and sales relevance confirmation |
| 5 | Distribution & Adoption | 20% | Completeness of distribution, sales notification, and playbook index update |
| **Total** | | **100%** | |

## Scale

Each criterion is scored **0-10**:
- **0**: No evidence / completely absent
- **5**: Partially present with significant gaps
- **10**: Fully present, comprehensive, no gaps

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score × weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Exceptional | Signal classified, response validated, and distributed within 24 hours with strong counter-positioning and proof points | Archive as exemplar; monitor impact on win rate over 30 days |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Strong | Response delivered within SLA with solid counter-positioning; minor gaps in proof points or distribution breadth | Approve; backfill missing proof points within one week |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Good | Response produced on time but counter-positioning lacks specificity or one validation step was skipped | Approve conditionally; add missing validation within 48 hours |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Adequate | Response exists but is generic, late, or missing cross-functional validation | Revise counter-positioning before distribution; escalate if SLA breach |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | Weak | Signal acknowledged but response is incomplete, unvalidated, or not distributed | Rework from assessment stage with PMM lead oversight |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Failing | No meaningful response produced or signal went undetected | Conduct post-mortem; review competitive monitoring coverage |

## Signal Tables

### Signal Detection Accuracy
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Signal sourced from two or more independent channels (e.g., sales call + press release); threat level classified with documented rationale; competitor correctly identified and tagged |
| 7-8 | Signal sourced and verified from at least one reliable channel; threat level assigned but rationale is brief |
| 5-6 | Signal detected but not verified against a second source; threat classification is present but uses vague labels |
| 3-4 | Signal noted informally without source documentation; no threat classification applied |
| 0-2 | Competitive move went undetected or was dismissed without investigation |

### Response Timeliness
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Response drafted, validated, and distributed within 24 hours of verified signal for strategic threats; 48 hours for tactical |
| 7-8 | Response distributed within 48 hours for strategic threats; 72 hours for tactical |
| 5-6 | Response delivered within one week but outside the defined SLA window |
| 3-4 | Response took more than one week; sales encountered the competitive move in deals before receiving guidance |
| 0-2 | No response produced; competitive signal aged out without action |

### Counter-Positioning Quality
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Counter-positioning directly addresses the competitor's move with three or more proof points; includes objection handlers and a recommended talk track tested with AEs |
| 7-8 | Counter-positioning addresses the move with two proof points and objection handlers; talk track present but untested |
| 5-6 | Counter-positioning is generic ("we are better because...") with one or fewer proof points; objection handlers missing |
| 3-4 | Response restates the competitor's announcement without actionable counter-arguments |
| 0-2 | No counter-positioning; response is purely informational with no sales-ready content |

### Cross-Functional Validation
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Product confirmed technical accuracy; sales leadership confirmed field relevance; feedback incorporated within one review cycle |
| 7-8 | One of product or sales validated; the other acknowledged but did not formally review |
| 5-6 | Validation requested but not completed before distribution; follow-up pending |
| 3-4 | No validation sought; response distributed based solely on PMM's assessment |
| 0-2 | Response contains technical inaccuracies that product would have caught or field-irrelevant messaging |

### Distribution & Adoption
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Battle card pushed to enablement platform, posted in competitive-intel channel, flagged in sales standup; playbook index updated with date and signal type |
| 7-8 | Distributed via two channels; playbook index updated; sales standup mention pending |
| 5-6 | Distributed via one channel only; playbook index not yet updated |
| 3-4 | Shared informally (e.g., DM to one sales manager) without systematic distribution |
| 0-2 | Response exists in a PMM document but was never distributed to field teams |
