# Framework: Market Intelligence Synthesis

Reference framework for collecting, triangulating, and synthesising market intelligence into actionable insights.

## Intelligence Question Framework

Every intelligence effort starts with a defined question:

| Question Type | Example | Typical Decision It Informs |
|---------------|---------|---------------------------|
| Competitive | "Is competitor X winning deals we used to win?" | Positioning refresh, battle card update |
| Market sizing | "Is segment Y large enough for a vertical motion?" | GTM strategy, resource allocation |
| Buyer behaviour | "How are buying committees changing in our ICP?" | Sales motion, messaging hierarchy |
| Pricing | "What is the market willingness-to-pay for capability Z?" | Pricing strategy, packaging |
| Trend | "Is the market consolidating or fragmenting?" | Category strategy, M&A consideration |

## Source Triangulation Model

Insights require corroboration from at least two independent source types:

| Source Type | Strengths | Biases | Examples |
|-------------|-----------|--------|----------|
| Analyst reports | Broad market perspective, structured frameworks | Vendor influence, lagging indicators | Gartner MQ, Forrester Wave, IDC MarketScape |
| Review sites | Authentic buyer sentiment, feature-level detail | Self-selection bias, recency bias | G2, TrustRadius, Capterra |
| Win/loss interviews | Direct buyer decision rationale | Survivorship bias, social desirability | Structured post-decision interviews |
| Sales call recordings | Real-time buyer language and objections | Filtered through sales narrative | Gong/Chorus transcripts |
| Customer advisory boards | Deep qualitative insight from power users | Existing customer bias (not prospects) | CAB meeting notes |
| Internal data | Quantitative usage and churn signals | Does not capture "why" behind behaviour | NPS, CSAT, usage analytics, churn reasons |

### Confidence Level Assignment

| Confidence | Definition | Evidence Required |
|------------|-----------|-------------------|
| High | Insight confirmed by 3+ independent source types with consistent direction | Multiple corroborating data points across analyst, buyer, and internal sources |
| Medium | Insight supported by 2 source types with partial alignment | Two sources agree on direction but differ on magnitude or specifics |
| Low | Insight based on single source or conflicting data | One source only, or two sources that contradict each other |

## Intelligence Brief Structure

| Section | Content | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Intelligence question | The specific question this brief answers | 1-2 sentences |
| Answer | Direct answer to the question, stated upfront | 2-3 sentences |
| Supporting evidence | Key findings organised by theme, with source attribution and confidence levels | Bulk of the brief |
| Implications | What this means for the specific decision | 3-5 bullet points |
| Recommendations | Concrete actions to take based on the intelligence | Prioritised list |
| Sources | Full list of sources consulted with dates | Reference section |

## Data Tagging Taxonomy

When collecting data points, tag each with:

| Tag | Values |
|-----|--------|
| Theme | Buyer need, competitive dynamic, pricing perception, market trend, adoption blocker, category shift |
| Source type | Analyst, review-site, win/loss, sales-call, CAB, internal-data |
| Confidence | High, medium, low |
| Recency | Current (< 3 months), recent (3-6 months), dated (> 6 months) |
| Relevance | Primary (directly answers the question), supporting (provides context), tangential |

## Intelligence Refresh Cadence

| Intelligence Type | Refresh Frequency | Trigger for Ad-Hoc Refresh |
|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Competitive landscape | Quarterly | Major competitor move (funding, pivot, acquisition) |
| Win/loss synthesis | Monthly | Win rate drops >5pp or new competitor emerges in >10% of deals |
| Market sizing | Annually | New segment entry consideration |
| Pricing perception | Semi-annually | Pricing change planned or competitor reprices |
| Buyer behaviour trends | Quarterly | Sales cycle length changes or conversion rates shift |
