# Framework: Launch Narrative Construction

Reference framework for building launch narratives that create a cohesive story across all channels.

## Three-Act Narrative Structure

Every launch narrative follows a three-act structure:

| Act | Purpose | Content | Duration in Pitch |
|-----|---------|---------|-------------------|
| Act 1: The Problem | Create tension and urgency | The status quo is broken; here is why it matters now | 30% of narrative |
| Act 2: The Solution | Resolve the tension | Here is what we built and how it works | 40% of narrative |
| Act 3: The Proof | Build credibility and momentum | Here is the evidence and what happens next | 30% of narrative |

## Messaging Hierarchy Template

```
Headline (under 12 words, concrete, benefit-oriented)
├── Sub-headline (expands headline with specificity and persona relevance)
├── Proof Point 1: [Claim] + [Evidence] + [Buyer outcome]
├── Proof Point 2: [Claim] + [Evidence] + [Buyer outcome]
├── Proof Point 3: [Claim] + [Evidence] + [Buyer outcome]
└── CTA (specific next action: try, book demo, read more)
```

## Headline Quality Bar

A launch headline must pass the "forward test": would a buyer forward this to their team?

| Criterion | Pass Example | Fail Example |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Concrete | "Close deals 40% faster with AI-powered proposals" | "The future of sales is here" |
| Benefit-oriented | "Never lose a deal to slow quoting again" | "Introducing QuotePro 3.0" |
| Under 12 words | "Real-time pricing intelligence for every sales conversation" | "We are proud to announce our latest product that helps sales teams" |
| Persona-relevant | "RevOps teams: automate your pricing workflow" | "A better way to work" |

## Proof Point Construction

Each proof point requires three components:

| Component | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Claim | What you assert is true | "Reduces quote turnaround from days to minutes" |
| Evidence | Verifiable support for the claim | "Beta customers averaged 4.2-hour turnaround vs. 3.1-day baseline" |
| Buyer Outcome | What the buyer gets from this | "Reps spend time selling, not waiting for pricing approvals" |

### Evidence Hierarchy (strongest to weakest)
1. Customer metric with named logo
2. Aggregate customer data (anonymised)
3. Third-party validation (analyst, reviewer)
4. Product demonstration
5. Internal benchmark (weakest; avoid as sole evidence)

## Narrative Brief Template

The narrative brief is the single-page source of truth for all launch content:

1. **Launch context**: What is being launched, tier level, target date
2. **Positioning reference**: Link to the canonical positioning document
3. **Headline + sub-headline**: Approved launch messaging
4. **Three-act narrative**: Problem, solution, proof (two to three paragraphs)
5. **Proof points**: Three proof points with claim + evidence + outcome
6. **Key visuals**: Description of hero visual or demo moment
7. **CTA**: Primary call-to-action for the launch
8. **Approved quotes**: Customer, executive, or partner quotes cleared for use

## Channel Adaptation Rules

The narrative is one story told at different depths:

| Channel | Depth | Emphasis |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Social post | Headline + CTA | Hook and link |
| Email | Headline + sub-headline + one proof point + CTA | Value and urgency |
| Blog post | Full three-act narrative | Story and evidence |
| Sales deck | Full narrative + all proof points + demo | Persuasion and objection handling |
| Press release | Headline + context + quotes + proof | Credibility and newsworthiness |
| In-app | Sub-headline + CTA | Adoption and activation |
