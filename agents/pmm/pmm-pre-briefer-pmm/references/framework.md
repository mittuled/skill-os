# Framework: PMM Pre-Launch Briefing

Reference framework for preparing and delivering structured briefing sessions to sales and CS teams.

## Briefing Session Structure

| Section | Duration | Purpose | Format |
|---------|----------|---------|--------|
| Positioning overview | 5 min | Set context: what is launching, who it is for, why it matters | Presenter-led with 2-3 slides |
| Demo or walkthrough | 10 min | Show the product in action from a buyer's perspective | Live demo or recorded walkthrough |
| Objection handling | 10 min | Prepare reps for the most common buyer pushback | Interactive exercise |
| Competitive context | 5 min | Position this launch relative to competitors | Battle card highlights |
| Q&A | 10 min | Address open questions from the field | Open floor |

Total: 40 minutes. Do not exceed 45 minutes.

## Objection-Handling Exercise Format

1. **Present the objection**: Read the objection exactly as a buyer would state it
2. **Rep responds**: Have 2-3 reps practise the response live
3. **Coach**: PMM provides feedback on alignment with approved talk track
4. **Repeat**: Move to next objection

### Common Objection Categories for Launches
| Category | Example Objection |
|----------|------------------|
| Timing | "We just renewed with [competitor], can this wait?" |
| Value | "How is this different from what we already have?" |
| Risk | "This is new — how do we know it works at scale?" |
| Price | "This seems expensive for what it does" |
| Complexity | "We do not have the resources to implement this" |

## Briefing Pack Contents Checklist

- [ ] Launch narrative brief (one-pager)
- [ ] Enablement brief with full messaging framework
- [ ] Updated battle cards for relevant competitors
- [ ] Pricing and packaging summary
- [ ] FAQ document (top 10 questions from product and sales)
- [ ] Cheat sheet (one-page)
- [ ] Demo recording or script
- [ ] Customer quotes or case study excerpts cleared for use

## Post-Briefing Feedback Collection

Ask attendees within 24 hours:

1. How useful was the briefing? (1-5 scale)
2. What was the most valuable section?
3. What was unclear or needs more detail?
4. What objections are you still worried about?
5. Would you feel confident pitching this to a prospect today? (Yes/No/Need more prep)
