# Framework: outbound-prospecting-executor

Defines the prospecting cadence, multi-channel outreach patterns, and daily activity targets for structured outbound execution.

## Prospecting Cadence

### Standard Cadence (Mid-Market)

| Day | Channel | Action | Notes |
|-----|---------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Email | Personalized cold email (pain-based hook) | Use AIDA or PAS framework from cold-outreach-builder |
| 2 | LinkedIn | Connection request with custom note | Reference email topic; keep under 300 chars |
| 3 | Phone | First call attempt (morning slot) | Voicemail script if no answer; reference email |
| 5 | Email | Follow-up email (social proof angle) | Different value angle from Email 1 |
| 7 | LinkedIn | Engage with prospect's content + send InMail | Like/comment first, then message |
| 9 | Phone | Second call attempt (afternoon slot) | New talk track; reference content engagement |
| 12 | Email | Case study or resource share | Attach relevant customer story |
| 15 | LinkedIn | Share relevant content via DM | Industry report, article, or insight |
| 18 | Phone | Third call attempt + voicemail | Final phone attempt; reference all prior touches |
| 21 | Email | Breakup email with final value offer | Low-pressure; leave door open |

### Enterprise Cadence (2x Spacing)

Double all day intervals for C-suite and VP-level targets. Enterprise cadence runs 42 days total. Add executive-to-executive email at Day 14 (VP Sales to prospect's peer-level contact).

### Velocity Cadence (0.5x Spacing)

Half all day intervals for SMB and mid-market IC-level targets. Velocity cadence runs 11 days total. Drop LinkedIn touches if the persona is not LinkedIn-active.

## Multi-Channel Outreach Patterns

### Channel Mix by Persona

| Persona | Email | Phone | LinkedIn | Direct Mail | Events |
|---------|-------|-------|----------|-------------|--------|
| C-Suite | 30% | 20% | 20% | 20% | 10% |
| VP | 35% | 25% | 25% | 10% | 5% |
| Director | 40% | 25% | 30% | 5% | 0% |
| Manager | 45% | 25% | 25% | 5% | 0% |
| IC | 50% | 15% | 30% | 0% | 5% |

### Channel Sequencing Rules

1. **Email-first**: Always lead with email to establish context before other channels
2. **Phone follows email**: Never cold call without a prior email touch (gives the prospect context and the SDR a reference point)
3. **LinkedIn reinforces**: Use LinkedIn to reinforce email messaging, not repeat it
4. **Direct mail for enterprise**: Reserve physical touchpoints for deals >$50K ACV
5. **Events as accelerators**: Use event invitations as mid-sequence value-adds, not primary outreach

### Multi-Threading Pattern

When a single contact goes unresponsive after 3+ touches:
1. Identify a second contact in the same department (peer or direct report)
2. Reference the first contact: "I have been exploring [topic] with [Name]'s team..."
3. Add the second contact to a parallel mini-sequence (3 touches, 7 days)
4. If second contact engages, loop back to first contact with social proof

### Weekly Targets

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| New sequences started | 25-30 |
| Meetings booked | 7-10 |
| Pipeline generated ($) | Based on ACV x meetings x conversion rate |
| Response rate (email) | >5% |
| Connect rate (phone) | >8% |

| 16:00 - 16:30 | 30 min | Next-day preparation and priority sequencing |

## Prospect Prioritization Matrix

### Priority Tiers

| Tier | Criteria | Daily Allocation |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| P1 - Hot | ICP Tier 1 + active intent signal + identified champion | 40% of activity |
| P2 - Warm | ICP Tier 1-2 + passive intent signal or recent engagement | 35% of activity |
| P3 - Target | ICP Tier 1-2 + no intent signal but strong firmographic fit | 20% of activity |
| P4 - Nurture | ICP Tier 3 or Tier 1-2 with negative timing signals | 5% of activity (automated) |
