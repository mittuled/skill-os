# Scoring Rubric: CS Onboarding Playbook

Evaluates the quality of a customer onboarding playbook across value milestones, journey completeness, segment adaptability, handoff design, and measurement.

## Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Scale | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------------|
| Value Milestone Clarity | 25% | 0-10 | How well-defined and measurable the onboarding success milestones are |
| Journey Completeness | 25% | 0-10 | Coverage of the end-to-end onboarding journey: stages, touchpoints, and content |
| Segment Adaptability | 20% | 0-10 | How well the playbook adapts to different customer segments and use cases |
| Handoff Quality | 15% | 0-10 | Clarity of transitions between sales-to-CS and onboarding-to-steady-state |
| Measurement Framework | 15% | 0-10 | Robustness of onboarding metrics: time-to-value, completion rate, drop-off tracking |
| **Total** | **100%** | | |

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score × weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Exceptional | Measurable milestones tied to business outcomes; segment-specific tracks validated with data; automated metrics driving iteration | Deploy playbook and establish quarterly review cadence |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Strong | Well-defined milestones and journey; segment tracks exist; handoffs documented; metrics tracked with minor automation gaps | Deploy with plan to automate remaining manual metrics |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Good | Milestones defined but some lack quantitative criteria; journey mapped with minor gaps; one handoff needs tightening | Deploy for primary segment; iterate on gaps before expanding |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Adequate | Milestones are mix of measurable and vague; single playbook for all segments; metrics defined but not consistently tracked | Revise milestones and add segment-specific adaptations before launch |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | Weak | Milestones reflect internal steps not customer value; no segment variation; handoffs informal; no measurement | Redesign playbook with customer outcome focus and CS leadership input |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Failing | No structured onboarding journey; no milestones or success criteria defined | Commission playbook creation from scratch with cross-functional input |

## Signal Tables

### Value Milestone Clarity
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Milestones are specific, measurable, and tied to customer business outcomes; each milestone has quantitative success criteria; milestones validated with actual customer data |
| 7-8 | Milestones are well-defined with measurable criteria; most are tied to customer outcomes; minor gaps in quantification |
| 5-6 | Milestones exist but some are vague ("customer is set up"); mix of measurable and subjective criteria |
| 3-4 | Milestones are mostly subjective; no quantitative criteria; milestones reflect internal process steps rather than customer value |
| 1-2 | No defined milestones; onboarding has no clear definition of success or completion |

### Journey Completeness
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Every stage from contract signing to first value milestone is mapped; touchpoints, content, and customer actions specified per stage; timeline is realistic and validated |
| 7-8 | Major stages mapped with touchpoints and content; minor gaps in secondary paths or edge cases; timeline exists |
| 5-6 | Core stages exist but some touchpoints or content deliverables undefined; timeline is aspirational rather than validated |
| 3-4 | Journey is partially defined; significant gaps between stages; no timeline or unrealistic timeline |
| 1-2 | No journey map; onboarding is ad-hoc with no structured sequence |

### Segment Adaptability
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Distinct playbook tracks for enterprise, mid-market, and self-serve; each track has segment-specific content, timeline, and touchpoint cadence; tracks validated with segment-specific data |
| 7-8 | At least 2 segment tracks defined; key differences in approach documented; minor gaps in segment-specific content |
| 5-6 | Single playbook with notes on segment variations; some awareness of different needs but no distinct tracks |
| 3-4 | One-size-fits-all playbook acknowledged as a limitation; no segment-specific adaptations |
| 1-2 | Single playbook with no acknowledgment that different segments have different needs |

### Handoff Quality
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Both handoffs (sales-to-CS, onboarding-to-steady-state) have documented checklists; information transfer is complete; customer experiences no gap in communication |
| 7-8 | Handoff checklists exist for both transitions; most information transfers cleanly; minor gaps identified and documented |
| 5-6 | One handoff is well-defined but the other is informal; some information lost in transition |
| 3-4 | Handoffs are informal; key customer context regularly lost between teams; customer notices the transition gap |
| 1-2 | No defined handoff process; customer falls through the cracks between teams |

### Measurement Framework
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Time-to-value, completion rate, per-stage drop-off, and CSAT measured; metrics are automated and dashboarded; data used to iterate on the playbook |
| 7-8 | Core metrics defined and tracked; most are automated; some manual data collection; periodic review of metrics |
| 5-6 | Metrics defined but inconsistently tracked; mostly manual data collection; data reviewed ad-hoc |
| 3-4 | Only one or two metrics tracked; no automation; no review cadence; data is anecdotal |
| 1-2 | No onboarding metrics defined or tracked; success is determined by gut feeling |
