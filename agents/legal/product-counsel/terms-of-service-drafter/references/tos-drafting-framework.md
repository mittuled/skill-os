# Terms of Service Drafting Framework

Reference framework for drafting and maintaining terms of service covering mandatory provisions, jurisdictional requirements, and enforceability best practices.

## Core ToS Sections

| Section | Purpose | Key Provisions |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| Account Terms | Establish the user relationship | Eligibility, account creation, credentials responsibility, account termination |
| Acceptable Use | Define permitted and prohibited conduct | Content standards, prohibited activities, enforcement actions |
| Intellectual Property | Clarify ownership rights | Company IP, user content licence, DMCA process |
| Payment Terms | Govern billing relationship | Pricing, billing cycle, taxes, refunds, auto-renewal |
| Liability | Limit company exposure | Warranty disclaimer, liability cap, indemnification |
| Dispute Resolution | Define resolution mechanism | Governing law, arbitration clause, class action waiver, forum selection |
| Termination | Define ending conditions | Termination for convenience, termination for cause, effect of termination |
| Modifications | Reserve right to update | Change notification, material change definition, continued use acceptance |
| Privacy | Connect to privacy practices | Privacy policy reference, consent mechanisms |

## Jurisdictional Mandatory Requirements

### United States
- **Auto-renewal**: California ARL (Section 17602) requires clear disclosure, affirmative consent, and easy cancellation
- **COPPA**: Under-13 users require parental consent; terms must address age restrictions
- **CAN-SPAM**: Email terms must comply with commercial email rules
- **Arbitration**: Class action waivers enforceable under FAA but check state-specific carve-outs
- **State consumer protection**: Varies by state; unconscionability doctrine limits one-sided terms

### European Union
- **Unfair Contract Terms Directive**: Terms must not create significant imbalance contrary to good faith
- **Consumer Rights Directive**: 14-day cooling-off period for online purchases, pre-contractual information requirements
- **Digital Content Directive**: Conformity guarantees for digital content, remedies for non-conformity
- **DSA**: Platform terms must reference content moderation policies and appeal mechanisms
- **GDPR**: Privacy terms must meet transparency requirements (Articles 13-14)

### United Kingdom
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015**: Unfair terms test, transparency requirement
- **Consumer Contracts Regulations**: 14-day cancellation right

### Australia
- **Australian Consumer Law**: Unfair contract terms for standard form contracts, consumer guarantees cannot be excluded

## Enforceability Checklist

| Factor | Requirement | Risk if Missing |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Acceptance Mechanism | Clickwrap (checkbox + "I agree") preferred over browsewrap | Browsewrap may be unenforceable if notice insufficient |
| Conspicuousness | Terms link visible, not hidden | Court may find insufficient notice |
| Readability | Clear language, logical structure | Unconscionability risk for impenetrable terms |
| Material Changes | Notice + opportunity to reject | Changes may be unenforceable without notification |
| Version Control | Dated versions with change history | Cannot prove which terms applied at relevant time |
| Age Verification | Age gate for age-restricted services | COPPA liability, age-restricted content exposure |

## Acceptance Mechanism Comparison

| Mechanism | Enforceability | Best For |
|-----------|---------------|----------|
| Clickwrap (checkbox) | Strong | Account creation, material changes |
| Sign-in-wrap (acknowledge on sign-in) | Moderate-Strong | Existing users, minor updates |
| Browsewrap (terms link in footer) | Weak | Low-risk informational sites only |
| Scrollwrap (must scroll to bottom) | Moderate | Long agreements requiring full review |
