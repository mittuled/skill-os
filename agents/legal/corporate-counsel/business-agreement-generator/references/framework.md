# Agreement Framework: business-agreement-generator

Comprehensive framework covering 10 agreement types, the 15-section standard structure, and type-specific guidance for drafting business agreements.

## 10 Supported Agreement Types

### 1. Services Agreement
- **Key characteristics**: One party provides defined services to another for compensation
- **Essential unique clauses**: Service level agreements (SLAs), acceptance criteria, change order process, service credits
- **Common pitfalls**: Vague scope leading to scope creep, missing acceptance procedures, undefined service levels
- **Negotiation focus**: Scope definition, SLA metrics and remedies, liability caps relative to fees

### 2. SaaS License Agreement
- **Key characteristics**: Subscription-based access to software hosted by the provider
- **Essential unique clauses**: Uptime commitments, data ownership and portability, subscription tiers, usage limits, API access
- **Common pitfalls**: Unclear data ownership on termination, auto-renewal without notice, uncapped price increases
- **Negotiation focus**: Data return/destruction on termination, SLA credits, price escalation caps

### 3. Reseller/Channel Agreement
- **Key characteristics**: One party resells the other's products or services to end customers
- **Essential unique clauses**: Territory and exclusivity, margin structure, end-customer terms flow-down, deal registration
- **Common pitfalls**: Channel conflict provisions missing, unclear end-customer support responsibility, territory overlap
- **Negotiation focus**: Exclusivity scope, minimum commitments, margin protection, co-marketing obligations

### 4. Strategic Partnership Agreement
- **Key characteristics**: Two parties collaborate on joint initiatives with shared objectives
- **Essential unique clauses**: Joint governance structure, resource commitments, success metrics, joint IP ownership
- **Common pitfalls**: Undefined decision-making authority, no exit mechanism for underperformance, IP ownership ambiguity
- **Negotiation focus**: Governance and decision rights, IP ownership of joint deliverables, exclusivity and non-compete

### 5. Joint Venture Agreement
- **Key characteristics**: Parties create a separate entity or formal collaboration for a specific business purpose
- **Essential unique clauses**: Capital contributions, profit/loss allocation, management structure, deadlock resolution, exit mechanisms
- **Common pitfalls**: Missing deadlock resolution, unclear capital call procedures, no exit valuation methodology
- **Negotiation focus**: Control rights, exit triggers and valuation, non-compete scope, capital commitment limits

### 6. Distribution Agreement
- **Key characteristics**: One party distributes physical or digital products on behalf of the manufacturer/publisher
- **Essential unique clauses**: Territory rights, inventory obligations, minimum purchase commitments, return policies, marketing requirements
- **Common pitfalls**: Inventory risk allocation unclear, territory cannibalization, price maintenance issues
- **Negotiation focus**: Exclusivity vs. minimum commitments, inventory risk, price protection, termination inventory handling

### 7. Consulting Agreement
- **Key characteristics**: Expert provides advisory or implementation services, typically project-based
- **Essential unique clauses**: Deliverable specifications, milestone payments, work product ownership, background IP carve-outs
- **Common pitfalls**: IP ownership of pre-existing materials unclear, scope creep without change orders, payment tied to subjective acceptance
- **Negotiation focus**: Work product ownership vs. license, background IP protections, milestone definitions, payment triggers

### 8. Referral Agreement
- **Key characteristics**: One party refers potential customers to another in exchange for referral fees
- **Essential unique clauses**: Referral qualification criteria, fee calculation and payment timing, attribution window, exclusivity of referral
- **Common pitfalls**: Disputed attribution, referral fee on renewals unclear, no minimum quality standard for referrals
- **Negotiation focus**: Attribution methodology, fee duration (initial sale vs. lifetime), exclusivity period, minimum referral volume

### 9. OEM/White-Label Agreement
- **Key characteristics**: One party's product is rebranded and sold as part of another party's offering
- **Essential unique clauses**: Branding rights and restrictions, customization scope, source code access, support tiering, sublicensing terms
- **Common pitfalls**: Unclear branding boundaries, customization costs not allocated, end-customer support confusion
- **Negotiation focus**: Branding control, customization rights and costs, exclusivity, pricing structure (per-unit vs. flat fee)

### 10. Franchise Agreement
- **Key characteristics**: Franchisor grants rights to operate a business using its system, brand, and methods
- **Essential unique clauses**: Franchise fee structure, territory protection, operational standards, training requirements, audit rights
- **Common pitfalls**: Territorial encroachment, unclear renewal conditions, excessive franchisor control without support
- **Negotiation focus**: Territory protection, renewal rights, fee escalation, franchisor support obligations

## 15-Section Standard Structure

| # | Section | Description | Applies To |
|---|---------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | Recitals | Background, purpose, and context of the agreement | All types |
| 2 | Definitions | Defined terms used throughout the agreement | All types |
| 3 | Scope of Services/Rights | What is being provided, licensed, or granted | All types |
| 4 | Term and Renewal | Duration, renewal mechanisms, and notice periods | All types |
| 5 | Compensation | Payment terms, fees, royalties, revenue sharing | All types |
| 6 | Intellectual Property | IP ownership, licensing, restrictions, background IP | All types |
| 7 | Confidentiality | Non-disclosure obligations, exceptions, duration | All types |
| 8 | Representations & Warranties | Promises about authority, compliance, non-infringement | All types |
| 9 | Indemnification | Hold-harmless provisions, defense obligations, procedures | All types |
| 10 | Limitation of Liability | Caps on damages, consequential damages exclusions | All types |
| 11 | Termination | For-cause triggers, convenience rights, effect of termination | All types |
| 12 | Dispute Resolution | Negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or litigation process | All types |
| 13 | Governing Law | Jurisdiction, venue, choice of law | All types |
| 14 | Force Majeure | Excused performance events, notification, mitigation | All types |
| 15 | Miscellaneous | Entire agreement, amendments, assignment, notices, severability, waiver, counterparts | All types |

## Type-Specific Clause Variations

### Scope (Section 3)
- **Services/Consulting**: Detailed SOW with deliverables, acceptance criteria, change order process
- **SaaS License**: Subscription tiers, feature access, usage limits, API rate limits
- **Reseller/Channel**: Authorized products, territory, end-customer restrictions
- **Partnership/JV**: Joint activities, resource commitments, governance

### Compensation (Section 5)
- **Services**: Fixed fee, T&M with cap, milestone-based with acceptance triggers
- **SaaS**: Subscription fees, usage-based pricing, price escalation caps
- **Reseller**: Discount off list, margin guarantee, volume rebates
- **Referral**: Percentage of first-year revenue, flat fee per qualified referral
- **Franchise**: Initial franchise fee, ongoing royalties (% of gross revenue), marketing fund contributions

### IP (Section 6)
- **Services/Consulting**: Work product assignment vs. license, background IP carve-out
- **SaaS/OEM**: License grant (not assignment), customization ownership, derivative works
- **Partnership/JV**: Joint IP ownership, commercialization rights, post-termination licenses

### Termination (Section 11)
- **SaaS**: Data return/destruction obligations, transition assistance period
- **Reseller/Distribution**: Sell-through rights for existing inventory, end-customer continuity
- **Franchise**: Post-termination non-compete, de-identification requirements
- **JV**: Buyout mechanisms, asset distribution, winding-down procedures
