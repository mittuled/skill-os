## Section 3: Scope of Services/Rights

[CUSTOMIZE: Define exactly what is being provided, licensed, or granted.

[TYPE-SPECIFIC: Services]

[TYPE-SPECIFIC: SaaS License]

[TYPE-SPECIFIC: Reseller/Channel]
Authorized products, territory, pricing authority, end-customer terms flow-down.

[TYPE-SPECIFIC: Partnership/JV]

GUIDANCE:

- [REVIEW]: Ensure scope is specific enough to prevent disputes but flexible enough for reasonable changes]

## Section 6: Intellectual Property

[CUSTOMIZE: IP ownership, licensing, and restrictions.

**License Grants**: [CUSTOMIZE: Scope, exclusivity, territory, sublicensing rights].

## Section 7: Confidentiality

[CUSTOMIZE: Non-disclosure obligations, exceptions, and duration.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Each party shall maintain the confidentiality of the other party's Confidential Information for 3 years after termination. Exceptions: (a) publicly available, (b) independently developed, (c) received from third party without restriction, (d) required by law with notice."
- Bad: Perpetual confidentiality with no exceptions; one-sided obligations
- Format: Prose with numbered exceptions; specify duration]

## Section 8: Representations and Warranties

[CUSTOMIZE: Promises about authority, compliance, non-infringement, and quality.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Each party represents that (a) it has authority to enter this Agreement, (b) performance will not violate any other agreement, (c) Deliverables will conform to the specifications in Exhibit A for 90 days after acceptance."
- Bad: One party makes extensive warranties while the other disclaims all warranties
- Format: Numbered representations; keep mutual where possible
- [REVIEW]: Warranty scope and duration — negotiate based on deal value]

## Section 9: Indemnification

[CUSTOMIZE: Hold-harmless provisions, defense obligations, and procedures.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Each party shall indemnify the other against third-party claims arising from (a) breach of representations, (b) negligence or willful misconduct, (c) IP infringement of materials provided by the indemnifying party. Indemnification procedure: prompt notice, defense control, cooperation, settlement approval."
- Bad: One-sided indemnification without reciprocal obligations
- Format: Prose with specific trigger events and procedures
- Annotation: Indemnification means one party agrees to cover the other party's losses from specified claims — essentially a promise to "make whole" if certain things go wrong.]

## Section 10: Limitation of Liability

[CUSTOMIZE: Caps on damages and consequential damages exclusions.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Each party's total aggregate liability shall not exceed the total Fees paid or payable in the 12 months preceding the claim. Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages. Carve-outs from the cap: indemnification obligations, breach of confidentiality, willful misconduct."
- Bad: Unlimited liability or no liability cap
- Format: Prose; specify cap amount, excluded damages, and carve-outs
- Annotation: This clause caps the maximum financial exposure from the agreement. The cap typically relates to fees paid, and certain serious breaches are "carved out" (excluded from the cap).
- [REVIEW]: Liability cap relative to deal value — standard is 12 months of fees]

## Section 11: Termination

[CUSTOMIZE: For-cause triggers, convenience rights, notice periods, and effect of termination.

[TYPE-SPECIFIC: SaaS] Data return/destruction within 30 days, transition assistance period.
[TYPE-SPECIFIC: Reseller/Distribution] Sell-through period for existing inventory.
[TYPE-SPECIFIC: Franchise] Post-termination non-compete, de-identification requirements.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Either party may terminate for convenience with 30 days' written notice. Either party may terminate for cause if the other party (a) materially breaches and fails to cure within 30 days of notice, or (b) becomes insolvent. Upon termination: all Fees earned through termination date are payable; Confidential Information returned or destroyed; license grants terminate except as specified."
- Bad: No termination for convenience; unclear post-termination obligations
- Format: Prose with specific triggers, cure periods, and post-termination obligations]

## Section 12: Dispute Resolution

[CUSTOMIZE: Escalation path from negotiation to mediation to arbitration or litigation.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Disputes shall be resolved by (1) good-faith negotiation between executives for 30 days, (2) mediation under [rules] for 60 days, (3) binding arbitration under [rules] in [venue]. Each party bears its own costs through mediation; prevailing party recovers costs in arbitration."
- Bad: "Disputes shall be resolved in court" without specifying venue, or skipping escalation steps
- Format: Numbered escalation steps with timeframes]

## Section 13: Governing Law

[CUSTOMIZE: Choice of law and venue.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflict of law principles. Exclusive venue for any litigation is the state or federal courts in Wilmington, Delaware."
- Bad: Silent on governing law or specifying a jurisdiction unfavorable to the company
- Format: Single paragraph; specify law, conflict-of-law exclusion, and venue]

## Section 14: Force Majeure

[CUSTOMIZE: Excused performance events, notification requirements, and mitigation obligations.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Neither party is liable for delays due to events beyond reasonable control (natural disaster, war, pandemic, government action, internet outage). Affected party must notify within 5 business days, mitigate impact, and resume performance when possible. If force majeure continues for 90+ days, either party may terminate."
- Bad: Overly broad force majeure that excuses payment obligations, or no termination right for extended force majeure
- Format: Prose with notification timeline, mitigation duty, and termination trigger]

## Section 15: Miscellaneous

[Standard boilerplate provisions.

- **Entire Agreement**: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes all prior negotiations.
- **Amendments**: Modifications require written agreement signed by both parties.
- **Assignment**: Neither party may assign without prior written consent, except in connection with a merger or acquisition.
- **Notices**: Written notices to addresses specified in the preamble; email acceptable with read receipt.
- **Severability**: If any provision is unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in effect.
- **Waiver**: Failure to enforce a provision is not a waiver of future enforcement.
- **Counterparts**: May be executed in counterparts, each constituting an original.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Include all 7 standard provisions; customize assignment restrictions based on deal
- Bad: Omitting severability or entire agreement clause
- Format: Bulleted list with one paragraph per provision]
