# Founder Equity Issuance Framework

Reference framework for structuring and executing founder equity issuance including securities law compliance, vesting terms, and documentation.

## Securities Law Framework

### Federal Exemptions
- **Rule 701** (17 CFR 230.701): Exemption for compensatory equity grants by non-reporting companies. Limits: greater of $1M, 15% of total assets, or 15% of outstanding securities in any 12-month period. Above $10M threshold in 12 months requires delivery of additional financial and risk disclosures.
- **Section 4(a)(2)**: Private placement exemption — no general solicitation, limited number of sophisticated offerees, disclosure of material information. Safe harbor under Regulation D Rule 506(b): up to 35 non-accredited investors (must be sophisticated), unlimited accredited investors.
- **Regulation D Rule 504**: Exemption for offerings up to $10M in 12-month period. May allow general solicitation in some cases.

### State Securities (Blue Sky) Laws
- **California**: Section 25102(f) exemption for issuances to 35 or fewer purchasers (must be officers, directors, or meet suitability standards). Section 25102(o) for compensatory plan issuances — requires board and stockholder approval.
- **New York**: Section 359-f exemptions for limited offerings. Martin Act implications for securities fraud.
- **Delaware**: No state-level registration requirement for issuances by Delaware entities to Delaware residents; rely on federal exemption for others.

### Compliance Documentation
- Securities exemption memo identifying the specific exemption relied upon
- Form D filing with SEC within 15 days of first sale (if relying on Regulation D)
- State notice filings as required by the applicable exemption
- Subscription agreements or stock purchase agreements as evidence of the transaction

## Founder Vesting Terms

### Standard Terms
| Term | Standard | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Vesting Period | 4 years | Industry standard for VC-backed startups |
| Cliff | 1 year | 25% vests at 12-month anniversary |
| Post-Cliff Vesting | Monthly (1/48 per month) | Some use quarterly; monthly is more employee-friendly |
| Acceleration (Single Trigger) | Not standard | Full acceleration on change of control; disfavored by investors |
| Acceleration (Double Trigger) | Common | 25-100% acceleration on termination without cause within 12 months of CoC |
| Repurchase Right | At lower of FMV or cost | Company right to repurchase unvested shares upon termination |

### Multi-Founder Considerations
- **Unequal splits**: Document the rationale (contribution history, go-forward commitment, capital contribution)
- **Different vesting starts**: If founders joined at different times, start vesting from each founder's join date, not company formation
- **Founder departure provisions**: Specify what happens to unvested shares (automatic forfeiture vs. company call option) and vested shares (ROFR, co-sale rights)
- **IP contribution credit**: Some founders receive vesting credit for pre-incorporation IP contribution

## Restricted Stock Purchase Agreement (RSPA) Key Terms

### Required Provisions
1. **Purchase Price**: Par value or FMV at time of grant (supported by 409A valuation or board determination)
2. **Vesting Schedule**: Explicit dates and percentages; cliff date; post-cliff monthly or quarterly schedule
3. **Repurchase Right**: Company option to repurchase unvested shares at the lower of original cost or current FMV upon termination of service
4. **Transfer Restrictions**: Shares may not be transferred without company consent; ROFR on any proposed transfer
5. **Section 83(b) Election**: Acknowledgment that recipient has been advised of the election option and the 30-day deadline
6. **Representations**: Recipient represents investment intent, accredited/sophisticated investor status, understanding of restrictions
7. **Spousal Consent**: If in a community property state (CA, TX, WA, etc.), spousal consent to restrictions and repurchase rights

### Optional Provisions
- **Lock-up Agreement**: Restriction on sales for 180 days following IPO
- **Drag-Along Rights**: Obligation to participate in a sale approved by requisite stockholders
- **Co-Sale Rights**: Right to participate in a founder's proposed transfer on pro rata basis
- **Market Standoff**: Agreement not to sell during specified periods

## Board Authorization Requirements

1. Board resolution authorizing issuance of shares to each named founder
2. Confirmation that issuance is within authorized share limits per Certificate of Incorporation
3. Determination of FMV for tax and accounting purposes
4. Approval of RSPA form and individual agreements
5. Confirmation of securities exemption relied upon
6. If share reserve exceeds certain thresholds, stockholder approval may be required

## Tax Considerations

### Without 83(b) Election
- No taxable event at grant (property subject to substantial risk of forfeiture under IRC 83(a))
- Ordinary income recognized at each vesting milestone: (FMV at vesting - amount paid) × shares vesting
- Tax withholding obligations at each vesting event
- Future gain above FMV at vesting taxed as capital gains

### With 83(b) Election (Filed within 30 days)
- Ordinary income recognized at grant: (FMV at grant - amount paid) × total shares
- For par value purchases at formation, taxable income is typically negligible ($0.00001 × shares)
- All future appreciation taxed as capital gains (long-term if held > 1 year from grant)
- Risk: if shares are forfeited (founder leaves before vesting), no tax deduction for income previously recognized
- Coordinate with 83b-election-coordinator for filing
