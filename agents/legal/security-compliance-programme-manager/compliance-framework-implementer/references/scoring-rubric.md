# Scoring Rubric: Compliance Framework Implementer

Evaluates compliance framework implementation quality across scope definition, control mapping, gap remediation, evidence maturity, and certification readiness.

## Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Scale | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------------|
| Scope Definition | 15% | 0-10 | Clarity and accuracy of framework scope: systems, data, and processes included/excluded |
| Control Mapping Coverage | 25% | 0-10 | Completeness of requirement-to-control mapping and cross-framework leverage |
| Gap Remediation Quality | 25% | 0-10 | Thoroughness of remediation: controls implemented, validated, and operationalized |
| Evidence Framework Maturity | 20% | 0-10 | Automation and reliability of evidence collection, storage, and review processes |
| Certification Readiness | 15% | 0-10 | Outcome of pre-certification internal audit: residual gaps, auditor-readiness |
| **Total** | **100%** | | |

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score × weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Exceptional | 100% controls mapped and validated; automated evidence collection; internal audit passed with zero findings | Schedule external auditor engagement for certification |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Strong | Controls mapped with minor gaps; evidence mostly automated; internal audit passed with minor findings remediated | Proceed to external audit with remediation timeline for minor items |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Good | 95%+ controls mapped; gap remediation underway; evidence framework functional but mostly manual | Complete remediation and automate evidence before scheduling external audit |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Adequate | 80-94% controls mapped; material gaps in remediation; evidence collection is manual and inconsistent | Accelerate remediation; delay certification timeline by one quarter |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | Weak | Under 80% mapped; controls exist on paper but are not operational; no evidence framework | Reset implementation plan; assign dedicated compliance engineering resource |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Failing | No systematic control mapping; no operational controls; compliance claims are unsubstantiated | Halt and engage external compliance consultancy for full programme build |

## Signal Tables

### Scope Definition
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Scope explicitly lists all in-scope systems, data types, and processes; exclusions are justified; scope is approved by stakeholders and auditor-validated |
| 7-8 | Scope is well-defined with clear boundaries; minor ambiguity in edge cases; stakeholder sign-off obtained |
| 5-6 | Scope exists but some systems or data flows are ambiguously categorized; no formal stakeholder approval |
| 3-4 | Scope is vague or overly broad; critical systems may be inadvertently excluded or included |
| 1-2 | No documented scope; implementation is proceeding without clear boundaries |

### Control Mapping Coverage
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | 100% of framework requirements mapped to controls; cross-framework mappings leverage shared controls; gap analysis is complete with zero unmapped requirements |
| 7-8 | 95%+ requirements mapped; cross-framework leverage identified for major overlaps; gaps clearly catalogued |
| 5-6 | 80-94% requirements mapped; some requirements lack clear control ownership; limited cross-framework analysis |
| 3-4 | Under 80% mapped; significant unmapped requirements; no cross-framework analysis performed |
| 1-2 | Mapping is incomplete or absent; requirements are addressed ad-hoc without systematic tracking |

### Gap Remediation Quality
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | All gaps remediated with controls validated as operating effectively; technical, administrative, and physical controls balanced; every control has an owner and operating procedure |
| 7-8 | Most gaps remediated; controls are implemented and tested; minor gaps have documented remediation timelines |
| 5-6 | Gaps identified and remediation underway; some controls implemented but not yet validated; ownership assigned |
| 3-4 | Many gaps remain open; remediation is behind schedule; controls exist on paper but are not operational |
| 1-2 | Gaps identified but no remediation action taken; policies written but no operational controls exist |

### Evidence Framework Maturity
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | 80%+ of evidence collection is automated; evidence is continuously collected and stored in an auditable repository; review cadence is defined and followed |
| 7-8 | Mix of automated and manual evidence collection; evidence is organized and retrievable; review cadence exists |
| 5-6 | Mostly manual evidence collection; evidence is stored but not consistently organized; review happens ad-hoc |
| 3-4 | Evidence collection is entirely manual and reactive; evidence is scattered across systems; no review process |
| 1-2 | No evidence collection process; compliance claims cannot be substantiated with artifacts |

### Certification Readiness
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Internal audit passed with zero findings; all controls operating effectively for required observation period; auditor engagement scheduled |
| 7-8 | Internal audit completed with minor findings only; findings have remediation plans with timeline before external audit |
| 5-6 | Internal audit completed but material findings exist; remediation in progress; external audit may need to be delayed |
| 3-4 | Internal audit reveals significant gaps; multiple controls not operating; certification timeline at risk |
| 1-2 | No internal audit performed; control environment is immature; certification is not achievable in current state |
