# Checklist: safe-note-setup

Covers every step required to prepare, execute, and register a SAFE note correctly from term selection through cap table recording.

## Pre-Execution: SAFE Structure Selection

- [ ] Confirm SAFE variant: post-money YC SAFE (preferred) vs. pre-money SAFE (legacy) — document the decision rationale
- [ ] Decide on valuation cap: obtain CFO and board alignment on the cap amount
- [ ] Decide on discount: typical range is 10–20%; model the dilution impact of the discount before agreeing
- [ ] Decide on MFN provision: if included, audit all prior SAFEs — an MFN in a new SAFE may entitle earlier investors to the new terms
- [ ] Decide on pro-rata rights: will this SAFE holder have the right to invest in the next priced round to maintain ownership percentage?
- [ ] Confirm the SAFE is post-money: if post-money, each SAFE converts independently and the dilution is more predictable — verify with legal
- [ ] Run conversion model at 3 scenarios: next round at 1x cap, 1.5x cap, and 2x cap — CEO and CFO sign off on maximum dilution outcome

## Document Preparation

- [ ] Select the correct template: YC Post-Money SAFE with Cap (standard) or custom template per legal recommendation
- [ ] Fill in all variable fields: investor name, investment amount, valuation cap, discount rate (if any), date
- [ ] Remove any template boilerplate that does not apply (e.g., remove discount provisions if the SAFE has cap only)
- [ ] Legal review completed — confirm outside counsel has reviewed for investor-specific terms or deviations from standard
- [ ] Confirm the company's state of incorporation is Delaware C-Corp — SAFE is not suited for LLCs or S-Corps
- [ ] Confirm the company is authorized to issue the SAFE (check bylaws for any constraints on convertible instruments)

## Execution

- [ ] SAFE agreement sent to investor for signature via DocuSign or equivalent
- [ ] Both parties have signed — verify the execution copy has all signatures before proceeding
- [ ] Wire instructions sent to investor (or received from investor if they are wiring first)
- [ ] Investment amount wired by investor — confirm via bank statement
- [ ] Wire receipt logged with date, amount, and investor name
- [ ] Countersigned executed copy distributed to investor via email and data room

## Post-Execution: Cap Table and Register

- [ ] SAFE recorded in the convertible instrument register immediately upon wire receipt
  - Fields: investor name, investment amount, valuation cap, discount rate, date, MFN status, pro-rata status
- [ ] SAFE register reconciled with bank records — every SAFE entry must have a matching wire
- [ ] SAFE reflected in the fully-diluted cap table as a convertible instrument (not yet converted to shares)
- [ ] Total SAFE stack modelled: if multiple SAFEs outstanding, run combined conversion model to show total dilution at next round
- [ ] Data room updated: add executed SAFE to the SAFE Register document in the cap table section

## MFN Compliance Check

- [ ] Review all prior SAFEs for MFN provisions
- [ ] If any prior SAFE has MFN: compare the new SAFE terms to all prior SAFEs
- [ ] If new SAFE has a lower cap or higher discount than any prior SAFE with MFN: offer the prior SAFE holder the better terms
- [ ] MFN notification sent to eligible holders within 5 business days of new SAFE execution
- [ ] Any MFN elections received and recorded in the SAFE register within 30 days

## Ongoing Maintenance

- [ ] Quarterly: confirm SAFE register is current — no new SAFEs issued without register entry
- [ ] Annually: model conversion scenarios for all outstanding SAFEs using the current company valuation context
- [ ] Before next fundraise: generate a full SAFE conversion schedule showing every SAFE converting at various next-round valuations
- [ ] Before next fundraise: confirm every SAFE holder's signing authority is current (no deceased holders, no dissolved entities)
