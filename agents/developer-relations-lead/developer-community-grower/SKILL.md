# developer-community-grower

## Agent: Developer Relations Lead

L2 developer relations lead responsible for developer community signal extraction, developer GTM, experience review, feedback synthesis, and community growth.

Department ethos: [ideal-marketing.md](../../../departments/marketing/ideal-marketing.md)

## Skill Description

Grows the developer community through events, content, and contribution programmes that convert individual developers into an engaged ecosystem.

## When to Use

- When the developer community has an established core but needs to scale to the next order of magnitude.
- When developer adoption metrics are healthy but community engagement (contributions, forum posts, event attendance) lags behind.
- When launching in a new market or language ecosystem and needing to build local developer community presence.
- When open-source contribution rates are declining and need structured revitalization.

## Workflow

1. **Audit current state**: Measure existing community size, activity, contributor count, event attendance, and content output. Deliverable: community baseline metrics report.
2. **Identify growth channels**: Evaluate which channels have the highest potential (meetups, hackathons, blog programme, ambassador network, open-source contributions, conference talks). Deliverable: channel opportunity matrix.
3. **Design growth programmes**: For top channels, define programme structure including cadence, content requirements, recognition, and success metrics. Deliverable: programme design documents.
4. **Build contributor pipeline**: Create a progression path from consumer to contributor to maintainer with clear milestones and rewards at each level. Deliverable: contributor ladder document.
5. **Execute and measure**: Launch programmes, track participation and output metrics, and iterate based on what produces engaged community members versus one-time participants. Deliverable: monthly community growth dashboard.
6. **Sustain and automate**: Codify successful programmes into repeatable playbooks and automate where possible (welcome bots, contribution tracking, badge systems). Deliverable: operational playbook with automation specs.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Event spam**: Running high-frequency, low-quality events that exhaust both organizers and attendees. *Why*: Developer time is scarce; repeated low-value events train developers to ignore future invitations, permanently reducing reach.
- **Growth without retention**: Focusing on new member acquisition while ignoring activation and retention of existing members. *Why*: A community that churns as fast as it grows wastes acquisition effort and signals instability to prospective members.
- **Ignoring intrinsic motivation**: Offering only swag and prizes without creating genuine learning, networking, or career advancement value. *Why*: Developers are motivated by mastery and belonging; extrinsic-only incentives attract transactional participants who do not sustain engagement.

## Output

**On success**: Produces a developer community growth plan containing baseline metrics, channel strategy, programme designs, contributor ladder, and operational playbook. Delivered to developer relations and marketing leadership.

**On failure**: Report which channels were evaluated, what blocked programme launch, and recommend prerequisite actions. Every error must be actionable.
