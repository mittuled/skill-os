# Scoring Rubric: distribution-viability-check

Evaluates whether a proposed acquisition channel can reach the target customer at a CAC that sustains a healthy LTV:CAC ratio with sufficient volume and activation speed.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | ICP Reach | 25% | Channel's ability to target and reach the ideal customer profile at adequate volume |
| 2 | Unit Economics | 30% | Expected LTV:CAC ratio and payback period under realistic assumptions |
| 3 | Volume Ceiling | 20% | Maximum monthly acquisition volume before audience saturation degrades economics |
| 4 | Channel Activation Speed | 15% | Time required to begin generating qualified traffic and measuring results |
| 5 | Risk and Dependency | 10% | Concentration risk and vulnerability to platform policy changes or competition |
| **Total** | | **100%** | |

## Scale

Each criterion is scored **0-10**:
- **0**: No evidence / completely absent
- **5**: Partially present with significant gaps
- **10**: Fully present, comprehensive, no gaps

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score × weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Exceptional | LTV:CAC ≥ 5:1, volume ceiling meets 12-month targets, ICP match confirmed, <30-day activation | Prioritize; allocate primary budget |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Strong | LTV:CAC 3:1–5:1, volume sufficient, clear ICP match, 30–60-day activation | Fund with 20–40% of acquisition budget |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Good | LTV:CAC 2:1–3:1 or volume ceiling borderline, moderate ICP match | Run small-scale validation test before full commitment |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Adequate | LTV:CAC 1:1–2:1, ICP targeting indirect, activation >90 days | Low priority; test only if other channels saturated |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | Weak | LTV:CAC < 1:1 or volume ceiling below minimum viable scale | Do not fund; revisit if pricing or ICP changes |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Failing | Channel cannot reach ICP or economics are structurally negative | Reject; document reason and revisit in 6+ months |

## Signal Tables

### ICP Reach

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Channel offers first-party audience segment matching ICP by job title, industry, and company size; audience size ≥ 10× monthly target acquisitions; verified by customer survey data confirming this channel is where ICP spends time |
| 7-8 | Channel has ICP-proximate targeting (related industries or titles); audience size 5–10× target; 1–2 trusted customers sourced from this channel historically |
| 5-6 | Channel targeting is demographic rather than professional/behavioural; estimated ICP match 40–60%; no historical customer evidence from channel |
| 3-4 | Channel reaches a broadly adjacent audience; ICP match estimated below 40%; targeting requires significant exclusion lists or negative keywords |
| 0-2 | Channel cannot be targeted to ICP; mass-audience channel with no filtering capability; historical data shows near-zero ICP conversion |

### Unit Economics

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Modelled LTV:CAC ≥ 5:1; payback period ≤ 6 months; CPC/CPM benchmarks within 20% of category median; assumptions validated by ≥ 1 small-scale test or comparable product data |
| 7-8 | Modelled LTV:CAC 3:1–5:1; payback 6–12 months; assumptions based on industry benchmarks with confidence adjustments; no conflicting signals |
| 5-6 | Modelled LTV:CAC 1.5:1–3:1 under optimistic assumptions; payback 12–18 months; assumptions untested; 2+ sensitivity drivers could flip economics negative |
| 3-4 | LTV:CAC < 1.5:1 under central assumptions; payback > 18 months; high CPC/CPM with low historical CTR evidence |
| 0-2 | LTV:CAC < 1:1 under any realistic scenario; channel cost structure makes positive unit economics structurally impossible without major pricing or cost changes |

### Volume Ceiling

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Addressable audience or search volume ≥ 50× monthly acquisition target; competitor spend data confirms channel is not saturated; economics hold at 5× current spend level |
| 7-8 | Addressable audience 20–50× target; some keyword or audience competition but not saturated; economics modelled to hold at 2–3× current spend |
| 5-6 | Addressable audience 5–20× target; moderate competition; economics likely to degrade at 2× spend due to CPM/CPC increases |
| 3-4 | Audience size only 2–5× monthly target; high competition; ceiling likely reached within 3 months of full budget deployment |
| 0-2 | Audience size barely exceeds monthly target; severely saturated niche; scaling budget produces sharply diminishing returns immediately |

### Channel Activation Speed

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Channel can generate first measurable traffic within 1 week; first conversion data available within 2 weeks; full ramp to target volume achievable within 30 days |
| 7-8 | First traffic within 2 weeks; initial conversion data in 30 days; full ramp in 30–60 days; no external approval dependencies |
| 5-6 | Channel requires 30–60 days to generate first meaningful data (e.g., SEO, slow content distribution); or requires external partnership negotiations |
| 3-4 | Channel activation takes 60–90 days; requires significant creative production, partnership agreements, or platform approval processes |
| 0-2 | Channel cannot be activated within 90 days; requires major technical integration, regulatory approval, or platform compliance review |

### Risk and Dependency

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Channel is owned or first-party (email, product-led); zero platform dependency; economics not subject to algorithm or policy changes |
| 7-8 | Single-platform dependency but platform is stable and market-dominant; low recent policy change history; cost variance <20% over prior 12 months |
| 5-6 | Single-platform dependency with moderate change history; CPM/CPC volatility 20–40% in prior 12 months; one policy change in prior 24 months affected economics |
| 3-4 | High platform concentration risk; recent policy changes affected similar advertisers; CPM/CPC volatility > 40%; no mitigation alternatives identified |
| 0-2 | Channel is dominated by one platform with active policy changes targeting this use case; high ban/restriction risk; zero viable alternatives |
