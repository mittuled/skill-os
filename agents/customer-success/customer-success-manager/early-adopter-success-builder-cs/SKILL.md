# early-adopter-success-builder-cs

## Agent: Customer Success Manager

L3 customer success manager (Nx) responsible for signal extraction, feedback synthesis, early adopter success, and customer activation.

Department ethos: [ideal-customer-success.md](../../../../departments/customer-success/ideal-customer-success.md)

## Skill Description

Builds programmes and interventions to ensure early adopters achieve their success milestones and become referenceable advocates.

## When to Use

- When early adopters or beta customers need higher-touch support to succeed with an immature product.
- When early adopter engagement is declining and interventions are needed to prevent churn.
- When the company needs early adopters to become references and their success must be deliberately managed.

## Workflow

1. **Define Early Adopter Success Criteria**: Establish what success looks like for early adopters specifically -- more forgiving than GA customers on polish, more demanding on core value delivery. Deliverable: early adopter success criteria.
2. **Design Intervention Programme**: Create the high-touch intervention plan: weekly check-ins, direct engineering access for blockers, fast-track feedback loops, and executive sponsor engagement. Deliverable: early adopter programme plan.
3. **Execute Interventions**: Run the programme with each early adopter. Track progress against milestones, resolve blockers with urgency, and collect detailed feedback. Deliverable: per-customer progress tracker.
4. **Cultivate Advocacy**: As early adopters achieve success, guide them toward advocacy -- testimonials, case studies, reference calls, and community participation. Deliverable: advocacy pipeline with willing participants.
5. **Feed Back to Product**: Synthesize early adopter experience into product feedback: what worked, what nearly caused failure, and what is needed for GA readiness. Deliverable: early adopter insights report for product team.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Treating early adopters like GA customers**: Applying standard CS processes to early adopters who need more flexibility and responsiveness. *Why*: early adopters tolerate product gaps but not process gaps; they need high-touch attention to stay engaged.
- **Ignoring early adopter feedback**: Collecting feedback without fast-tracking it to product for action. *Why*: early adopters give feedback expecting it will influence the product; if nothing changes, they disengage.
- **Extracting advocacy too early**: Pushing for references before the early adopter has genuinely achieved success. *Why*: premature reference requests feel transactional and damage the trust that early adopter relationships depend on.

## Output

**On success**: Produces an early adopter programme record with per-customer progress, success milestone confirmations, advocacy pipeline, and product insights report. Delivered to the CS Manager and product team.

**On failure**: Report which early adopters did not reach success milestones, what interventions were attempted, and what product changes or additional support are needed.
