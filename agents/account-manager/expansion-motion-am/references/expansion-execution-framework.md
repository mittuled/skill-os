# Account Expansion Execution Framework

Reference for the `expansion-motion-am` skill.

---

## 1. Expansion Motion Types

| Motion Type | Description | Trigger Signals | Typical Deal Size |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Upsell** | Move customer to a higher plan tier | Plan limit reached (seats, usage, features) | 20–100% of current ACV |
| **Cross-sell** | Add complementary product or module | Adjacent use case identified in QBR or support ticket | 25–75% of current ACV |
| **Multi-year renewal** | Convert annual to multi-year contract | High satisfaction + upcoming renewal | Same ACV; longer term |
| **Seat expansion** | Add users to existing plan | Team growth, new department adoption | Proportional to seats added |
| **Usage-based expansion** | Customer naturally grows into higher usage tier | Usage data approaching tier ceiling | Automatic or proactive |

---

## 2. Expansion Readiness Criteria

Before initiating an expansion motion, confirm:

| Criterion | Signal | How to Verify |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| Customer health is green | NPS ≥8 or CSAT ≥4 | Health score dashboard |
| No open critical support issues | Zero P1/P2 open tickets | Support ticketing system |
| Product adoption is high | Key features actively used | Product analytics |
| Business relationship is warm | Last contact within 30 days | CRM activity log |
| Opportunity has been framed | AM Lead has completed opportunity framing | CRM opportunity note |

If any criterion fails: resolve before advancing the expansion conversation.

## 3. Expansion Conversation Framework

### Opening (Value Anchoring)
Start with the customer's business outcome, not the product feature.

```
Bad:  "We have a new Pro tier with more features."
Good: "In our last QBR, you mentioned [business goal]. I've been thinking about how
       we could help you get there faster — can I share what I've seen work for
       customers in your situation?"
```

### Discovery Questions for Expansion

- "What would need to be true for you to get even more value from [product] this year?"
- "Are there other teams in your organisation dealing with [problem the product solves]?"
- "How are you handling [adjacent pain point] today?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about how you use [product], what would it be?"
- "What does success look like for your team over the next 12 months?"

### Proposal Structure

1. **Recap the customer's goal** (show you listened)
2. **Present the business case** (outcome the expansion enables)
3. **Explain what changes** (plan / product / seats)
4. **Show the economics** (cost increase vs. expected value)
5. **Propose a path forward** (trial, phased rollout, immediate)

## 4. Objection Handling Guide

| Objection | Likely Root Cause | Response Approach |
|-----------|------------------|------------------|
| "We don't have budget right now." | Timing / budget cycle | Explore timeline; tie to annual planning; offer multi-year pricing to reduce annual cost |
| "We're not fully using what we have." | Adoption issue | Acknowledge; offer success resources; reframe expansion as solving the adoption blocker |
| "We need to talk to IT / procurement first." | Process step | Welcome it; offer to support the internal business case; schedule a follow-up with stakeholders |
| "We're happy with the current tier." | No urgency | Surface future pain; reference growth projections; offer a no-obligation pilot of upgraded features |
| "We're considering a competitor." | Relationship risk | Understand the driver; do not panic; seek to understand before defending; escalate to AM Lead |
| "Can we get a discount?" | Price sensitivity | Do not discount immediately; explore multi-year, prepay, or timing offers first |

---

## 5. Negotiation Principles

1. **Anchor on value, not cost** — reference the ROI they are already getting before discussing new pricing
2. **Trade, don't give** — every concession (discount, extension) should come with something in return (longer term, reference, expansion commitment)
3. **Know your floor** — understand the minimum acceptable deal before entering negotiation
4. **Create urgency without pressure** — connect to the customer's own timeline and goals, not artificial deadlines
5. **Document everything** — commitments made verbally must be captured in the contract

---

## 6. Post-Close Handoff Requirements

After a successful expansion, brief the implementation or customer success team on:

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| What was sold | `[New plan / module / seats]` |
| What was promised | `[Any specific commitments: timeline, features, support level]` |
| Customer context | `[Stakeholders, sensitivities, goals]` |
| Go-live timeline | `[When the customer expects the expansion to be active]` |
| CRM update | `[Deal value updated, close date set, expansion type recorded]` |
