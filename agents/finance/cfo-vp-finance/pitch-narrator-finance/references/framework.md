# Framework: Financial Pitch Narrative

This framework guides the construction of investor-grade financial narratives for funding rounds.

## The Three-Act Financial Story

Every investor pitch narrative has three acts. Each act maps directly to slides and must flow logically.

| Act | Question Answered | Key Content |
|-----|------------------|-------------|
| 1. Traction Proof | "Where are we today?" | ARR, MRR growth rate, gross margin, key cohort data |
| 2. Growth Trajectory | "Where are we going?" | 3-year projections, growth rate drivers, market expansion |
| 3. Unit Economics Case | "Why do the economics work?" | LTV/CAC, payback period, path to contribution margin positivity |

## Metric Hierarchy for Investor Audiences

Present metrics in this order of priority:

1. **ARR and ARR growth rate** — the fundamental SaaS health signal
2. **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** — the most trusted indicator of product-market fit
3. **Gross margin** — proof of the business model's scalability
4. **Burn multiple** = Net Burn / Net New ARR — efficiency of growth spend
5. **CAC payback period** — capital efficiency signal
6. **Runway** — time to next milestone or profitability

### Benchmark Reference Points (Series A/B SaaS)

| Metric | Strong | Acceptable | Weak |
|--------|--------|------------|------|
| ARR Growth (YoY) | >150% | 100-150% | <100% |
| NRR | >120% | 100-120% | <100% |
| Gross Margin | >75% | 65-75% | <65% |
| Burn Multiple | <1.0x | 1.0-1.5x | >1.5x |
| CAC Payback (months) | <12 | 12-18 | >18 |
| LTV/CAC Ratio | >5x | 3-5x | <3x |

## Use-of-Proceeds Structure

Allocate proceeds to specific operational drivers that support the revenue projection:

| Category | Typical Allocation | Milestone Connection |
|----------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Sales & Marketing | 40-50% | Pipeline coverage, new logo acquisition |
| R&D / Product | 25-35% | Product roadmap milestones, platform build-out |
| G&A / Infrastructure | 10-20% | Scale infrastructure, compliance readiness |
| Reserve / Working Capital | 5-10% | Runway buffer, unforeseen needs |

## Projection Methodology: Bottoms-Up vs. Top-Down

**Bottoms-Up (required as the primary method):**
- Sales capacity model: reps × quota attainment × ACV = new ARR
- Expansion model: existing ARR × net expansion rate
- Churn model: beginning ARR × gross churn rate
- Formula: Ending ARR = Beginning ARR + New ARR + Expansion − Contraction − Churn

**Top-Down (for sanity check only):**
- TAM × market share capture rate
- Use to confirm bottoms-up is within range of market opportunity

## Assumption Defensibility Test

For each projection assumption, apply the "diligence question" test — if an investor asks "how did you get to that number?", the answer must reference:
1. A specific operational input (e.g., number of AEs, quota, ramp time)
2. Historical data that supports the assumption
3. A comparable benchmark or peer reference

Assumptions that fail this test must be revised before the narrative is finalized.

## Common Investor Q&A Scenarios

| Q | A Framework |
|---|-------------|
| "Why will your growth rate sustain?" | Point to pipeline coverage ratio (>3x target ARR), sales hiring plan, and product-led expansion levers |
| "What happens if you miss the plan?" | Reference downside scenario showing runway extends to next milestone under -20% revenue |
| "How defensible is your gross margin?" | Explain fixed vs. variable COGS, infrastructure leverage, and roadmap for automation |
| "What drives NRR above 100%?" | Quantify expansion revenue by cohort: upsell, cross-sell, usage growth by plan tier |
