# Scoring Rubric: unit-econ-viability-gate

This rubric evaluates whether the unit economics are viable enough to justify committing to a business model or pricing structure.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | LTV/CAC Ratio | 30% | Whether the lifetime value of a customer materially exceeds acquisition cost |
| 2 | CAC Payback Period | 25% | How quickly the company recoups customer acquisition investment |
| 3 | Gross Margin Sustainability | 20% | Whether gross margins are high enough to support the growth model |
| 4 | Churn and Retention Durability | 15% | Whether churn assumptions are realistic and retention is defensible |
| 5 | Sensitivity Resilience | 10% | How well the unit economics hold under adverse scenario inputs |
| **Total** | | **100%** | |

## Scale

Each criterion is scored **0-10**:
- **0**: No evidence / completely absent
- **5**: Partially present with significant gaps
- **10**: Fully present, comprehensive, no gaps

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score × weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Exceptional | LTV/CAC >5x, payback <12 months, GM >75%, churn <5%, robust downside resilience | PASS — proceed to market with confidence; no conditions required |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Strong | LTV/CAC 3-5x, payback 12-18 months, GM 70-75%, churn 5-8%, downside still viable | PASS — minor monitoring conditions may apply |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Good | LTV/CAC 2.5-3x, payback 18-24 months, GM 65-70%, manageable downside risk | CONDITIONAL PASS — define improvement timeline and monitoring triggers |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Adequate | LTV/CAC 2-2.5x, payback 24-36 months, GM 55-65%, some downside fragility | CONDITIONAL PASS — specific conditions required; re-gate within 90 days |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | Weak | LTV/CAC <2x, payback >36 months, GM <55%, high churn sensitivity | FAIL — do not proceed; requires fundamental model revision |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Failing | LTV/CAC <1x, payback not achievable within runway, GM <40%, or data too unreliable to assess | FAIL — halt business model commitment; escalate to board |

## Signal Tables

### LTV/CAC Ratio

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Gross-margin-based LTV/CAC >5x across all segments; calculation uses fully-loaded CAC including sales comp, marketing, and overhead allocation; segmented by channel and customer tier |
| 7-8 | LTV/CAC 3-5x for primary segments; fully-loaded CAC used; one segment may be below 3x but represents <20% of revenue |
| 5-6 | LTV/CAC 2-3x at blended level; CAC excludes some overhead; segmented data available but inconsistent quality |
| 3-4 | LTV/CAC 1.5-2x; CAC calculation excludes significant costs (e.g., fully-loaded sales comp); no segment-level breakdown |
| 0-2 | LTV/CAC <1.5x; cost data incomplete; LTV uses revenue (not gross margin) basis; or ratio cannot be calculated due to missing inputs |

### CAC Payback Period

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | CAC payback <12 months across all primary segments; payback calculated on gross-margin basis; compared against runway — payback represents <30% of runway |
| 7-8 | CAC payback 12-18 months; gross-margin basis; runway comfortable (payback is <50% of runway) |
| 5-6 | CAC payback 18-24 months; may mix revenue and GM basis; payback represents 50-70% of runway |
| 3-4 | CAC payback 24-36 months; calculation methodology unclear; payback represents >70% of runway — creates capital efficiency risk |
| 0-2 | CAC payback >36 months; no payback calculation methodology; payback not achievable within current runway; or data insufficient to calculate |

### Gross Margin Sustainability

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Gross margin >75%; positive trajectory over trailing 6 months; COGS breakdown shows infrastructure, support, and licensing as separate lines; path to further expansion documented |
| 7-8 | Gross margin 70-75%; stable trend; COGS breakdown available; minor variability in infrastructure costs |
| 5-6 | Gross margin 60-70%; some volatility; COGS partially broken down; improvement plan exists but lacks specifics |
| 3-4 | Gross margin 45-60%; declining trend or significant volatility; COGS mostly lumped; no clear path to improvement |
| 0-2 | Gross margin <45%; declining trend or missing data; COGS not broken down; model viability at current margins is questionable |

### Churn and Retention Durability

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Gross churn <5% annually; NRR >120%; churn rate validated against 6+ months of cohort data; specific churn drivers identified and mitigated; expansion revenue quantified by mechanism |
| 7-8 | Gross churn 5-8%; NRR 105-120%; 3-6 months of cohort validation; churn drivers documented; some expansion revenue |
| 5-6 | Gross churn 8-12%; NRR 95-105%; cohort data <3 months or incomplete; churn drivers partially identified |
| 3-4 | Gross churn 12-20%; NRR <95%; limited cohort history; assumed churn rate not grounded in data; no expansion revenue |
| 0-2 | Gross churn >20%; no NRR calculation; churn assumptions are theoretical with no customer data basis; or data unavailable |

### Sensitivity Resilience

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Model remains PASS under all three stress scenarios: churn +3pp, CAC +20%, GM -5pp; break-even thresholds clearly documented; monitoring triggers defined |
| 7-8 | Model passes two of three stress scenarios; one scenario moves to CONDITIONAL but not FAIL; monitoring triggers documented |
| 5-6 | Model passes one stress scenario; two scenarios push to CONDITIONAL; downside path to recovery described |
| 3-4 | Model fails two or more stress scenarios; viability depends on best-case assumptions holding; no monitoring framework |
| 0-2 | Model fails all stress scenarios or stress testing was not performed; base case economics only presented; no contingency planning |
