# Checklist: board-meeting-preparer

Covers every step required to prepare for a quarterly or special board meeting from initial timeline setup through CEO pre-meeting briefing.

## Timeline and Agenda Setup (3+ Weeks Before)

- [ ] Confirm the board meeting date, time, format (in-person / virtual / hybrid), and expected duration
- [ ] Confirm quorum: verify all directors can attend; flag conflicts immediately to CEO — rescheduling requires more notice than a week
- [ ] Draft the agenda with the CEO: standard sections (consent items, financials, operations, strategic discussion) plus any special topics
- [ ] For each agenda item, identify: the presenter, the expected time allocation, and whether it is decision-required or discussion-only
- [ ] Identify all materials needed per agenda item and assign an owner for each
- [ ] Set internal submission deadline for all department materials: 10 business days before the meeting

## Material Collection (2 Weeks Before)

- [ ] Confirm submissions are on track — send reminder to all owners 3 business days before the internal deadline
- [ ] CFO financial package: P&L, balance sheet, cash flow statement, budget vs. actuals, KPI dashboard
  - Verify the financial close is complete before requesting the package — incomplete books produce unreliable materials
- [ ] CPO product update: milestone progress, roadmap status, key product metrics
- [ ] CTO engineering update: system reliability, security, key technical milestones
- [ ] CBO GTM update: pipeline, bookings, revenue, customer wins and churn
- [ ] CEO strategic section: key decisions, M&A, hiring, capital plans
- [ ] Legal: draft consent resolutions for all routine items requiring board approval
- [ ] Chase any overdue submissions — escalate to CEO if a department is unresponsive within 24 hours of missing the deadline

## Deck Assembly (10 Days Before)

- [ ] Assemble the board deck in the following order: consent items, KPI dashboard, financial review, department updates, strategic discussion
- [ ] Numerical consistency check: verify every metric appears identically across all sections (ARR in CEO section must match ARR in CFO section)
- [ ] Narrative coherence check: does the deck tell a consistent story? Do any sections contradict each other?
- [ ] Depth calibration: board deck is strategic, not operational — no slide should require 10 minutes of explanation
- [ ] "So what" check: every data slide must end with an insight, decision, or recommendation — not just a chart
- [ ] Slide count: typical quarterly board deck is 20-35 slides; more than 40 slides requires a scope reduction
- [ ] Attach appendix: financial statements, detailed cap table, supporting metrics, any consent resolution background memos

## CEO Review (7 Days Before)

- [ ] Send consolidated draft to CEO for narrative review
- [ ] Incorporate CEO edits
- [ ] Final check: all consent resolutions included, all financial statements attached, all board asks clearly stated

## Distribution (5 Days Before)

- [ ] Final materials approved by CEO
- [ ] Distribute via secure channel: board portal preferred; encrypted email if portal is unavailable
  - Never distribute board materials via unencrypted email or public file sharing links
- [ ] Confirm delivery: all directors have accessed the portal or acknowledged receipt
- [ ] Send a cover note with the materials listing: key discussion topics, decisions required, and page/slide references

## Logistics and CEO Briefing (48 Hours Before)

- [ ] Confirm all logistics: video link tested, room booked, dial-in confirmed, materials printed if in-person
- [ ] Prepare CEO briefing: anticipated questions from each director, contentious topics, recommended responses, who the swing vote is on any contested motion
- [ ] Flag any director who has pre-meeting questions — route questions to the appropriate department owner
- [ ] Prepare consent resolutions for in-meeting execution: all resolutions signed and dated correctly before the meeting starts

## During the Meeting

- [ ] Record attendance — confirm quorum before starting any formal business
- [ ] Record all decisions made (motion, mover, seconder, vote count)
- [ ] Record all action items (owner, deliverable, due date)
- [ ] Note any dissenting votes on resolutions
- [ ] Note any material information shared verbally that is not in the pre-read materials (this goes into the minutes)

## Post-Meeting (Within 48 Hours)

- [ ] Draft minutes circulated to CEO and legal for review
- [ ] Minutes distributed to all directors for review and approval
- [ ] Action item tracker updated and sent to all action item owners with due dates
- [ ] Any follow-up materials requested during the meeting compiled and sent within 5 business days
