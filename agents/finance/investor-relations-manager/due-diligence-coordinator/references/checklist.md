# Checklist: due-diligence-coordinator

Covers every step required to manage a diligence process from initial request receipt to archive and retrospective.

## Request Intake (Day 1)

- [ ] Diligence request list received from investor — confirm format (email, spreadsheet, standard template)
- [ ] Normalize the format: import all items into a tracking spreadsheet with columns: ID, request, category, owner, due date, status, response location
- [ ] Categorize every item by department: Finance, Legal, Product, Engineering, HR, Sales/Marketing, Operations
- [ ] Assign priority: P1 (investor indicated urgency or item is typically decision-critical), P2 (standard diligence), P3 (supplemental or nice-to-have)
- [ ] Brief the CEO on the request list: who the investor is, what stage, and any sensitive items to handle carefully
- [ ] Confirm the overall diligence timeline with the investor — typical expectation: 3-4 weeks for Series A, 4-6 weeks for Series B+

## Owner Assignment (Day 1–2)

- [ ] Assign a specific person to every item — no item without an owner
- [ ] Set due dates: P1 items due within 5 business days, P2 within 10 business days, P3 within 15 business days
- [ ] Brief each owner on: who the investor is, why this item matters to them, the sensitivity level, and the redaction requirements
- [ ] Confirm each owner accepts the assignment and due date — escalate conflicts to the CEO
- [ ] Set up recurring daily check-in: 15-minute Slack standup or async status update

## Response Coordination (Ongoing)

- [ ] Daily status review: which items are on track, which are at risk, which are overdue?
- [ ] Chase overdue items within 24 hours of missing deadline — escalate to department head if not resolved within 48 hours
- [ ] Review every response before sending: completeness (does it answer the question?), accuracy (does it match cap table, GL, and legal records?), redaction (is any information overshared per company policy?)
- [ ] Organize responses in the data room — never email documents directly; all responses go through the controlled data room
- [ ] Send the investor a batch update when a significant portion of requests are complete (do not send item by item)

## Red Flag Triage (Ongoing)

- [ ] Review the full request list for items that could surface sensitive information: pending litigation, employment disputes, IP ownership questions, customer concentration, related-party transactions
- [ ] For each potential red flag: brief the CEO and legal before preparing the response
- [ ] Prepare the narrative for each red flag: acknowledge the issue, provide context, and explain the mitigation or resolution
- [ ] Never discover a red flag at the same time as the investor — proactive disclosure with context is the standard

## Q&A Management (Ongoing)

- [ ] Route all follow-up questions from the investor to the original item owner
- [ ] Provide the owner with the context from the original response and the investor's specific follow-up
- [ ] Track every Q&A exchange in the tracker — the investor's question, the owner, the response, and the date sent
- [ ] Escalate any follow-up question that implies the investor is unsatisfied with the original response — this is a signal to address more directly

## Process Close-Out

- [ ] Investor confirms diligence is complete (or passes) — get written confirmation
- [ ] All materials archived in the data room under a dated diligence folder
- [ ] Retrospective conducted within 2 weeks: what was difficult to produce? What questions recurred? What gaps exist in standard documentation?
- [ ] Retrospective findings added to the standard diligence readiness checklist for future rounds
- [ ] Data room access revoked for any investor who passed or whose process has concluded
- [ ] If the round closed: final diligence package archived as the closing data room version
