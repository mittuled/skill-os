# Framework: monthly-investor-update

Defines the structure, content standards, and communication rules for monthly investor updates.

## Update Structure

The monthly update follows a fixed 6-section order. Never reorder sections — investors read these updates quickly and consistency aids scanning.

| Section | Purpose | Word Limit |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Subject Line | Signal update type and timeframe | 10 words max |
| Highlights | 3 most important wins or milestones | 3 bullets, 20 words each |
| Key Metrics | Verified SaaS metrics table | Table, no prose |
| Lowlights | Honest problems and setbacks | 1-2 items, 30 words each |
| Asks | Specific requests to investors | 3 items max |
| Outlook | What to expect next month | 50 words max |

Total word limit: 600 words. If you need more, you are writing a memo, not an update.

## Required Metrics Table

Include all 8 metrics every month. If a metric is not yet tracked, mark it "Not yet tracked" — never omit it or leave the field blank.

| Metric | Definition | Frequency |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| ARR | Annualized recurring revenue (MRR × 12) | Monthly |
| MRR Growth (MoM) | (Current MRR − Prior MRR) / Prior MRR | Monthly |
| Net Revenue Retention | (Starting ARR + expansion − contraction − churn) / Starting ARR × 100 | Monthly |
| Gross Burn | Total cash out (GAAP cash basis) | Monthly |
| Net Burn | Gross burn − cash collected from customers | Monthly |
| Runway | Cash / monthly net burn | Monthly |
| Gross Margin | (Revenue − direct COGS) / Revenue | Monthly |
| Headcount | Full-time employees only (list part-time separately if material) | Monthly |

## Highlights Section Rules

- Highlights are wins, milestones, and positive developments — not plans
- Each highlight must name a specific number, customer, or milestone: "closed 3 enterprise contracts ($180K ARR)" not "strong sales month"
- Do not list more than 3 highlights — if everything is a highlight, nothing is
- At least one highlight per quarter must relate to product or technology (not just revenue)

## Lowlights Section Rules

- Always include at least one lowlight — zero lowlights is a signal the update is not honest
- Each lowlight must include: what happened, why it happened, and what you are doing about it
- Lowlights are not failures — they are transparency signals that build investor confidence
- Do not bury lowlights at the end of a long paragraph — the section must be explicitly labeled

Acceptable lowlight format: "[What happened]. Root cause: [why]. Response: [what you are doing]."

## Asks Section Rules

- Maximum 3 asks per update — more than 3 exhausts goodwill
- Every ask must be specific: "introduction to VP Sales at [Company X]" not "sales introductions"
- Include the context that makes the ask actionable: "We are targeting healthcare enterprise and need warm intros to VP-level buyers at health systems with 500+ beds"
- Rotating ask types: hiring referrals, customer introductions, advice/expertise, follow-on capital signal

| Ask Type | Format |
|---------|--------|
| Hiring | "[Role]: We are hiring a [title]. Looking for referrals with [specific background]." |
| Intro | "[Company]: Introduction to [name or title] at [company] — [one sentence context]." |
| Advice | "[Topic]: Would value 30 min with anyone who has experience [specific situation]." |

## Tone and Voice Standards

- Write in the voice of the CEO — first person, direct, confident but not promotional
- Use numbers, not adjectives: "ARR grew 12% MoM" not "strong growth"
- No jargon without explanation — assume investors scan 20 updates a month
- No forward-looking hype: "We believe we are on track to..." → "We expect to hit $X ARR by [date]"

**Prohibited phrases:**
- "We are excited to announce..."
- "We are crushing it"
- "Incredible momentum"
- "Transformative quarter"
- "We are well-positioned"

## Cadence and Timing

- Send on the same calendar day each month (e.g., first Monday of the month)
- Send within 10 calendar days of month-end — later than 15 days signals operational dysfunction
- Material events (fundraise announcement, CEO departure, large customer loss) warrant an out-of-cycle update within 24 hours of the event becoming final
- Never miss a month without communication — a brief "short update this month due to [reason]" is better than silence
