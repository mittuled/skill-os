# Framework: Thought Leadership Programme Runner

Defines the PESO model, authority-building methodology, content placement strategy, and speaker programme for establishing executives as category authorities.

## PESO Model Application

PESO (Paid, Earned, Shared, Owned) provides the channel framework for amplifying thought leadership content across all media types.

| Channel | Type | Thought Leadership Applications | Investment Level |
|---------|------|--------------------------------|-----------------|
| **Paid** | Sponsored content, promoted posts, native advertising | Amplify bylines via LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads; promote podcast appearances; boost speaking event announcements | Medium — use to extend reach of high-quality earned placements |
| **Earned** | Press coverage, journalist-written features, awards | Byline placements in tier-1 publications; podcast guest appearances; journalist profiles of executives; industry award nominations | High — the primary credibility signal; cannot be bought directly |
| **Shared** | Social media, employee advocacy | Executive LinkedIn posts linking to placements; reposted bylines; event live-coverage; quote cards | Low cost — owned by PR and executive social strategy |
| **Owned** | Company blog, newsletter, website | Long-form articles that serve as byline source material; executive newsletter; podcast transcript repurposing | Low cost — feeds content into earned and paid channels |

### PESO Sequencing

1. Create owned content (company blog, executive newsletter) as the source material
2. Pitch derived bylines to earned publications
3. Amplify earned placements through shared channels (executive and company LinkedIn)
4. Consider paid amplification for highest-performing earned placements to extend reach to target audience segments

## Authority-Building Framework

Executive authority is built along three dimensions: **expertise depth**, **audience relevance**, and **content consistency**.

### Dimension 1: Expertise Depth

| Level | Indicators | Placement Target |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| Emerging | 1–3 published bylines; known within company only | Trade publications; company blog; podcast guest for smaller shows |
| Recognised | 5–10 bylines; quoted in Tier 2 press; speaks at regional events | Tier-2 publications; industry association events; mid-size podcasts |
| Established | 10+ bylines; regularly quoted in Tier 1 press; keynote at industry events | Tier-1 publications (Harvard Business Review, WSJ, FT, TechCrunch); flagship conference keynotes |
| Category authority | Defines the narrative others respond to; first-call expert for journalists | Book deal, Forbes/Inc columnist, analyst reference, conference chair |

### Dimension 2: Audience Relevance

Map each executive's expertise to a specific ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) segment. Thought leadership that does not reach the target buyer is a vanity exercise.

| Executive | Expertise Theme | Target Audience | Target Publication |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| CEO | Company strategy, market vision | Board, investors, enterprise buyers | Fortune, FT, Bloomberg |
| CPO | Product innovation, UX | Product practitioners, enterprise IT | Product Hunt, Pragmatic Institute, Fast Company |
| CTO | Engineering, AI, infrastructure | Technical buyers, engineering leaders | InfoQ, The New Stack, Hacker News |
| Domain Expert VP | Vertical market insight | Sector-specific buyers | Trade publications for the vertical |

### Dimension 3: Content Consistency

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Bylines per executive per quarter | 1–2 placed | Below this and momentum does not build; above this and quality degrades |
| Speaking appearances per executive per quarter | 1–2 confirmed | Conference pipelines require 3–6 months lead time; build pipeline to hit this |
| Podcast appearances per executive per quarter | 1 | Evergreen content with long shelf life; lower effort than live events |
| LinkedIn posts per executive per week | 2–3 | Social amplification of placements; builds direct audience |

## Byline Placement Strategy

### Publication Tiers

| Tier | Publication Examples | Acceptance Criteria | Typical Lead Time |
|------|---------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Tier A | Harvard Business Review, WSJ, FT, MIT Tech Review, Fast Company | Original research or contrarian insight; 1,500–2,500 words; 3–6 month editorial lead | 3–6 months |
| Tier B | Forbes, Inc, TechCrunch, Wired, VentureBeat | Timely insight; 800–1,200 words; topic pitch accepted before writing | 4–8 weeks |
| Tier C | Vertical trade publications, industry association journals | Expertise-forward; often accept contributed content directly | 2–4 weeks |
| Tier D | Company blog, LinkedIn Article, Medium | No editorial gatekeeping; used as source material for pitching higher tiers | Immediate |

### Pitch Protocol for Byline Placements

1. Draft a 200-word concept pitch — topic, argument, target audience, and why this author is credible
2. For Tier A/B, pitch the editor before writing the full piece
3. For Tier C/D, write the full piece first, then submit
4. Confirm exclusivity policy before submitting — most Tier A/B publications require exclusivity (do not submit simultaneously)
5. If rejected, revise and pitch the next tier down; do not discard — strong thinking finds a home

## Speaker Programme Framework

### Conference Tier Model

| Tier | Examples | Selection Criteria | Value |
|------|---------|-------------------|-------|
| Flagship | Davos, TED, Fortune CEO, CES, Dreamforce keynote | Industry-defining audiences; 10,000+ attendees; competitive selection | Highest authority signal; multi-year brand halo |
| Category leader | Gartner Summit, Forrester, SaaStr, re:Invent | Directly addresses buyer and analyst audience | High — influences category perception |
| Vertical event | Industry-specific conferences (Money 20/20, HLTH, etc.) | Reaches specific ICP segments | Medium — targeted audience reach |
| Emerging / regional | Local tech events, startup summits, university talks | Lower selection bar; good for building portfolio | Low authority signal; useful for new executives |

### Speaker Application Pipeline Management

| Stage | Actions | Lead Time |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Identify | Source open CFPs (Call for Papers) 6–12 months out | 9–12 months before event |
| Apply | Submit speaker application with tailored abstract and executive bio | 6–9 months before event |
| Confirm | Speaker slot confirmed; negotiate speaking terms and promotional rights | 3–6 months before event |
| Prepare | Develop presentation deck; rehearse; brief executive on audience profile | 4–8 weeks before event |
| Amplify | Pre-event social posts; live session promotion; post-event content repurposing | Before and after event |

## Programme Health Metrics

| Metric | Measurement | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Placement rate | Pitches submitted vs. placements secured | 30%+ acceptance rate for Tier B/C |
| SOV from thought leadership | Attributed mentions in press from placements | Track via earned-media-monitor |
| Social engagement on placements | Avg LinkedIn engagement rate on shared bylines | 3–5% (likes + comments / impressions) |
| Pipeline attribution | Deals where thought leadership influenced contact or deal stage | Track via CRM touchpoint data |
| Speaker pipeline fill rate | Confirmed speaking slots vs. quarterly target | 80%+ of quarterly targets met |
