# Framework: Media Relationship Builder

Defines the journalist tiering model, relationship cadence system, beat mapping methodology, and embargo/exclusivity protocols for building durable press relationships.

## Journalist Beat Mapping

Beat mapping matches journalists to the company's news categories before outreach begins.

### Beat Categories

| Beat | Coverage Focus | Typical Outlets | Relevance Triggers |
|------|--------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Enterprise tech | B2B software, SaaS, cloud infrastructure | TechCrunch, The Information, ZDNet, CRN | Product launches, funding, integrations |
| AI / ML | Artificial intelligence, automation, data | Wired, MIT Tech Review, VentureBeat | AI feature releases, research, use cases |
| Vertical industry | Sector-specific news (fintech, healthtech, etc.) | Industry-specific trade publications | Customer wins, vertical market entry |
| Business press | Company strategy, financials, leadership | WSJ, FT, Bloomberg, Fortune | Funding rounds, M&A, executive hires, IPO |
| Local / regional | Company's HQ city and regional expansion markets | Regional business journals | Office openings, hiring, community news |
| Startup / VC | Funding, founder stories, startup ecosystem | Axios Pro Rata, Crunchbase News | All funding events |

### Beat Research Protocol

For each journalist on the target list:
1. Review last 20 published articles — note recurring topics, source types used, and writing style
2. Check LinkedIn, Twitter/X for stated beats and topics they engage with
3. Note publication format preferences (data-driven vs. narrative vs. analysis)
4. Record preferred contact method if stated (email vs. DM vs. tip line)
5. Identify mutual connections or prior company mentions (warm or cold start signal)

## Journalist Tiering Model

| Tier | Criteria | Relationship Investment | Outreach Frequency |
|------|---------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Tier 1 — Strategic | Covers the company's exact category at a top-10 outlet; byline reaches 100K+ readers | High: personal meetings, exclusive briefings, off-the-record access | Monthly non-transactional + every announcement |
| Tier 2 — Core | Covers the category or adjacent space; byline reaches 20K–100K readers | Medium: quarterly check-ins, PR exclusives for major news | Every major announcement + quarterly check-in |
| Tier 3 — Opportunistic | Covers the category occasionally; byline reaches <20K; trade or regional press | Low: pitch-only outreach for directly relevant news | News-driven only |
| Tier 4 — Monitor | Covers competitors but not the company yet; potential future relationship | Minimal: monitor coverage, no outreach until beat relevance confirmed | No proactive outreach; track for beat shifts |

## Relationship Cadence System

Relationships are maintained through a mix of transactional (announcement-driven) and non-transactional (value-first) touchpoints.

### Non-Transactional Touchpoints

| Type | Frequency | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Data share | Monthly | Share proprietary data or industry survey results before public release |
| Commentary offer | As news breaks | Offer on-the-record executive comment on a breaking industry story within 2 hours |
| Congratulatory note | After publication | Brief message acknowledging a well-researched piece (no ask attached) |
| Source introduction | Quarterly | Connect the journalist with a relevant external expert or customer willing to speak |
| Background briefing | Bi-annually | Off-the-record session on company strategy and market view — no pitch |

### Transactional Touchpoints

| Type | Timing | Best Practice |
|------|--------|--------------|
| Announcement pitch | 48–72 hours before embargo lift | Personalised; include exclusive angle or data for Tier 1 contacts |
| Post-release follow-up | 24 hours after distribution | Only if no response to initial pitch; provide additional angle |
| Interview request | As part of major announcement | Propose specific spokesperson and angle; make scheduling frictionless |

## CRM Best Practices

Track every journalist interaction in a relationship CRM or shared tracking sheet.

### Record Fields per Journalist

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Name | Full name + preferred address form |
| Outlet + URL | Publication and beat page |
| Beat(s) | Primary + secondary coverage areas |
| Tier | 1/2/3/4 |
| Last contact date | YYYY-MM-DD |
| Last touchpoint type | Transactional / Non-transactional |
| Contact method | Email / Twitter / LinkedIn / Phone |
| Preferred pitch format | Data-led / Narrative / Embargo / Exclusive |
| Coverage history | Links to articles mentioning the company |
| Notes | Sensitivities, stated interests, out-of-office patterns |

### Health Indicators

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| No response to 3 consecutive pitches | Pause outreach; revisit beat alignment; try different channel |
| Response rate drops below 20% | Audit pitch format and topic relevance for that journalist |
| Journalist changes beat or outlet | Update tier and cadence; re-research new beat |
| Last contact >90 days | Re-engage with non-transactional touchpoint before next pitch |

## Embargo and Exclusivity Protocols

### Embargo Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Set embargo at least 48 hours before desired publication date | Allows journalists time to research without last-minute pressure |
| Confirm embargo acceptance explicitly | Verbal or written agreement before sharing embargoed materials |
| Limit embargoed distribution to Tier 1 and 2 contacts | Wide embargo distribution increases break risk |
| Include lift time in all embargoed materials | Format: "EMBARGO: [Date] [Time] [Timezone]" |
| Embargo break protocol | If an outlet breaks embargo, notify all embargoed contacts immediately and assess whether to lift for all |

### Exclusivity Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Reserve exclusives for Tier 1 contacts only | Exclusives are a relationship investment, not a default |
| Define exclusivity window explicitly | Example: "48-hour exclusive window before we offer to other outlets" |
| Do not offer the same exclusive to multiple outlets simultaneously | One outlet per exclusive offer; if they pass, offer to the next |
| Track exclusive offers in CRM | Record which outlet received each exclusive and the outcome |
| Exclusivity does not mean indefinite | If the outlet does not publish within the window, the story is released widely |
