# Framework: Follow-Up Sequence Design

This framework defines the five follow-up scenario types, cadence matrices, tone guides, value-add progression strategies, exit criteria, and channel mix used by the follow-up-sequence-builder skill.

## Scenario Types

### 1. Post-Demo Follow-Up

**Trigger**: Prospect attended a live demo or received a recorded walkthrough and has not responded to next-step requests.

**Cadence matrix**:

| Touch | Day | Channel | Purpose |
|-------|-----|---------|---------|
| 1 | 0 (same day) | Email | Thank you + recap key discussion points + next step CTA |
| 2 | 1 | LinkedIn | Brief personal note referencing a specific moment from the demo |
| 3 | 3 | Email | Share a relevant case study that mirrors their use case |
| 4 | 7 | Email | Address the #1 objection raised during the demo (or preempt the most common one) |
| 5 | 14 | Email | Final value offer — ROI calculator, custom analysis, or peer introduction |

**Tone**: Warm, confident, consultative. The prospect has invested time in a demo — they have interest. Your job is to make the next step easy, not to re-sell.

**Value-add progression**:
1. **Touch 1**: Recap value — remind them what they saw and why it matters to their specific situation. Include any custom answers to questions they asked during the demo.
2. **Touch 2**: Social proximity — a personal, informal touchpoint that reinforces the human connection.
3. **Touch 3**: Peer proof — a case study from a company similar in size, industry, or use case. Not a generic customer story.
4. **Touch 4**: Objection handling — proactively address the concern that is likely preventing them from moving forward.
5. **Touch 5**: Decision support — provide a tool or resource that helps them build the internal case (ROI calculator, executive summary they can forward, or offer to present to their team).

**Exit criteria**: No response after Touch 5 (Day 14). Move to re-engagement track with 30-day pause.

### 2. Post-Content Follow-Up

**Trigger**: Prospect downloaded a white paper, ebook, or report, attended a webinar, or engaged with gated content.

**Cadence matrix**:

| Touch | Day | Channel | Purpose |
|-------|-----|---------|---------|
| 1 | 0 (immediate) | Email | Confirm content delivery + add a related insight not in the content |
| 2 | 3 | Email | Ask a question about the specific topic they engaged with |
| 3 | 7 | Email | Bridge from content topic to product relevance with a brief case study |

**Tone**: Educational, low-pressure, curiosity-driven. Content engagement signals interest in the topic, not necessarily in your product. Earn the right to talk about your solution by demonstrating expertise first.

**Value-add progression**:
1. **Touch 1**: Extend the content — share an insight, data point, or perspective that adds to what they just consumed. Not "did you enjoy the white paper?" but "one thing we did not cover in the report is..."
2. **Touch 2**: Engage their thinking — ask a question that connects the content topic to their specific situation. This invites a reply without asking for a meeting.
3. **Touch 3**: Bridge to relevance — connect the dots between the content topic and how your product addresses it. Use a brief proof point. Ask if the connection resonates.

**Exit criteria**: No response after Touch 3 (Day 7). Add to nurture track. Do not force more touches — content engagement is low-intent and over-following-up creates negative associations with your brand.

### 3. Post-Meeting Follow-Up

**Trigger**: Prospect had an initial discovery call, intro call, or qualification meeting but no next step was booked.

**Cadence matrix**:

| Touch | Day | Channel | Purpose |
|-------|-----|---------|---------|
| 1 | 0 (same day) | Email | Meeting summary + proposed next step with specific date/time |
| 2 | 2 | Email | Share a relevant resource connected to a pain point discussed |
| 3 | 5 | Phone/LinkedIn | Direct outreach — acknowledge silence, restate value, offer flexibility |
| 4 | 10 | Email | New angle — share a trigger event, industry insight, or peer story |

**Tone**: Direct, action-oriented, respectful. They gave you their time — they have some level of interest. The goal is to remove friction from the next step, not to re-pitch.

**Value-add progression**:
1. **Touch 1**: Document the conversation — send a concise summary of what was discussed, the pain points they described, and a specific proposed next step. Include exact dates/times. Not "let me know when you are free" but "does Thursday at 2pm work?"
2. **Touch 2**: Deliver on something from the meeting — if they mentioned a challenge, send a resource that addresses it. If they asked a question you could not answer, provide the answer.
3. **Touch 3**: Change the channel — a phone call or LinkedIn message after two emails signals genuine interest and effort. Acknowledge you have sent a couple of emails and offer a brief conversation.
4. **Touch 4**: Bring new information — a trigger event at their company, an industry trend, or a peer case study that is newly relevant. Not a rehash of the meeting.

**Exit criteria**: No response after Touch 4 (Day 10). Escalate to AE for account-level strategy review. If AE confirms the deal is still viable, move to re-engagement track with 21-day pause.

### 4. Re-Engagement Follow-Up

**Trigger**: Prospect was actively engaged (multiple emails opened, meetings held, demo attended) but has gone silent for 14+ days without explanation.

**Cadence matrix**:

| Touch | Day | Channel | Purpose |
|-------|-----|---------|---------|
| 1 | 0 | Email | Acknowledge the silence directly + offer a valuable resource |
| 2 | 7 | LinkedIn | Light-touch engagement — comment on or share their content |
| 3 | 21 | Email | Share a significant new development (product update, case study, industry news) |
| 4 | 45 | Email | Breakup with a door-open offer |

**Tone**: Curious, zero-pressure, human. Something changed on their end — priorities shifted, a blocker emerged, or they chose a competitor. Your job is to understand, not to pressure.

**Value-add progression**:
1. **Touch 1**: Direct acknowledgment — "I noticed we have not connected since [last interaction]. Priorities shift and I completely understand. In case it is useful, here is [resource]." No guilt. No "I have tried reaching you multiple times."
2. **Touch 2**: Ambient presence — engage with their LinkedIn content organically. Like a post, leave a thoughtful comment. This keeps you visible without sending another email.
3. **Touch 3**: Material news — only reach out again if you have something genuinely new: a product feature they asked about, a case study in their exact space, or a significant industry development. This touch must pass the test: "Would I want to receive this?"
4. **Touch 4**: Clean breakup — acknowledge that now may not be the right time. Offer to reconnect in a future quarter. Provide a final resource. Leave the relationship intact for later.

**Exit criteria**: No response after Touch 4 (Day 45). Archive from active sequence. Add to quarterly re-engagement list for one final touch in 90 days. If no response at 90 days, remove from outbound entirely.

### 5. Event Follow-Up

**Trigger**: Met the prospect at a conference, trade show, meetup, or networking event (in-person or virtual).

**Cadence matrix**:

| Touch | Day | Channel | Purpose |
|-------|-----|---------|---------|
| 1 | 0 (same day or next morning) | Email | Personal note referencing specific conversation + offer to continue |
| 2 | 1 | LinkedIn | Connect with a note referencing the event |
| 3 | 3 | Email | Share a resource connected to what you discussed at the event |
| 4 | 7 | Email | Bridge to a meeting with a specific agenda tied to their interests |

**Tone**: Warm, personal, relationship-first. Event connections are warmer than cold outreach — leverage the shared experience. Reference specific details from the conversation to differentiate from the 50 other vendors who scanned their badge.

**Value-add progression**:
1. **Touch 1**: Personal recall — reference a specific moment: "Great talking about [specific topic] at [event]. Your point about [their insight] stuck with me." Not "Nice meeting you at the conference." Include one specific follow-up from the conversation.
2. **Touch 2**: Social connection — connect on LinkedIn with a note that references the event and conversation. Establishes the ongoing relationship.
3. **Touch 3**: Deliver value — share a resource directly connected to the conversation topic. An article, report, or tool that addresses something they mentioned.
4. **Touch 4**: Propose a focused conversation — not a "demo" or "sales call" but a continuation of the event conversation with a specific agenda. "We talked about [topic] — I have some data on how [peer companies] are approaching this. Worth 20 minutes?"

**Exit criteria**: No response after Touch 4 (Day 7). Add to nurture track. Event connections have a longer shelf life — include in next event invite list and quarterly newsletter.

## Channel Mix by Scenario

| Scenario | Email | LinkedIn | Phone | Total Touches |
|----------|-------|----------|-------|---------------|
| Post-Demo | 4 | 1 | 0 | 5 |
| Post-Content | 3 | 0 | 0 | 3 |
| Post-Meeting | 3 | 0-1 | 1 | 4-5 |
| Re-Engagement | 3 | 1 | 0 | 4 |
| Event Follow-Up | 3 | 1 | 0 | 4 |

**Channel selection principles**:
- **Email**: Primary channel for all scenarios. Allows detailed messaging and is easily trackable.
- **LinkedIn**: Use for social proximity and ambient engagement. Never copy-paste email content into LinkedIn messages.
- **Phone**: Reserved for post-meeting and high-value deal scenarios only. Phone calls for post-content or event follow-up feel disproportionate and aggressive.

## Exit Criteria Decision Tree

```
Has the prospect responded to any touch?
├── Yes → Reclassify based on response. If positive, advance deal stage.
│         If objection, address and continue. If "not now", schedule re-engagement.
└── No → Has the sequence completed all touches?
    ├── Yes → What was the scenario?
    │   ├── Post-Demo → Move to re-engagement track (30-day pause)
    │   ├── Post-Content → Move to nurture track (newsletter, future content)
    │   ├── Post-Meeting → Escalate to AE for strategy review
    │   ├── Re-Engagement → Archive from active outreach (90-day quarterly check)
    │   └── Event Follow-Up → Move to nurture track + future event invite list
    └── No → Continue sequence. Check for bounce/unsubscribe signals.
        If email bounced → Verify address, try LinkedIn only.
        If unsubscribed → Remove immediately. Do not contact again.
```

## Seniority Adjustments

| Prospect Seniority | Cadence Multiplier | Channel Preference | Tone Adjustment |
|--------------------|-------------------|--------------------|-----------------|
| IC / Manager | 1x (standard) | Email-heavy | Direct, practical |
| Director | 1.25x | Email + LinkedIn | Professional, outcome-focused |
| VP | 1.5x | Email + LinkedIn | Executive, ROI-focused |
| C-Suite | 2x | Email + Executive alignment | Strategic, peer-to-peer |

**Principle**: Higher seniority = wider spacing between touches, more formal tone, and preference for executive-to-executive alignment on later touches. Never call a C-suite prospect without an existing relationship.
