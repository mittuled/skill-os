## 8-Dimension Analysis

### 1. Company Size

[Headcount, departmental breakdown, office locations, and growth trend.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "220 employees (LinkedIn, verified Feb 2025). Engineering: ~55 (25%). Sales: ~30 (14%). Added 40 employees in last 6 months (+22%). HQ: San Francisco. Satellite: London (opened Q3 2024)."
- Bad: "Medium-sized company with multiple offices."
- Format: Quantitative data with source attribution and date]

### 2. Industry

[Vertical, sub-vertical, and classification codes.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Primary: Financial Technology. Sub-vertical: B2B Payment Infrastructure. NAICS: 522320 (Financial Transactions Processing). Adjacent verticals served: e-commerce platforms, SaaS marketplaces."
- Bad: "Fintech company."
- Format: Structured classification with adjacent market notes]

### 3. Funding

[Total raised, last round, investors, and runway signals.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Total raised: $47M. Last round: Series B, $30M (Jan 2025), led by Andreessen Horowitz with participation from Index Ventures. Hiring velocity suggests 18-24 month runway at current burn rate."
- Bad: "Well-funded startup."
- Format: Specific figures with source and date, plus runway inference]

### 4. Tech Stack

[Engineering stack, infrastructure, and business tools.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Backend: Python/Django (job postings). Infrastructure: AWS (LinkedIn engineering posts). Frontend: React/TypeScript (GitHub repos). CRM: Salesforce (G2 review mention). Data: Snowflake + dbt (job posting, Jan 2025)."
- Bad: "Uses modern technology stack."
- Format: Tool/technology per category with evidence source]

### 5. Growth Trajectory

[Hiring velocity, revenue estimates, and expansion signals.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Posting 12 new roles/month (up from 5/month 6 months ago). Engineering and sales hiring concentrated. Revenue estimated $8-12M ARR based on headcount and vertical benchmarks. Expanding into European market (London office + 3 EU-focused job postings)."
- Bad: "Company is growing fast."
- Format: Quantified trends with time comparisons and evidence]

### 6. Competitive Landscape

[Direct competitors, market position, and differentiation.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Direct competitors: Stripe Connect (market leader), Adyen for Platforms (enterprise), PayPal Commerce Platform (legacy). Acme differentiates on developer experience and time-to-integration (avg 3 days vs. 3 weeks for Stripe Connect per G2 reviews). Positioned as challenger in Gartner's 2024 Market Guide."
- Bad: "Competes with other fintech companies."
- Format: Named competitors with positioning context and source]

### 7. Leadership Team

[Key executives with tenure, background, and relevance.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "CEO: Jane Smith (co-founder, 5yr tenure, ex-Stripe engineering lead). CTO: John Doe (joined 8 months ago, ex-AWS, database infrastructure background — signals platform investment). VP Sales: Maria Garcia (joined 3 months ago, ex-Datadog — signals go-to-market acceleration)."
- Bad: "Experienced leadership team."
- Format: Name, role, tenure, background, and sales-relevance inference]
