## Executive Summary

[2-3 sentences summarizing the prospect's core challenge, the proposed solution, and the expected business outcome.
GUIDANCE: Lead with the prospect's problem in their own words. State the outcome they will achieve, not the product you are selling. End with the most compelling reason to act now. Maximum 250 words.]

## 1. Cover Page

[Company logo, prospect logo, proposal title, date, version.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Clean layout with both logos, clear title "Proposal for [Company]: [Solution to Their Problem]"
- Bad: Generic "Sales Proposal" title with no prospect personalization
- Format: Single page, minimal text, professional design]

## 2. Current Situation

[Prospect's current state as confirmed through discovery.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Your compliance team currently spends 160 hours/month on manual reporting across 3 systems, with a 12% error rate that triggered 2 audit findings in the last fiscal year."
- Bad: "Many companies in your industry struggle with compliance."
- Format: 3-5 paragraphs using the prospect's own language. Quantify the cost of the problem in dollars, hours, or risk.]

## 3. Proposed Solution

### Solution Overview

[High-level description of the proposed solution tied to the prospect's needs.

- Bad: "Our platform has 50+ features including..."
- Format: 2-3 paragraph narrative, then requirements matrix below]

## 5. Competitive Comparison

### Why Us

[GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Unlike [Alternative], our solution provides [specific capability] that directly addresses your requirement for [X]. CustomerY achieved [quantified result] using this capability."
- Bad: "We are the market leader."
- Format: 3-5 bullet points, each with a proof point]

### Why Now

[GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Each month of delay costs approximately $X in continued manual effort. Your target go-live of [date] requires contract signature by [date] to allow [X weeks] of implementation."
- Bad: "Act now for a limited-time discount."
- Format: Quantify cost of delay, reference prospect's timeline]

### Why Not Alternatives

[GUIDANCE:
- Good: "CompetitorX requires [X months] implementation vs. our [Y weeks], resulting in [Z months] faster time-to-value. Their TCO over 3 years is [$X] higher when factoring in professional services."
- Bad: "CompetitorX is worse than us."
- Format: Factual comparison on prospect's evaluation criteria only]
