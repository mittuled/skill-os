## Executive Summary

The mid-market FinTech segment (200–1,000 employees) presents a high-conviction expansion opportunity with estimated ACVs of $25K–$100K and a consultative-hybrid sales motion. Buyers are compliance-led with CFO or CCO as economic buyer. The beachhead pain — 40+ hours of manual quarterly reporting — maps directly to our automation value metric and creates a defensible ROI narrative against Workiva and ComplyAdvantage.

---

## Segment Profile

**Segment:** Mid-Market FinTech
**ICP Firmographics:** 200–1,000 employees, US-regulated financial services, compliance team of 3–10 FTEs, uses legacy spreadsheet or point-solution workflows.

**Primary Pain Points:**
1. Manual regulatory reporting consuming 40+ hours per quarter per compliance analyst
2. Inconsistent data trails leading to audit findings and remediation costs
3. Compliance headcount scaling costs exceeding budget as reporting volume grows

### Buying Committee

| Role | Typical Title | Primary Priority | Engagement Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Economic Buyer | CFO or Chief Compliance Officer | Risk reduction, audit readiness | Stage 2+ |
| Champion | Compliance Manager | Workflow automation, time savings | Stage 1 |
| Technical Evaluator | IT Director | Data integration, SOC 2 posture | Stage 2–3 |
| End User | Compliance Analyst | Ease of use, report accuracy | Stage 3 |

---

## Competitive Positioning

**Value Proposition (outcome-based):** Cut regulatory reporting time by 70% with a complete, auditable data trail — eliminating the compliance risk of manual workflows.

### Competitive Matrix

| Competitor | Our Win Themes | Our Risk Themes | Landmine to Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| ComplyAdvantage | Faster implementation, lower TCO | Narrower for AML-heavy buyers | Don't lead with AML features |
| Workiva | Simpler UX, better mid-market pricing | Brand recognition gap | Don't let them anchor on Workiva's enterprise logo list |
| Manual spreadsheets | Audit trail, time savings, error reduction | Change management resistance | Discovery must quantify current error cost before proposing change |

---

## Deal Structure

**Pricing Model:** Per-seat
**Value Metric:** Reports automated per month
**Target ACV:** $62,500 (midpoint of $25K–$100K band)
**Discount Authority:** AEs hold up to 10%; VP Sales approves 10–20%; above 20% requires CFO review
**Contract Term:** 12-month initial, push for 24-month on enterprise-leaning buyers
**Procurement Norm:** Mid-market FinTech typically buys through security review + legal redline; budget in compliance or IT cost center

---

## Sales Motion Blueprint

**Motion Type:** Consultative-Hybrid — product demo anchored in buyer-specific use case, supported by ROI calculator and compliance risk quantification.

### Pipeline Stage Gates (MEDDIC)

| Stage | Exit Criteria | MEDDIC Elements Required |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Discovery | Pain confirmed, preliminary ROI shared | Identify Pain, Metrics |
| 2 — Qualification | Economic buyer identified, champion confirmed | Economic Buyer, Champion |
| 3 — Evaluation | Evaluation criteria agreed, timeline set | Decision Criteria, Decision Process |
| 4 — Proposal | Proposal accepted, legal review started | All MEDDIC elements validated |
| 5 — Close | Contract signed, kickoff scheduled | Full MEDDIC closure confirmed |

**Disqualification criteria:** No identified compliance pain, no budget authority access by Stage 2, no internal champion willing to sponsor the evaluation.
