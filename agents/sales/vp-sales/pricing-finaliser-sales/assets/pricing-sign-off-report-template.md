## Executive Summary

[2-3 sentences stating the sign-off decision (go/no-go/conditional), the composite score, and the primary driver of the decision.
GUIDANCE: Lead with the decision, then the score, then the key reason. Do not describe the evaluation process.]

## Field Feedback Summary

[Aggregated pricing feedback from the field.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Across 28 closed deals in Q3-Q4, average selling price was $42K vs. list price of $48K (12.5% discount rate). Top objection: 'price per seat too high for teams >50' (mentioned in 9 deals, 6 lost). AE consensus: current pricing sellable for teams <50, needs tier adjustment above 50."
- Bad: "Reps say the pricing is too high"
- Format: Table with deal count, average selling price, list price, discount rate, top objections with frequency, and AE sentiment summary]

## Willingness-to-Pay Analysis

[Comparison of proposed pricing against actual deal data.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table showing each tier with list price, average selling price, discount depth distribution (quartiles), deal velocity by tier, and risk flags for tiers where discount rate >20%
- Bad: "Customers are willing to pay our prices"
- Format: Tier-level table with quantified metrics and explicit risk flags]

## Competitive Pricing Benchmark

[Proposed pricing mapped against top 3 alternatives.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Matrix with competitor name, their list price, their street price, our proposed price, spread (positive/negative), and whether spread is justified by specific differentiators
- Bad: "We are priced competitively"
- Format: Table with one row per competitor plus a summary assessment]

## Pipeline Impact Model

[Effect of proposed pricing on current pipeline.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table listing deals at risk (deal name, stage, value, specific pricing impact), deals that benefit, and net pipeline value change with confidence range
- Bad: "Pipeline should be fine"
- Format: Deal-level table with rollup summary showing net impact]

## Scoring Summary

[Per-criterion scores using the scoring rubric.

GUIDANCE:
- Format: Table with Criterion, Weight, Score (0-10), Weighted Score, and Evidence Summary
- Include composite score and corresponding grade band
- Reference: `references/scoring-rubric.md`]

## Recommendations

[Prioritized actions based on the sign-off decision.

GUIDANCE: Each recommendation should be:
- Specific (not "fix pricing" but "restructure Tier 3 to $X per seat for teams >50, effective [date]")
- Actionable (assignable to Product, Finance, or Sales Ops)
- Prioritized (P1/P2/P3)
- Time-bound (specific deadline or milestone)]
