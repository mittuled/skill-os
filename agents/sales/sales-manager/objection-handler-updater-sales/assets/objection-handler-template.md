## How to Use This Handler

This handler provides **frameworks**, not scripts. Adapt the language to fit your authentic voice and the specific nuance of the buyer's concern. The structure is:

1. **Acknowledge** — Show you heard and understood the concern without deflecting.
2. **Reframe** — Shift the frame from objection to a more useful perspective.
3. **Evidence** — Support the reframe with a specific proof point.
4. **Check** — Confirm the response landed and test for residual concern.

If an objection recurs across multiple deals, log it as a signal in CRM and flag to the Sales Manager.

## Pricing Objections

---

### OBJ-PRICE-01: "Your pricing is too expensive."

**Frequency:** High — appears in [N]% of deals
**Win rate when this objection arises:** [X]% (vs. [Y]% overall)
**Deal stage most common:** [STAGE]

**Acknowledge:**
> "That's a fair thing to raise — budget decisions are real, and I want to make sure this is the right investment for you."

**Reframe:**
> "Let me make sure we're comparing the right things. The question isn't whether [COMPANY NAME] costs more than [ALTERNATIVE] — it's what the total cost of each path actually looks like. When [CUSTOMER REFERENCE] ran that comparison, they found that [SPECIFIC OUTCOME — e.g., the manual work [COMPANY NAME] eliminated saved them $[AMOUNT] in engineering hours in the first quarter alone]."

**Evidence:**
- Case study: [CUSTOMER NAME] — saved $[AMOUNT] in [TIME PERIOD] ([LINK TO CASE STUDY])
- ROI data: Customers in [SEGMENT] see [X]x return within [Y] months on average ([METRIC SOURCE])
- Proof point: "[CUSTOMER QUOTE]" — [NAME], [TITLE], [COMPANY]

**Check:**
> "Does that change how you're thinking about the investment, or is there a specific number we need to get to for this to move forward?"

**What not to do:** Do not immediately offer a discount. Discounting before exploring ROI signals that you don't believe in the price and trains buyers to always negotiate.

## Competitive Objections

---

### OBJ-COMP-01: "We're also looking at [COMPETITOR NAME]."

**Frequency:** High — [COMPETITOR] mentioned in [N]% of deals
**Win rate in competitive deals vs. [COMPETITOR]:** [X]%

**Acknowledge:**
> "Makes sense — [COMPETITOR] is a well-known option and worth evaluating. I'm glad you're doing a thorough comparison."

**Reframe:**
> "The decision usually comes down to [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR — e.g., depth of [CAPABILITY] vs. breadth of features]. Where teams that switched from [COMPETITOR] to us typically land is that [SPECIFIC DIFFERENTIATOR]. What does [CRITERIA] look like on your priority list?"

**Evidence:**
- Win story: [CUSTOMER NAME] chose us over [COMPETITOR] because [REASON] — [OUTCOME]
- Battle card: [LINK TO COMPETITIVE BATTLE CARD]
- Third-party validation: [G2 / ANALYST REPORT / PEER REVIEW — LINK]

**Check:**
> "What's the criteria where [COMPETITOR] is leading in your evaluation right now?"

**Do not:** Disparage the competitor. Speak to your differentiated value, not their weaknesses.

---

### OBJ-COMP-02: "[COMPETITOR] has a feature we need that you don't have."

**Frequency:** Medium
**Most common missing feature cited:** [FEATURE NAME]

**Acknowledge:**
> "I appreciate you being direct about that — let me make sure I understand exactly what you need [FEATURE] to do for you."

**Reframe (if roadmap):**
> "That capability is on our roadmap for [QUARTER]. In the meantime, [WORKAROUND DESCRIPTION] achieves the same outcome for most teams. Would it help to see how [REFERENCE CUSTOMER] handles that today?"

**Reframe (if not on roadmap):**
> "That's genuinely not something we do today, and I don't want to oversell. What I'd want to understand is how critical that specific feature is relative to [STRONG CAPABILITY WHERE YOU WIN]. For [X]% of teams in your situation, [ALTERNATIVE] covers the requirement — but you're in the best position to judge whether it fits."

**Evidence:**
- Roadmap reference: [PRODUCT ROADMAP LINK — internal]
- Workaround reference: [INTERNAL LINK TO SE RESOURCES]
- Customer reference for workaround: [CUSTOMER NAME]

## Timing Objections

---

### OBJ-TIME-01: "We're not ready to make a decision until [QUARTER / NEXT YEAR]."

**Frequency:** High in early pipeline

**Acknowledge:**
> "Timing matters — I don't want to push for a decision before you're ready to execute on it."

**Reframe:**
> "What I'd like to understand is whether the timing is driven by budget cycles, bandwidth, or something else. If it's budget, [PAYMENT OPTION — e.g., quarterly payments] may align better to your cycle. If it's bandwidth, what would need to be true internally for this to be a priority?"

**Evidence:**
- Customer who delayed and the cost of delay: [ANECDOTE — e.g., "One [INDUSTRY] team pushed the decision 6 months and estimated it cost them $[AMOUNT] in [METRIC] during that window."]

---

### OBJ-TIME-02: "We're in the middle of another initiative and don't have bandwidth."

**Acknowledge:**
> "That's real — trying to onboard a new platform mid-initiative usually backfires."

**Reframe:**
> "Our fastest onboarding is [X] hours of active time spread over [N] weeks — most of it is async. Would it help to scope what the first 30 days actually look like so you can judge whether it conflicts with your current initiative?"
