# Scoring Rubric: icp-builder

Evaluates prospect-to-ICP fit across 6 dimensions to determine segment prioritization and pipeline focus.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Company Size | 15% | Employee count and revenue relative to product's sweet spot |
| 2 | Industry Fit | 20% | Alignment between prospect's industry and proven use cases |
| 3 | Technology Stack | 15% | Compatibility and integration readiness with existing tools |
| 4 | Growth Stage | 15% | Company maturity and trajectory relative to product adoption patterns |
| 5 | Pain Intensity | 20% | Severity and urgency of the problem the product solves |
| 6 | Budget Authority | 15% | Ability and willingness to allocate budget for the solution |
| **Total** | | **100%** | |

## Scale

Each criterion is scored **0-10**:
- **0**: No evidence / completely absent
- **5**: Partially present with significant gaps
- **10**: Fully present, comprehensive, no gaps

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score × weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Tier 1 — Ideal Fit | Matches ICP on all 6 dimensions with strong signals; historically highest win rate and LTV | Prioritize immediately; assign senior AE; fast-track through pipeline |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Tier 1 — Strong Fit | Matches on 5+ dimensions with minor gaps; strong conversion likelihood | Prioritize; assign dedicated AE; standard sales motion |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Tier 2 — Good Fit | Matches on 4+ dimensions; some gaps that may extend sales cycle | Pursue with standard resources; monitor for stalls |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Tier 2 — Adequate Fit | Mixed signals across dimensions; conversion possible but not probable | Pursue only if pipeline is thin; allocate minimal resources |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | Tier 3 — Weak Fit | Significant gaps on multiple dimensions; historically low win rate | Deprioritize; nurture only; do not allocate AE time |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Deprioritize | Poor fit across most dimensions; high risk of wasted resources or churn | Disqualify; redirect to self-serve or partner channel if applicable |

## Signal Tables

### Company Size

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Employee count 100-1,000 (or product sweet spot); revenue $10M-$100M ARR; dedicated team for the problem domain; multi-department potential |
| 7-8 | Employee count within 50% of sweet spot; revenue within range; at least one team dedicated to the problem |
| 5-6 | Employee count at edges of sweet spot (50-99 or 1,001-2,000); may lack dedicated team; single-department use case |
| 3-4 | Employee count outside sweet spot but not disqualifying; limited organizational complexity to justify investment |
| 0-2 | Fewer than 20 employees or 10,000+; product requires organizational scale that does not exist or enterprise bureaucracy that blocks adoption |

### Industry Fit

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Industry with 3+ closed-won references; regulatory or operational drivers create urgent need; product features built for this vertical |
| 7-8 | Industry with 1-2 closed-won references; clear use case alignment; no major compliance or integration blockers |
| 5-6 | Adjacent industry with transferable use cases; no direct references but logical fit; may require minor product adaptation |
| 3-4 | Industry with no references and unclear use case fit; prospect would be a pioneering deployment requiring significant validation |
| 0-2 | Industry fundamentally misaligned with product capabilities; regulatory constraints prevent deployment; no path to product-market fit |

### Technology Stack

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Native integration with prospect's core systems; API-first architecture on their side; dedicated IT/ops team for tool management; modern cloud infrastructure |
| 7-8 | Integration possible with standard connectors; prospect uses common platforms; IT team available for implementation support |
| 5-6 | Integration requires custom development or middleware; prospect uses legacy systems with limited API access; IT bandwidth constrained |
| 3-4 | Significant integration barriers; prospect's stack requires major workarounds; no API access to critical systems |
| 0-2 | Incompatible technology stack; prospect uses air-gapped or proprietary systems; integration not feasible without platform change |

### Growth Stage

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Series B-D or equivalent revenue stage; actively scaling teams and processes; pain intensifies with growth; budget allocated for operational tooling |
| 7-8 | Series A or early growth; building scalable processes for the first time; recognizes the problem but may lack budget maturity |
| 5-6 | Pre-revenue or very early stage (problem exists but competing priorities); or mature/stable company with low growth urgency |
| 3-4 | Seed stage with no operational complexity yet; or declining company reducing spend; problem is theoretical not experienced |
| 0-2 | Pre-product company with no operational need; or company in active downsizing/restructuring with frozen budgets |

### Pain Intensity

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Prospect quantifies the cost of the problem unprompted; active RFP or evaluation in progress; executive sponsor identified; timeline pressure from regulation, board mandate, or competitive threat |
| 7-8 | Prospect acknowledges pain and has explored solutions; problem is on the roadmap; budget discussion has occurred; no active RFP but open to evaluation |
| 5-6 | Prospect recognizes the problem but has not prioritized solving it; "nice to have" not "must have"; no budget allocated; would consider if approached |
| 3-4 | Prospect is unaware of the problem or considers it minor; workarounds are acceptable; no trigger event on the horizon |
| 0-2 | Prospect does not have the problem; or has recently solved it with a competing solution; or actively dismissed the need |

### Budget Authority

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Economic buyer identified and engaged; budget confirmed or allocated; procurement process known and timeline established; previous purchases in similar price range |
| 7-8 | Economic buyer identified but not yet engaged; budget likely available based on company stage and spend patterns; procurement process understood |
| 5-6 | Budget holder unclear; budget may exist but not allocated; prospect requires internal approval with unknown timeline; committee decision |
| 3-4 | No budget allocated; champion has influence but no spending authority; requires budget creation (not allocation); long approval chain |
| 0-2 | No identifiable budget authority; company in budget freeze; prospect is an individual contributor with no path to procurement; free-tier expectation |
