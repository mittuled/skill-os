# Scoring Rubric: pricing-finaliser-sales

Evaluates whether proposed pricing is sellable based on field feedback, competitive positioning, and pipeline impact before go-to-market launch.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Field Feedback Alignment | 25% | Degree to which proposed pricing aligns with actual field feedback from AEs and SEs on buyer willingness-to-pay |
| 2 | Competitive Positioning | 20% | Whether proposed pricing creates defensible positioning against the top 3 alternatives buyers evaluate |
| 3 | Discount Rate Health | 20% | Whether the pricing structure minimizes the gap between list price and street price, keeping discount rates within policy |
| 4 | Pipeline Impact | 20% | Net effect of proposed pricing on current pipeline: deals preserved vs. deals at risk |
| 5 | Structural Simplicity | 15% | Whether the pricing model is simple enough for reps to explain and buyers to approve without excessive internal selling |
| **Total** | | **100%** | |

## Scale

Each criterion is scored **0-10**:
- **0**: No evidence / completely absent
- **5**: Partially present with significant gaps
- **10**: Fully present, comprehensive, no gaps

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score x weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 - 10.0 | Ship-Ready | Pricing is field-validated, competitively positioned, and pipeline-safe | Sign off and launch immediately |
| A | 8.0 - 8.9 | Strong | Minor adjustments needed but fundamentally sellable | Sign off with documented minor conditions |
| B | 7.0 - 7.9 | Sellable with Risk | Some gaps in field alignment or competitive positioning | Conditional sign-off with 60-day review checkpoint |
| C | 5.0 - 6.9 | Needs Work | Material gaps in multiple criteria that will impact win rates | Return to Product/Finance with specific remediation requirements |
| D | 3.0 - 4.9 | Not Sellable | Field feedback or competitive data contradicts proposed pricing | Block launch; require fundamental restructuring |
| F | 0.0 - 2.9 | No Basis | Pricing proposed without field data, competitive context, or pipeline analysis | Reject; initiate field research before re-proposal |

## Signal Tables

### Field Feedback Alignment

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | 20+ deal data points reviewed; proposed price within 10% of average selling price; <5% of reps report pricing as primary objection; win rate stable or improving at proposed price |
| 7-8 | 10-20 deal data points; proposed price within 20% of average selling price; <15% of reps report pricing objections; win rate impact estimated as neutral |
| 5-6 | 5-10 deal data points; proposed price diverges from average selling price by 20-30%; pricing objections present but not dominant loss reason |
| 3-4 | <5 deal data points; proposed price diverges >30% from field reality; pricing is a top-3 loss reason in recent quarter |
| 0-2 | No field data collected; pricing set without AE/SE input; no win/loss analysis available |

### Competitive Positioning

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | All 3 top competitors benchmarked with street pricing; positive spread (premium justified by demonstrated value) for 2+ competitors; competitive win rate >50% |
| 7-8 | 3 competitors benchmarked; positive spread for 1+ competitor; competitive win rate 40-50%; premium justified by specific differentiators |
| 5-6 | 2 competitors benchmarked; mixed spread (premium justified for some segments, not others); competitive win rate 30-40% |
| 3-4 | 1 competitor benchmarked; negative spread (premium without differentiation) for primary competitor; competitive win rate <30% |
| 0-2 | No competitive pricing data gathered; pricing set without market context |

### Discount Rate Health

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Average discount rate <10%; 90%+ deals close within standard discount authority; no tier with discount rate >15%; discount distribution is tight (low variance) |
| 7-8 | Average discount rate 10-15%; 80%+ deals close within standard authority; one tier may exceed 15% but with business justification |
| 5-6 | Average discount rate 15-20%; 60-80% deals require manager-level discount approval; systematic discounting in one segment |
| 3-4 | Average discount rate 20-30%; majority of deals require VP-level discount approval; list price widely perceived as a starting point for negotiation |
| 0-2 | Average discount rate >30%; list price is fiction; every deal is a custom negotiation; no discount policy adherence |

### Pipeline Impact

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | <5% of current pipeline value at risk from pricing change; majority of pipeline benefits from simplification or repositioning; net pipeline impact positive |
| 7-8 | 5-10% of pipeline at risk; identified deals have mitigation plans; net pipeline impact neutral to slightly positive |
| 5-6 | 10-20% of pipeline at risk; some deals lack mitigation plans; net pipeline impact uncertain |
| 3-4 | 20-35% of pipeline at risk; several large deals specifically threatened; no mitigation plans in place |
| 0-2 | >35% of pipeline at risk; pricing change triggers repricing conversations on committed deals; net pipeline impact clearly negative |

### Structural Simplicity

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Pricing fits on one page; rep can explain in <60 seconds; buyer can get internal budget approval without a pricing deck; value metric is intuitive |
| 7-8 | Pricing requires a one-page explainer; rep needs 2-3 minutes to walk through; buyer may need one internal meeting to explain |
| 5-6 | Pricing requires a multi-page guide; reps frequently ask for deal desk support; buyer needs multiple internal approvals with explanation |
| 3-4 | Pricing is highly conditional (custom quotes, usage calculations, multiple variables); reps cannot quote without deal desk; buyer procurement finds it confusing |
| 0-2 | Pricing model is opaque even internally; no two reps explain it the same way; buyer cannot understand total cost without a detailed analysis |
