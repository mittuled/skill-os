## Executive Summary

Pricing strategy for the Insights Analytics Platform is anchored in field willingness-to-pay data from 90 discovery calls and 35 closed-lost records. The recommended value metric is **seats** (scales with team size, buyer understands intuitively). A three-tier rate card is proposed with balanced scenario pricing. Key risk signal: 30% of closed-lost deals cited pricing as a primary reason — pricing strategy must address this at the SMB tier first.

---

## Value Metric Selection

**Recommended metric:** Seats (per named user, annual license)

**Rationale:** Seats scale naturally with team size in analytics tools, are measurable at point of sale, and correlate with the customer's expansion of usage. Discovery call data confirms buyers anchor their budget to headcount — not data volume or report count. Seats create a natural land-and-expand motion as teams grow.

**Alternative considered:** Records Processed — rejected because SMB buyers cannot forecast data volume at purchase time, creating a friction point that slows time-to-close.

## Tier Definitions and Rate Card

| Segment | List Price (Annual) | Floor Price | Competitive Avg | Our Premium/Discount |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SMB (< 200 employees) | $14,000 | $10,500 | $9,500 | +47% — requires strong value narrative |
| Mid-Market (200–1,000) | $42,000 | $31,500 | $28,333 | +48% — defensible with feature differentiation |
| Enterprise (1,000+) | $140,000 | $105,000 | $95,000 | +47% — premium justified by depth of analytics |

**Note:** SMB premium vs competitive average is high — recommend monitoring win rate closely in Q1 and adjusting SMB list price to $10,000–$12,000 if win rate falls below 20%.

## Discount Policy

| Authority Level | Max Discount | Approval Required |
|---|---|---|
| Account Executive | Up to 10% | Self-service |
| VP Sales | 10–20% | VP Sales verbal |
| CFO | 20–35% | CFO written sign-off |
| Board | 35–50% | Board approval required |

Floor prices are non-negotiable. Any deal below floor price requires a strategic rationale and board sign-off.

## Scenario Revenue Projections

| Scenario | Price Multiplier | Est. Win Rate | Avg ACV | Projected ARR (40 deals) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Aggressive | 1.15x | 20.0% | $230,750 | $1,846,000 |
| Balanced | 1.0x | 25.0% | $200,650 | $2,006,500 |
| Conservative | 0.85x | 30.0% | $170,553 | $2,046,630 |

**Recommended scenario: Balanced** — maximises ARR while maintaining a 25% win rate assumption. Conservative scenario produces marginally more ARR but requires 30% win rates not yet demonstrated in mid-market.
