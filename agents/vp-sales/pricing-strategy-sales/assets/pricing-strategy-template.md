GUIDANCE: Lead with the recommended model and why it fits the segment. Include the expected revenue impact.]

## Willingness-to-Pay Analysis

[Field-sourced pricing signal data by segment.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table showing segment, sample size, stated WTP range, revealed WTP (actual selling price), price sensitivity rating (high/medium/low), and key anchoring factors (competitor pricing, internal budget norms, ROI expectations)
- Bad: "Customers will pay what we charge"
- Format: Segment-level table with quantified data points and minimum 10 data points per segment]

## Competitive Pricing Landscape

[Competitor pricing and packaging mapped.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Matrix with competitor, their tiers (names + prices), packaging per tier (features, limits), contract terms, published vs. street price, and our positioning opportunity per tier
- Bad: "Competitors charge similar prices"
- Format: Detailed comparison table with one row per competitor-tier combination]

## Value Metric Recommendation

[The metric that best correlates with customer value.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Recommended value metric: active users (not seats provisioned). Justification: correlation analysis across 40 deals shows 0.78 correlation between active users and renewal likelihood vs. 0.31 for provisioned seats. Scales with value: customers with more active users derive more value and have higher NRR."
- Bad: "We should charge per seat"
- Format: Metric name, correlation evidence, scaling justification, measurability at point of sale, comparison with 2-3 alternative metrics considered and rejected]

## Pricing Model Design

[Tier definitions, rate card, and discount policy.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table with tier name, target segment, value metric range, price point, included features, and ACV range. Separate table for discount policy with authority levels and conditions.
- Bad: "Three tiers: Basic, Pro, Enterprise"
- Format: Two tables -- one for tier structure, one for discount policy. Include entry price point and anchor price rationale.]

## Scenario Projections

[Three scenarios modeled against current pipeline.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table with scenario name (Aggressive/Balanced/Conservative), price point per tier, projected ACV impact, projected win rate impact, projected revenue attainment, and risk factors
- Bad: "We expect revenue to increase"
- Format: Side-by-side comparison table with quantified projections per scenario, plus a recommendation for which scenario to pursue with rationale]

## Recommendations

[Prioritized actions to implement the pricing strategy.

GUIDANCE: Each recommendation should be:
- Specific (not "implement pricing" but "configure Tier 2 at $X/user/month in billing system by [date]")
- Actionable (assignable to Finance, Product, or RevOps)
- Prioritized (P1/P2/P3)]
