## Executive Summary

[2-3 sentences summarizing the sales motion recommended for the target segment, the primary buyer persona, and the expected ACV range.
GUIDANCE: Lead with the recommended motion type and why it fits the segment. Do not describe methodology.]

## Segment Profile

[Firmographic and behavioral profile of the beachhead segment.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: "Mid-market B2B SaaS companies (50-500 employees) with dedicated RevOps teams, $5M-50M ARR, currently using spreadsheet-based forecasting, buying cycle 45-60 days, budget authority at VP Sales level"
- Bad: "Technology companies that need our product"
- Format: Table with rows for company size, industry, ARR range, tech maturity, buying cycle, and budget authority]

## Buyer Persona Map

[Buying committee roles mapped to engagement strategy.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table with columns for Role, Title, Priority (P1/P2), Pain Points, Messaging Hook, and Engagement Channel. Minimum 4 roles: Economic Buyer, Champion, Technical Evaluator, End User
- Bad: "The buyer is the VP of Sales"
- Format: Table with one row per persona, ordered by influence on deal outcome]

## Competitive Positioning Matrix

[Top 3 alternatives with differentiation analysis.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Matrix with columns for Alternative, Type (competitor/DIY/status quo), Strength, Weakness, Our Differentiator, Win Theme, and Loss Theme. Include "status quo" as an alternative
- Bad: "We are better than competitors"
- Format: Table with one row per alternative, plus a summary row for positioning statement]

## Deal Structure Template

[Recommended commercial terms for the segment.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Pricing model (per-seat/usage/platform), ACV range ($X-$Y), standard contract term, discount guardrails (max %, approval levels), typical expansion path, procurement norms for the segment
- Bad: "Price TBD based on deal"
- Format: Structured list with explicit numbers and thresholds]

## Sales Motion Blueprint

[Motion type, pipeline stages, and qualification criteria.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Motion type with justification, 5-7 pipeline stages mapped to buyer milestones, MEDDIC criteria per stage gate with minimum thresholds, expected velocity metrics (days per stage, overall cycle)
- Bad: "We use a consultative sales approach"
- Format: Table mapping each stage to buyer milestone, entry criteria, required activities, exit criteria, and expected duration]

## Recommendations

[Prioritized next steps for operationalizing this opportunity frame.

GUIDANCE: Each recommendation should be:
- Specific (not "build a playbook" but "Sales Manager builds discovery question bank targeting VP RevOps persona by [date]")
- Actionable (assignable to a person/team)
- Prioritized (P1/P2/P3)]
