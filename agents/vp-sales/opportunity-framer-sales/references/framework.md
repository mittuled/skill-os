# Framework: Opportunity Framing

## Purpose

Provides the structured methodology for framing a sales opportunity against a beachhead segment, ensuring all downstream sales execution (playbook, process, enablement) is grounded in validated segment, buyer, and competitive data.

## Segment Analysis Method

### Firmographic Profiling

| Dimension | What to Capture | Source |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Company size | Employee count range, revenue range | CRM, LinkedIn, market data |
| Industry vertical | Primary and adjacent verticals | CRM deal history, market research |
| Tech maturity | Current stack, build-vs-buy tendency | SE notes, discovery calls |
| Buying behavior | Cycle length, committee size, procurement process | Closed-won deal data |
| Budget authority | Title and level of economic buyer | Win/loss interviews |

### Buyer Committee Mapping (Power Map)

Map every deal's buying committee to these archetypes:

1. **Economic Buyer** -- Holds budget authority, signs contract. Cares about ROI, risk, and strategic alignment.
2. **Champion** -- Internal advocate who drives evaluation. Cares about solving their specific pain and looking good internally.
3. **Technical Evaluator** -- Validates product meets technical requirements. Cares about architecture, integration, and security.
4. **End User** -- Day-to-day user of the product. Cares about UX, workflow fit, and time savings.
5. **Blocker** -- Stakeholder who may oppose the purchase. Identify their concern and neutralization strategy.

For each archetype, document: typical title, priority (P1 must-engage, P2 should-engage), key pain points, preferred messaging angle, and best engagement channel.

## Competitive Positioning Method

### Three Alternatives Framework

Every segment evaluates three types of alternatives:

1. **Direct competitor** -- Another vendor solving the same problem
2. **DIY / internal build** -- The prospect builds their own solution
3. **Status quo** -- Do nothing, keep current process

For each alternative, assess:
- **Where they win**: Specific scenarios where this alternative is genuinely better
- **Where they lose**: Specific scenarios where our product is stronger
- **Landmines to set**: Discovery questions that expose the alternative's weakness
- **Proof points**: Customer evidence that demonstrates our advantage

### Outcome-Based Positioning

Frame the value proposition around customer outcomes, not features:

| Instead of (Feature) | Use (Outcome) |
|----------------------|---------------|
| "We have AI-powered forecasting" | "Our customers reduce forecast error by 30%, giving their board confidence in projections" |
| "We integrate with Salesforce" | "Your reps see pipeline insights inside the tool they already live in, no tab-switching" |
| "We have real-time dashboards" | "Your VP Sales sees pipeline changes the moment they happen, not at Friday's review" |

## Deal Structure Design Method

### Pricing Model Selection

| Motion Type | Recommended Model | Rationale |
|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Product-led | Usage-based or freemium + seat | Aligns cost to value realized, low barrier to entry |
| Velocity (SMB) | Per-seat, monthly/annual | Simple to understand, fast procurement |
| Enterprise | Platform fee + usage | Captures platform value, scales with adoption |
| Consultative | Outcome-based or tiered | Anchors price to business impact |

### Discount Guardrails

| Discount Level | Approval Required | Conditions |
|---------------|-------------------|------------|
| 0-10% | AE authority | Standard negotiation, annual commitment |
| 11-20% | Sales Manager | Multi-year deal or strategic account justification |
| 21-30% | VP Sales | Executive approval with written justification |
| 30%+ | Not authorized | Requires pricing exception process |

## Sales Motion Selection Criteria

| Criterion | Product-Led | Velocity | Consultative | Enterprise |
|-----------|------------|----------|--------------|------------|
| ACV range | <$5K | $5K-$25K | $25K-$100K | $100K+ |
| Buying committee | 1-2 people | 2-4 people | 4-6 people | 6+ people |
| Cycle time | <14 days | 14-45 days | 45-90 days | 90-180 days |
| SE involvement | None | Light | Moderate | Heavy |
| Champion required | No | Helpful | Required | Required |
| POC typical | No (free trial) | Rare | Common | Expected |

## MEDDIC Stage-Gate Criteria

For each pipeline stage, the following MEDDIC elements must be validated:

| Stage | Minimum MEDDIC Criteria |
|-------|------------------------|
| Discovery | Identified Pain confirmed, Metrics discussion initiated |
| Qualification | Economic Buyer identified, Decision Criteria captured |
| Evaluation | Decision Process mapped, Champion confirmed and active |
| Proposal | Metrics agreed (ROI case), all Decision Criteria addressed |
| Negotiation | Economic Buyer engaged, paper process and timeline confirmed |
| Closed-Won | All 6 MEDDIC elements confirmed, contract executed |
