# Framework: Competitive Deep Diver

Defines the intelligence-gathering methodology, analysis dimensions, and competitive response framework for thorough competitor analysis.

## Intelligence Source Matrix

| Source Type | Examples | Strength | Limitation |
|-------------|---------|---------|-----------|
| Product surfaces | Website, app, documentation, changelog | Reveals features, UX, positioning | May not reflect private or beta functionality |
| Pricing pages | Public pricing, G2 reviews mentioning price | Reveals pricing model and tier logic | Enterprise pricing often hidden; ask sales |
| Job postings | LinkedIn, Greenhouse, Lever | Reveals hiring priorities = future investment areas | 3-6 month lag; speculative |
| Press releases | PR Newswire, TechCrunch, company blog | Fundraising, partnerships, customer wins | Curated — only positive news |
| Review sites | G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Reddit, HackerNews | Unfiltered user sentiment, competitor weaknesses | Sample bias toward strong opinions |
| Patent filings | USPTO, EPO, Google Patents | Technical direction and proprietary claims | 2-3 year lag; does not imply production use |
| Engineering blogs | Company blog, dev.to, GitHub repos | Technical architecture decisions | Only what they choose to share |
| Sales battle cards | Internal (from sales team) | Objection patterns, real-world pricing, customer concerns | Anecdotal; subject to sales bias |
| LinkedIn signals | Headcount growth, executive hires | Company health and strategic priorities | Public data only; noisy |

## Porter's Five Forces (Category Context)

Before deep-diving a competitor, use Porter's Five Forces to contextualise their position:

| Force | Analysis Question | Implication for Competitor |
|-------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Threat of new entrants | How easy is it to enter this category? | High threat → competitor under pricing pressure |
| Buyer power | How easily can customers switch? | High buyer power → competitor likely to offer concessions |
| Supplier power | Are there unique technology dependencies? | High supplier power → competitor has input costs the company may share |
| Threat of substitutes | What non-obvious alternatives exist? | Substitutes shape what customers tolerate |
| Competitive rivalry | How intense is head-to-head competition? | High rivalry → competitor is more likely to respond to product moves |

## Competitive Intelligence Dimensions

### Product Analysis

| Dimension | What to Capture | Signal to Look For |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Feature set | Current capability map (vs. company) | Gaps that create sales vulnerability; strengths that require roadmap response |
| Technical architecture | API-first vs. monolithic; integration ecosystem | Build/buy flexibility for their customers |
| Product-led signals | Free tier, trial, PLG motion | Expansion into the company's acquisition channels |
| UX quality | Onboarding time, complexity, help documentation | Competitive disadvantage if they are significantly better/worse |

### Strategy Analysis

| Dimension | What to Capture | Signal to Look For |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Positioning | ICP, differentiation claims, messaging | Whitespace the competitor is leaving; head-on collisions to prepare for |
| Pricing | Model (seat/usage/flat), tiers, discounting | Price-based objections in sales; under- or over-pricing signals |
| GTM motion | PLG, enterprise sales, partner-led | Which acquisition channels they are investing in |
| Customer segments | Named customers, case studies, market focus | Segment they are abandoning vs. doubling down on |

## Strategic Response Framework

After analysis, classify the required response:

| Response Type | Trigger | Action |
|--------------|---------|--------|
| Match | Competitor has a feature that is causing sales losses in >20% of deals | Add to roadmap; consider fast-follow or acquisition |
| Differentiate | Competitor is investing in a direction that would commoditise the category | Double down on differentiation; reposition messaging |
| Ignore | Competitor is strong in a segment the company is not targeting | Monitor; do not change strategy |
| Pre-empt | Competitor is about to enter the company's core segment (job posting signal) | Accelerate key features; lock in customer contracts |
