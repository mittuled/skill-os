# research-roadmap-contributor

## Agent: Applied Research Lead

L1 applied research leader (1x) responsible for market research synthesis, technology trend analysis, competitive deep dives, benchmarking, and contributing to the research roadmap.

Department ethos: [ideal-applied-research.md](../../../../departments/applied-research/ideal-applied-research.md)

## Skill Description

The research roadmap contributor translates research findings into product roadmap inputs and identifies open research questions that should be answered before key product decisions are made.

## When to Use

- When the quarterly or annual product roadmap planning cycle begins and research input is needed.
- When a product decision requires market evidence that has not yet been gathered.
- When research findings challenge or validate existing roadmap assumptions.

## Workflow

1. **Review current roadmap**: Examine the product roadmap to understand planned initiatives and their underlying assumptions. Deliverable: assumption inventory.
2. **Map research to roadmap items**: Connect existing research findings to roadmap items, noting where evidence supports, challenges, or is missing for each initiative. Deliverable: research-roadmap alignment matrix.
3. **Identify open questions**: Flag roadmap items that lack research validation and formulate the specific research questions needed. Deliverable: prioritised research question backlog.
4. **Present to product**: Share the alignment matrix and open questions with the product team during roadmap planning. Deliverable: research input presentation with recommendations.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Research as a blocker**: Insisting every roadmap item needs research validation before proceeding. *Why*: some decisions are low-risk and do not warrant research investment; research should focus on high-stakes unknowns.
- **Contributing findings without context**: Sharing raw research data without explaining its relevance to specific roadmap items. *Why*: product managers cannot translate raw data into roadmap implications without explicit connection.

## Output

**On success**: A research-roadmap alignment matrix showing which roadmap items have supporting evidence, which lack validation, and a prioritised list of research questions to address before key decisions.

**On failure**: Report which roadmap items could not be assessed (e.g., insufficient context on the initiative), what was mapped, and recommend follow-up conversations with product to fill gaps.
