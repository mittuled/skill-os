# Framework: early-community-builder

Defines the founding member recruitment model, norm-setting process, content seeding formula, and retention benchmarks for bootstrapping a new community.

## Founding Member Profile

Target **20–50 founding members** before any public launch. Quality over volume: founding members set the cultural DNA.

| Profile Dimension | Criteria | Why It Matters |
|------------------|---------|---------------|
| Domain expertise | Deep knowledge of the community's subject area | Models the level of conversation the community aims for |
| Network influence | Active in adjacent communities or social channels | Brings in second-order members and credibility |
| Engagement motivation | Intrinsic interest, not just reward-seeking | Sustains engagement after initial novelty fades |
| Communication quality | Writes clearly, responds constructively | Establishes communication norms others emulate |
| Diversity of perspective | Different roles, backgrounds, or use cases | Prevents echo chamber from day one |

## Sourcing Channels for Founding Members

| Channel | Best For | Outreach Approach | Expected Conversion |
|---------|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Beta user list | Product-adjacent communities | Personal email with specific invitation | 20–40% |
| LinkedIn connections | Professional communities | Direct message with value proposition | 10–20% |
| Twitter/X followers | Developer and creator communities | Public tweet + DM combination | 5–15% |
| Existing communities | Adjacent interest groups | Participate first, then invite | 15–25% |
| Podcast/newsletter audiences | Niche expertise communities | Author collaboration invite | 10–30% |
| Internal team network | Early-stage startups | Direct ask from founder or exec | 30–50% |

## Personal Invitation Template Structure

Effective founding member invitations include:
1. **Why you specifically** — acknowledge their expertise or contribution
2. **What the community is for** — one sentence on purpose and audience
3. **What founding means** — concrete benefits and expectations
4. **The ask** — single clear CTA with low friction (link or 5-minute call)

## Norm-Setting Framework

Co-create norms *with* founding members, not *for* them. Schedule a norm-setting session (async or live) in week 1.

| Norm Category | Example Questions to Surface | Output |
|--------------|------------------------------|--------|
| Tone and communication | How do we disagree constructively? | Communication guidelines |
| Content scope | What's on-topic vs. off-topic here? | Topic guidelines |
| Recognition | How do we celebrate contributions? | Recognition rituals |
| Moderation | What crosses the line and what happens? | Moderation policy |
| Privacy | What's shareable outside this community? | Privacy norms |

## Ritual Calendar Template

Establish at least **2 recurring rituals** before public launch. Rituals provide predictable engagement moments.

| Ritual Type | Frequency | Purpose | Example |
|-------------|-----------|---------|---------|
| Introduction thread | One-time, recurring for new members | Onboarding and relationship building | "Introduce yourself: what are you working on?" |
| Weekly prompt | Weekly | Low-friction activity touchpoint | "Share your win this week" |
| AMA with guest | Monthly | High-value content event | "AMA with [expert]" |
| Feedback loop | Monthly | Members influence community direction | "What should we discuss next month?" |
| Milestone celebration | Ad hoc | Recognition and community pride | "Congrats to [member] on [achievement]" |

## Content Seed Package (Minimum Before Launch)

Apply the **3-2-1-1 formula**:
- **3 resource posts**: Curated guides, templates, or reference material directly useful to members
- **2 discussion starters**: Open questions inviting opinion, experience, or debate
- **1 introduction thread**: "Introduce yourself" as the default onboarding ritual
- **1 ritual announcement**: Explain the first recurring ritual so members know what to expect

Founding members should author at least 4 of the 7+ posts to model peer-to-peer contribution.

## Early-Stage Retention Benchmarks

| Week | Target: % of Founding Members Still Active | If Below Target |
|------|------------------------------------------|-----------------|
| Week 1 | > 80% | Personal check-in with silent members |
| Week 2 | > 70% | Re-engagement prompt; ask for feedback |
| Week 4 | > 60% | One-on-one conversation; redesign ritual |
| Week 8 | > 50% | Evaluate founding member fit; expand cohort |

**Active** = logged in and posted at least once in the week.

## Transition Threshold to Public Launch

Before opening to public, verify:
- Founding member 4-week retention ≥ 50%
- At least 20 posts seeded across ≥ 5 topics
- Community guidelines published and visible
- At least 1 ritual has run successfully
- Moderation coverage confirmed for launch week
