# Scoring Rubric: company-name-selection

Evaluates naming candidates against criteria that determine whether a name can carry a brand effectively across markets, registrations, and time.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Memorability | 25% | How easily the name is recalled after a single exposure, including phonetic simplicity and distinctiveness |
| 2 | Brand Fit | 25% | How well the name reflects the brand personality, values, and target audience expectations |
| 3 | Distinctiveness | 20% | How differentiated the name is from competitors and category conventions |
| 4 | Domain & Handle Availability | 15% | Availability of .com and primary TLDs, social handles, and low acquisition cost if domain is taken |
| 5 | Trademark Risk | 10% | Absence of conflicting registrations in target jurisdictions and product categories |
| 6 | Linguistic Safety | 5% | Freedom from negative connotations, mispronunciation risks, or offensive meanings in target languages |
| **Total** | | **100%** | |

## Scale

Each criterion is scored **0-10**:
- **0**: No evidence / completely absent
- **5**: Partially present with significant gaps
- **10**: Fully present, comprehensive, no gaps

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score × weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Exceptional | Name is highly memorable, a perfect brand fit, clearly distinctive, .com available or easily acquirable, no trademark conflicts, clean linguistically | Proceed to legal trademark filing and domain registration immediately |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Strong | Name meets all key criteria with minor gaps (e.g., .com taken but .io or brand-specific TLD available at reasonable cost) | Proceed with awareness of the one gap; resolve before launch |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Good | Name is solid but has one notable weakness — slightly generic, or a low-risk trademark proximity — that requires a documented mitigation plan | Proceed with mitigation plan in place; revisit after trademark counsel review |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Adequate | Name has two or more moderate weaknesses — forgettable in testing, domain is expensive, or minor negative connotations in one market | Return to generation round with specific constraints; do not invest in identity design yet |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | Weak | Name has significant gaps in multiple criteria; likely to create confusion, legal risk, or poor recall in market | Discard candidate; re-run generation with tighter brief |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Failing | Name is generic, conflicts with an existing trademark, has harmful connotations, or is impossible to recall | Reject immediately; do not present to stakeholders |

## Signal Tables

### Memorability

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Name recalled correctly by 4+ of 5 testers 24 hours after first exposure; 5 characters or fewer, or a single clear word; phonetic rhythm is distinctive |
| 7-8 | Name recalled by 3 of 5 testers; 1-2 syllables; no common mispronunciation variants |
| 5-6 | Recalled correctly by 2 of 5 testers; name contains a common word that reduces distinctiveness in memory; 3+ syllables with no natural stress pattern |
| 3-4 | Difficult to spell from hearing alone; testers substitute similar-sounding words; compound of two generic words |
| 0-2 | Not recalled by any tester without a prompt; acronym with no inherent meaning; name is a string of letters chosen only for availability |

### Brand Fit

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Name expresses the brand personality and values without explanation; 5 of 5 testers from the target audience associate the name with the intended category and emotional register |
| 7-8 | Name is consistent with the brand personality; 3-4 of 5 testers make the intended association with context; minor explanation needed for the connection |
| 5-6 | Name is neutral — neither contradicts nor expresses the brand; target audience testers see it as plausible but no stronger; requires logo/tagline to communicate fit |
| 3-4 | Name implies a different category, audience, or personality than intended; testers associate it with a competitor or adjacent space |
| 0-2 | Name actively contradicts the brand positioning; implies opposite values or targets the wrong audience segment |

### Distinctiveness

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | No competitors or adjacent brands use this name or close variants; name is categorically novel (invented or new combination); stands out in a category scan |
| 7-8 | Name is unique in the direct competitive set; one or two non-competing brands use a similar name in unrelated categories; still stands out in category context |
| 5-6 | Name uses a common word or pattern in the category (e.g., "-ly" suffix, "get" prefix); distinguishable but not distinctive; easy to confuse with adjacent brands |
| 3-4 | Name closely resembles two or more other brands in the category; requires visual differentiation to avoid confusion; Google search returns competitor results for the exact name |
| 0-2 | Name is identical or one character away from an established competitor; generic category descriptor with no differentiating element |

### Domain & Handle Availability

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | .com available and unregistered; primary social handles (@name) available on all target platforms; no cybersquatting activity on variants |
| 7-8 | .com taken but available for acquisition under $5,000 OR brand-specific TLD available (e.g., .io, .ai, .co); 3 of 4 primary social handles available |
| 5-6 | .com requires negotiation ($5,000-$25,000 estimated); alternative TLD available; 2 of 4 social handles available; some squatting on variants |
| 3-4 | .com requires significant acquisition ($25,000+) OR is held by an active business; only 1 social handle available; name requires a modifier in all digital contexts |
| 0-2 | .com unavailable and unacquirable; no acceptable TLD available; all major social handles taken by active accounts; brand would launch with permanent domain confusion |

### Trademark Risk

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | No conflicting registrations found in target jurisdictions (USPTO, EUIPO, target country registries) for relevant International Classes; preliminary counsel review confirms low risk |
| 7-8 | One registration found in an unrelated class (e.g., a clothing brand) with low likelihood of confusion; counsel confirms acceptable risk; no opposition activity on similar marks |
| 5-6 | One registration in an adjacent class with moderate likelihood of confusion; counsel recommends proceeding with monitoring; potential opposition risk within 3 years |
| 3-4 | Active registration in the same class in a target market; counsel advises against use without licensing; opposition proceeding likely; legal cost to proceed is significant |
| 0-2 | Direct conflict with a registered trademark in the same class and jurisdiction; use would constitute infringement; counsel advises rejection |

### Linguistic Safety

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Verified clean in all target language markets; native speaker review confirms no negative connotations, slang, or offensive meanings; pronunciation is consistent across major language families |
| 7-8 | Clean in primary markets; one minor pronunciation variation in a secondary market (e.g., vowel sound difference) that is not offensive; no negative connotations found |
| 5-6 | Pronunciation varies significantly in one target market causing confusion (not offence); or the name sounds like a mundane, unrelated word in one language |
| 3-4 | Name has mildly negative associations (slang meaning, awkward sound) in one significant target market; requires local marketing adjustment |
| 0-2 | Name translates to or sounds like an offensive, harmful, or embarrassing word in a target market; use would require immediate rebranding on market entry |
