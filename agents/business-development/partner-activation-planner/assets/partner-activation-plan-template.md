## Executive Summary

[2-3 sentences summarizing the partner, programme type, 90-day pipeline target, and the strategic rationale.
GUIDANCE: Lead with the expected commercial outcome. Do not describe the planning process.]

## Partner Fit Scorecard

[Assessment of partner across key fit dimensions.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table with dimension (customer overlap, technical complementarity, GTM alignment, strategic value), weight, score (1-5), weighted score, and evidence for each score
- Bad: "The partner is a good fit"
- Format: Scored table with evidence column. Include composite score and fit tier (Strong/Moderate/Weak). Reference: `references/framework.md`]

## Programme Design

[Activation programme type and revenue model.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Programme type (co-sell, co-market, technology integration, channel/reseller) with specific definition of joint activities. Revenue model (referral fee %, revenue share %, reseller margin %) with financial terms.
- Bad: "We will partner on sales"
- Format: Structured sections for programme type, revenue model, and joint activity descriptions]

## Milestone Plan

[Quarterly milestones with 90-day success gates.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table with milestone name, target date, metric (pipeline generated, leads sourced, integration delivered), target value, and success/failure criteria. Include explicit 90-day gate: "If [metric] < [threshold] at day 90, escalate for restructure decision"
- Bad: "Hit our targets each quarter"
- Format: Timeline table with clear quantified targets per milestone]

## Mutual Enablement Plan

[What each side delivers to enable the other.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Two-column layout (We Deliver / They Deliver) with specific items, owners, and deadlines. Include training sessions, documentation, demo environments, battle cards, and co-branded content.
- Bad: "Both sides will enable each other"
- Format: Table with item, owner (our side), owner (their side), deadline, and status]

## Governance Framework

[Operating cadence and escalation structure.

GUIDANCE:
- Good: Table with meeting type (weekly sync, monthly review, quarterly exec), attendees, agenda items, frequency, and owner. Separate section for escalation path with levels and SLA.
- Bad: "We will meet regularly"
- Format: Meeting cadence table plus escalation path diagram/table]

## Recommendations

[Pre-activation preparation tasks.

GUIDANCE: Each recommendation should be:
- Specific (not "prepare materials" but "create co-branded solution brief with [partner name] positioning by [date]")
- Actionable (assignable to BD, Marketing, Product, or Engineering)
- Prioritized (P1 = blocks activation / P2 = degrades activation / P3 = nice-to-have)]
