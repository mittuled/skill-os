# Scoring Rubric: assumption-mapper

Evaluates the completeness and rigour of an assumption mapping exercise covering categorisation, scoring, risk matrix construction, and validation planning.

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Assumption Coverage | 25% | Breadth and accuracy of assumptions across desirability, feasibility, viability, usability, and adaptability domains |
| 2 | Confidence-Impact Scoring | 25% | Quality of evidence-based scoring on confidence and impact axes with justified ratings |
| 3 | Risk Matrix Quality | 20% | Clarity of the 2x2 risk matrix with correctly plotted assumptions and actionable quadrant labels |
| 4 | Validation Plan Rigour | 30% | Appropriateness and cost-efficiency of proposed validation methods for critical assumptions |

| **Total** | | **100%** | |

## Scale

Each criterion is scored **0-10**:
- **0**: No evidence / completely absent
- **5**: Partially present with significant gaps
- **10**: Fully present, comprehensive, no gaps

**Composite Score** = Σ (criterion score × weight)

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Composite Score | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------|----------------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| A+ | 9.0 – 10.0 | Exceptional | All five domains covered with 8+ assumptions, evidence-backed scoring, clear risk matrix, and lean validation methods with cost and timeline estimates | Proceed to MVP scoping using the assumption map |
| A | 8.0 – 8.9 | Strong | Four or more domains covered, scoring justified, matrix clear, validation plan actionable | Minor refinements then proceed to MVP scoping |
| B | 7.0 – 7.9 | Good | Most domains covered, scoring reasonable, matrix readable, validation methods identified | Review gaps in one domain before proceeding |
| C | 5.0 – 6.9 | Adequate | Three domains covered, some scoring lacks evidence, matrix present but cluttered, validation methods generic | Facilitate a workshop to fill domain gaps and sharpen validation methods |
| D | 3.0 – 4.9 | Weak | Fewer than three domains covered, flat list without meaningful scoring, no matrix, vague validation ideas | Restart with a structured assumption extraction session |
| F | 0.0 – 2.9 | Failing | No structured assumption mapping performed | Block further investment until assumption mapping is complete |

## Signal Tables

### Assumption Coverage

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | 8-15 assumptions extracted across all five domains (desirability, feasibility, viability, usability, adaptability) with clear phrasing as testable propositions |
| 7-8 | Assumptions cover four domains with 6-10 entries, each phrased as a testable statement |
| 5-6 | Assumptions cover three domains but some are phrased as opinions rather than testable propositions |
| 3-4 | Assumptions listed for one or two domains only, or phrased as facts rather than hypotheses |
| 0-2 | No assumptions documented or all entries are solution descriptions rather than assumptions |

### Confidence-Impact Scoring

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Every assumption rated on both confidence (with cited evidence) and impact (with consequence description), using a consistent high/medium/low scale |
| 7-8 | Most assumptions scored on both axes with evidence citations for confidence ratings |
| 5-6 | Assumptions scored but evidence for confidence ratings is missing or inconsistent |
| 3-4 | Partial scoring with only one axis rated, or ratings applied without any supporting evidence |
| 0-2 | No scoring applied to assumptions |

### Risk Matrix Quality

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Clear 2x2 matrix with labeled axes and quadrants, all scored assumptions correctly plotted, top 3-5 critical assumptions highlighted in the low-confidence/high-impact quadrant |
| 7-8 | Matrix present with correct axis labelling, most assumptions plotted, critical zone identified |
| 5-6 | Matrix drawn but some assumptions misplotted or critical zone not explicitly called out |
| 3-4 | Matrix attempted but axes mislabelled or assumptions clustered without differentiation |
| 0-2 | No risk matrix produced |

### Validation Plan Rigour

| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Each critical assumption paired with the cheapest viable validation method (fake-door, survey, data pull, prototype test), with estimated cost, timeline, owner, and success criteria |
| 7-8 | Validation methods assigned to critical assumptions with cost and timeline but missing success criteria or owner |
| 5-6 | Validation methods identified but not matched to cost-efficiency; some methods disproportionately expensive for the assumption being tested |
| 3-4 | Generic validation suggestions ("do more research") without specific methods, costs, or timelines |
| 0-2 | No validation plan produced |
