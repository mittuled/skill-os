# Scoring Rubric: Vendor Performance Reviewer

## Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Scale | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------------|
| SLA Adherence | 30% | 0-10 | Compliance with contractual SLA targets: uptime, response time, resolution time |
| Responsiveness | 20% | 0-10 | Speed and quality of vendor communication: escalation handling, proactive updates |
| Delivery Quality | 25% | 0-10 | Quality of deliverables: defect rates, rework frequency, consistency |
| Value for Money | 15% | 0-10 | Cost-effectiveness relative to market alternatives and delivered outcomes |
| Relationship & Collaboration | 10% | 0-10 | Partnership behaviour: roadmap alignment, willingness to co-invest, transparency |
| **Total** | **100%** | | |

## Grade Bands

| Grade | Score Range | Label | Action |
|-------|-----------|-------|--------|
| A+ | 90-100 | Excellent | Proceed with confidence |
| A | 75-89 | Good | Minor concerns only |
| B | 60-74 | Acceptable | Address flagged items |
| C | 40-59 | Caution | Significant risks |
| D | 20-39 | High Risk | Consider alternatives |
| F | 0-19 | Unacceptable | Do not proceed |

## Signal Tables

### SLA Adherence
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | All SLA targets met or exceeded for the full review period, zero breaches |
| 7-8 | SLA targets met 95%+ of the time, breaches were minor and quickly remediated |
| 5-6 | SLA targets met 85-94% of the time, a few notable breaches with impact |
| 3-4 | SLA targets met less than 85%, repeated breaches in the same area, slow remediation |
| 1-2 | Systematic SLA failures, vendor disputes SLA measurements, no improvement trend |

### Responsiveness
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Responds within SLA on every ticket, proactively communicates issues before they escalate, named contacts always reachable |
| 7-8 | Consistent response times, escalations handled smoothly, occasional proactive communication |
| 5-6 | Generally responsive but occasional delays, escalations require follow-up to progress |
| 3-4 | Frequent delays in response, escalations stall without intervention, communication is reactive only |
| 1-2 | Regularly unresponsive, escalations ignored or deprioritized, no clear point of contact |

### Delivery Quality
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Deliverables consistently exceed expectations, near-zero defect rate, no rework required |
| 7-8 | Deliverables meet expectations, low defect rate, rework is rare and minor |
| 5-6 | Deliverables generally acceptable, moderate defect rate, occasional rework needed |
| 3-4 | Deliverables frequently below standard, high defect rate, significant rework cycles |
| 1-2 | Deliverables routinely unacceptable, persistent quality failures, rework is the norm |

### Value for Money
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Pricing is competitive, vendor delivers above-market value, total cost of ownership is demonstrably lower than alternatives |
| 7-8 | Pricing is at market rate, value delivered aligns with cost, no significant hidden charges |
| 5-6 | Pricing is slightly above market, value is adequate but not differentiated, some unexpected costs |
| 3-4 | Pricing is above market with declining value, frequent change orders, hidden costs eroding ROI |
| 1-2 | Overpriced relative to delivered value, vendor captures value through lock-in rather than performance |

### Relationship & Collaboration
| Score | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | Vendor acts as a strategic partner, shares roadmap, co-invests in solutions, transparent about challenges |
| 7-8 | Good working relationship, vendor is open to feedback and makes adjustments |
| 5-6 | Transactional relationship, vendor is cooperative but does not proactively invest in the partnership |
| 3-4 | Strained relationship, vendor is defensive about issues, limited transparency |
| 1-2 | Adversarial relationship, vendor withholds information, blames customer for problems |
