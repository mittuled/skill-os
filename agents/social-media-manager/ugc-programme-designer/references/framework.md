# Framework: ugc-programme-designer

This framework defines the methodology for identifying, acquiring rights to, amplifying, and sustaining a user-generated content programme.

## UGC Identification Methods

| Method | Description | Best For | Signal Strength |
|--------|-------------|---------|----------------|
| Brand hashtag monitoring | Monitor programme hashtag and brand handle tags | High-volume consumer programmes | Medium — requires active campaign promotion |
| Social listening queries | Monitor brand name + product mentions without prompting | Organic advocacy discovery | High — unprompted content is most authentic |
| NPS promoter follow-up | Reach out to NPS score 9–10 respondents for content | B2B customer stories | High — high-trust starting point |
| Community platform scan | Monitor community forum, Slack, or Discord for shareable stories | SaaS community programmes | High — captures in-depth use cases |
| CS relationship mining | CS team surfaces customer wins that warrant public sharing | Enterprise customer stories | Very High — pre-qualified, relationship-backed |
| Review platform harvest | Monitor G2, Capterra, Trustpilot for shareable quotes | Social proof for ads and website | Medium — requires rights acquisition per use |

## Contributor Segment Prioritisation

| Segment | Identification | Content Potential | Outreach Approach |
|---------|---------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Power users (daily active, high feature usage) | Product analytics | High — deep product knowledge | Direct outreach via CS or in-product |
| NPS promoters (score 9–10) | CS survey data | High — motivated to advocate | Personalised CS follow-up |
| Community moderators and contributors | Community platform | High — visible influence on peers | Community manager direct outreach |
| Social advocates (organic brand mention history) | Social listening | Medium — already creating content | Social engagement + programme invite |
| New customers (recent win, strong implementation) | CRM + CS | Medium — fresh success story | CS-facilitated intro |

## Rights Acquisition Process

Rights must be acquired before repurposing any user-created content in branded contexts.

| Use Case | Rights Required | Acquisition Method |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Organic reshare on brand social | Basic repost rights | Public comment on original post: "May we share this? ^[Name]" + explicit "yes" reply |
| Website testimonial | Written testimonial rights | Email approval with specific use case stated |
| Paid ad creative | Full commercial use rights | Signed release form with usage scope, duration, and compensation (if any) |
| Case study or video | Comprehensive content rights | Legal release covering all derived content |
| Sales deck or enablement | Internal use rights | Informal written approval from contributor |

**Rights log**: Maintain a rights log with contributor name, content URL, approval date, rights granted, and expiry (if applicable).

## Amplification Tiers

| Tier | Criteria | Amplification Actions | Contributor Recognition |
|------|---------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Tier 1 — Showcase | High-quality, on-message, strong results story | Feature on homepage, dedicated case study, paid amplification, PR pitch | Public shoutout, gift/swag, conference speaking invite |
| Tier 2 — Amplify | Good quality, relevant story, shareable format | Reshare on brand channels, include in email campaigns, add to resource library | Public reshare with tag, thank-you DM |
| Tier 3 — Curate | Adequate quality, positive sentiment | Add to internal UGC library for future use, private acknowledgement | Thank-you DM or email |
| Tier 4 — Archive | Off-brand, low quality, or not safe for use | Archive for reference only, do not amplify | No public acknowledgement |

## Programme Mechanics: Incentive and Participation Design

| Mechanic | Description | Best For | Risk |
|----------|-------------|---------|------|
| Hashtag campaign | Defined hashtag + content prompt + prize | High-volume consumer or prosumer audiences | Low signal-to-noise ratio if prize attracts non-users |
| Ambassador programme | Tiered recognition system (e.g., Bronze/Silver/Gold) for repeat contributors | B2B community building | Requires sustained management; perception of fake advocacy if not authentic |
| Content template prompts | Provide a template (e.g., "How I use [Product] to [outcome]") to lower barrier | First-time contributors; specific use case capture | May reduce authenticity if over-structured |
| Customer story series | Structured interview-based series with co-created content | Enterprise customers; high-value stories | High effort per story; requires CS coordination |
| In-product prompts | Trigger a share prompt after key product moments (milestone, first value) | SaaS products with quantifiable outcomes | Requires product engineering support |

## Community Building Loop

A healthy UGC programme sustains itself through a reinforcing loop:

1. **Seed**: Publish high-quality first-party content to set the quality bar
2. **Invite**: Personally invite power users and NPS promoters to contribute
3. **Amplify**: Feature contributor content visibly on owned channels with full attribution
4. **Recognise**: Acknowledge contributors publicly and privately
5. **Reward**: Provide tangible or status-based incentives for top contributors
6. **Feedback**: Share engagement metrics with contributors to demonstrate impact
7. **Sustain**: Regularly surface new contributors through listening and CS relationships

## Programme Health Metrics

| Metric | Calculation | Healthy Target | Action if Below |
|--------|------------|---------------|----------------|
| Submission rate | New UGC submissions / month | ≥ 10 quality submissions/month | Increase contributor outreach, add incentive |
| Amplification rate | Amplified pieces / total submissions | ≥ 50% | Review quality bar; add content templates |
| Contributor retention | Contributors active in last 3 months / all-time contributors | ≥ 30% | Strengthen recognition and reward programme |
| Engagement lift | Avg engagement on UGC posts vs. brand-created posts | UGC ≥ 20% higher | If below, improve curator selection criteria |
| Conversion attribution | UGC-featuring pages: avg conversion rate vs. non-UGC pages | ≥ 15% lift | A/B test UGC placement and format |
