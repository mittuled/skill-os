# market-research-synthesiser

## Agent: Applied Research Lead

L1 applied research leader (1x) responsible for market research synthesis, technology trend analysis, competitive deep dives, benchmarking, and contributing to the research roadmap.

Department ethos: [ideal-applied-research.md](../../../departments/applied-research/ideal-applied-research.md)

## Skill Description

The market research synthesiser consolidates findings from multiple primary and secondary research sources into a unified, actionable synthesis that informs strategic decisions about market entry, positioning, and product direction.

## When to Use

- When multiple research inputs (surveys, interviews, reports, analytics) need to be combined into a coherent view.
- When strategic planning requires an updated market landscape summary.
- When research sources provide conflicting signals and a reconciled perspective is needed.
- When a new market opportunity needs validation through synthesised evidence.

## Workflow

1. **Inventory sources**: Catalogue all available research inputs (customer interviews, surveys, analyst reports, market data, internal analytics). Deliverable: source inventory with recency and reliability ratings.
2. **Extract key findings**: Pull the most relevant findings from each source related to the synthesis question. Deliverable: extracted findings log.
3. **Reconcile conflicts**: Identify where sources disagree and determine which signals are more reliable based on methodology and recency. Deliverable: conflict reconciliation notes.
4. **Synthesise themes**: Combine findings into coherent themes: market size, growth drivers, customer segments, competitive dynamics, and unmet needs. Deliverable: thematic synthesis document.
5. **Derive implications**: Translate themes into strategic implications for the company's product, positioning, and go-to-market. Deliverable: strategic implications memo.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Cherry-picking data**: Selecting only research that supports a pre-existing narrative. *Why*: biased synthesis misleads strategy and erodes trust in the research function.
- **Synthesising stale inputs**: Combining research older than 12 months without noting its limitations. *Why*: markets move fast; stale data produces conclusions that may no longer be true.
- **Synthesis without source transparency**: Presenting conclusions without citing which sources support them. *Why*: stakeholders cannot assess confidence levels or challenge specific findings if sources are hidden.

## Output

**On success**: A market research synthesis document containing thematic findings, conflict reconciliation, strategic implications, and source citations, formatted for executive consumption.

**On failure**: Report which research gaps prevented complete synthesis (e.g., no primary research on a key segment), what partial synthesis was produced, and recommend research to fill gaps.
