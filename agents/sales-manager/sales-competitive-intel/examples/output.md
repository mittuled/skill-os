## Battle Card: Workday Analytics

### Overview

Enterprise HCM suite with embedded analytics. Strong in HRIS-attached deals. Recent Prism Analytics launch strengthened their standalone analytics story.

### Where They Win (Acknowledge Honestly)

- Deep HRIS integration — zero data migration for existing Workday customers
- Large enterprise trust and brand recognition
- Workday Prism closes gap on custom dashboards (NEW — this is why win rate dropped)

### Where We Win

- Workday Prism requires expensive PS engagement and 4-6 months to configure — we deliver in 14 days
- No real-time data — Workday batch refresh only; PeopleMetrics is real-time
- Purpose-built for analytics — not a module inside an HR system

### Trap Questions to Ask Prospects

1. "Are you already on Workday HCM?" — establishes whether switching costs are real
2. "How important is having analytics inside your HR system of record?" — surfaces consolidation buyers
3. "What's your IT team's bandwidth for a new analytics implementation?" — exposes timeline risk

### Landmines to Avoid

- Do NOT discuss total implementation cost — Workday responds with TCO arguments that favor their platform
- Avoid feature-by-feature comparisons — Workday has more features; win on speed and flexibility

### Talk Tracks

**When prospect is on Workday HCM:**
"Our customers who run Workday tell us Prism Analytics takes 4-6 months to configure and requires SI involvement. PeopleMetrics connects to Workday in a day and delivers first insights in week 1."

**When prospect likes Workday brand:**
"Workday is a great system of record. PeopleMetrics is purpose-built for analytics — it's the difference between using Excel to run a presentation vs. PowerPoint."

### Proof Points

- **Deel:** Replaced Workday Prism — 14-day implementation vs. 6-month Prism rollout
- **Rippling customer:** 60% reduction in manual reporting hours vs. Workday embedded reports

## Win/Loss Patterns

**We win when:**
- Prospect frustrated with Workday implementation timelines
- Decision made by Analytics/Data team (not IT or HR IT)
- Prospect is NOT deep in the Workday ecosystem

**We lose when:**
- IT insists on single-vendor strategy
- Procurement has an existing Workday enterprise agreement
- Champion is the Workday admin

**Dangerous deal stages:** Technical Evaluation, Negotiation

**Action:** Flag any deal where the champion is a Workday admin or IT lead — escalate to AE manager for executive alignment play before technical evaluation begins.
