# Framework: Sales Playbook Construction

## Purpose

Provides the methodology for building a sales playbook that reps actually use, covering input gathering, component design, and quality standards.

## Input Gathering Method

### Source Priority

| Source | What to Extract | Priority |
|--------|----------------|----------|
| Opportunity Frame (VP Sales) | ICP, buyer personas, motion type, competitive positioning | P1 -- foundational |
| PMM Positioning | Value proposition, messaging framework, competitive differentiation | P1 -- messaging anchor |
| Win/Loss Data | Patterns in why deals close or die, objection themes, competitor frequency | P1 -- evidence base |
| Top-Rep Interviews | What they actually say, questions they ask, techniques that work | P1 -- field reality |
| SE Feedback | Technical objections, demo best practices, POC patterns | P2 -- technical layer |
| CS Feedback | Post-sale surprises, onboarding friction, retention drivers | P2 -- long-term view |

### Top-Rep Interview Protocol

Interview 2-3 highest-performing reps (by win rate, not just revenue). Ask:

1. "Walk me through your last 3 wins from first touch to close. What did you do differently?"
2. "What's the first question you ask in every discovery call? Why?"
3. "When you hear [common objection], what do you actually say?"
4. "What competitor do you see most? How do you position against them?"
5. "What information do you wish you had at the start of every deal?"
6. "What part of our current sales process slows you down or doesn't help?"

Record and transcribe. Mine for patterns, not prescriptions.

## MEDDIC Qualification Design

### Implementation Principles

1. **Tailor definitions to your product**: "Metrics" for a security product means risk reduction quantified; for a productivity tool it means time saved. Make each element specific.
2. **Define discovery questions per element**: 2-3 open-ended questions that naturally uncover the information without feeling like an interrogation.
3. **Provide "what good looks like"**: Concrete examples from real deals showing what a strong answer sounds like.
4. **Define red flags**: Specific answers or behaviors that signal the element is weak (e.g., "Champion says 'I'll handle it internally' but cannot name the economic buyer").
5. **Score conservatively**: Score based on buyer-confirmed evidence, not seller assumption.

### Scoring Matrix

| Score | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 0 | Not discovered | No information on economic buyer identity |
| 1 | Seller assumption | "I think the CFO approves" (not buyer-confirmed) |
| 2 | Buyer-confirmed | "Our CFO, Jane Smith, approves all software >$50K" (buyer stated) |
| 3 | Validated | Met with CFO Jane Smith, she confirmed budget and timeline |

## Discovery Question Design Principles

### Structure: Persona x Phase

Organize questions in a matrix:

| Phase | Purpose | Example Starter |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Pain Discovery | Uncover the specific problem and its impact | "What's not working about your current approach to [X]?" |
| Impact Quantification | Attach numbers to the pain | "What does this cost you in [time/money/risk] per [period]?" |
| Decision Process | Map how they buy | "Walk me through how your team evaluated the last tool you purchased" |
| Timeline Validation | Understand urgency | "What happens if this doesn't get solved in the next [period]?" |
| Champion Development | Identify and activate internal advocate | "Who else in your org is feeling this pain?" |

### Question Quality Criteria

- **Open-ended**: Cannot be answered with yes/no
- **Non-leading**: Does not suggest the "right" answer
- **Persona-appropriate**: Uses language the buyer uses, not internal jargon
- **Progressive**: Each question builds on the previous answer
- **Actionable**: The answer directly informs a MEDDIC element or deal strategy

## Battle Card Design Standard

Each battle card must contain:

| Section | Content | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Positioning | One sentence: who they are and how they position | 1 sentence |
| Strengths | Where they genuinely beat us (honesty builds rep trust) | 3-5 bullets |
| Weaknesses | Where they fall short (specific, evidence-based) | 3-5 bullets |
| Landmines | Questions to ask in discovery that expose their weakness | 3-5 questions |
| Trap Questions | Questions buyers should ask them that they cannot answer well | 2-3 questions |
| Why We Win | Specific deal evidence of beating them | 2-3 examples |
| Why We Lose | Specific deal evidence of losing to them | 2-3 examples |
| Counter-Talk | What to say when a prospect mentions them | 3-5 sentence framework |

## Playbook Adoption Principles

1. **Length limit**: Core playbook under 30 pages. Supplementary materials linked, not inlined.
2. **Searchable**: Every section titled for scanning. Reps find answers in <30 seconds.
3. **Example-rich**: Every framework illustrated with a real deal example.
4. **Living document**: Quarterly review cadence. Changelog visible. Version numbered.
5. **Rep-authored**: At least 30% of content sourced from top-rep interviews.
