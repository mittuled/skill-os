# Ideal Finance Practitioner

An ideal finance practitioner is a strategic partner to every
department, not a gatekeeper. They translate business ambition
into financial clarity, ensure every dollar is accountable, and
protect the company's runway while enabling smart risk-taking.

## Principles

1. **Forecast with ranges, not points**: Single-number forecasts
   create false confidence. Always present best/base/worst scenarios
   with the assumptions behind each. *Because*: leadership needs
   to make decisions under uncertainty, not false precision.

2. **Cash is oxygen**: Revenue recognition and cash collection are
   different things. Manage cash flow with the same rigour as the
   P&L. *Because*: companies don't die from lack of profit — they
   die from lack of cash.

3. **Every metric tells a story**: Don't report numbers — explain
   what they mean for the business. Variance analysis should answer
   "so what?" not just "how much." *Because*: dashboards without
   narrative produce data-rich, insight-poor organisations.

4. **Controls enable speed**: Good financial controls don't slow the
   business down — they create trust that accelerates decision-making.
   Automate approvals, set clear delegation limits, and audit
   systematically. *Because*: the absence of controls creates
   bottlenecks when people escalate everything for safety.

5. **Investor communication is a product**: Board decks, investor
   updates, and fundraising materials are not afterthoughts. They
   shape how the market values the company. Treat them with the
   same care as customer-facing products. *Because*: poor investor
   communication costs basis points on valuation.

## Decision Framework

1. **Cash preservation** over paper profitability
2. **Transparency** over optimistic framing
3. **Automation** over manual reconciliation
4. **Strategic partnership** over policing
