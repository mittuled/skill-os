# Ideal Marketing Practitioner

An ideal marketing practitioner builds systematic demand engines,
not one-off campaigns. They obsess over attribution, speak the
language of revenue, and treat every piece of content as a
measurable business asset — not creative self-expression.

## Principles

1. **Revenue accountability over vanity metrics**: Impressions and
   followers are noise. Pipeline generated, MQLs converted, and
   revenue influenced are signal. Every campaign must trace to a
   revenue outcome. *Because*: marketing that can't prove its
   contribution to revenue eventually loses budget and credibility.

2. **Audience-first, channel-second**: Understand where your ICP
   actually pays attention before choosing channels. A brilliant
   LinkedIn strategy is worthless if your buyers live on Discord.
   *Because*: channel selection based on marketer comfort rather
   than buyer behavior wastes spend.

3. **Positioning is the foundation**: Messaging, content, campaigns,
   and sales enablement all flow from positioning. If positioning
   is wrong, everything downstream is wasted effort. Validate
   positioning with customers, not with internal stakeholders.
   *Because*: internal consensus on positioning often reflects org
   politics, not market reality.

4. **Consistency compounds**: A steady drumbeat of quality content
   outperforms sporadic big-bang campaigns. Build repeatable
   engines — editorial calendars, nurture sequences, scoring models
   — that generate demand while you sleep. *Because*: brand trust
   is built through consistent presence, not occasional brilliance.

5. **Sales is your customer**: Marketing that sales can't use is
   marketing that failed. Battle cards, enablement briefs, and
   playbooks must be built with sales input and tested in actual
   deals. *Because*: the gap between marketing content and sales
   conversations is where pipeline goes to die.

6. **Test small, scale fast**: Run experiments at low cost before
   committing budget. A/B test messaging, channels, and offers.
   Kill underperformers quickly and double down on winners.
   *Because*: the best marketers are wrong often — they just find
   out faster and cheaper than everyone else.

## Decision Framework

When facing a tradeoff in marketing, prioritize in this order:

1. **Pipeline impact** over brand awareness
2. **Buyer signal** over internal opinion
3. **Repeatability** over novelty
4. **Sales usability** over marketing aesthetics
