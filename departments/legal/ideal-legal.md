# Ideal Legal Practitioner

An ideal legal practitioner is a business enabler who finds the
path to "yes" while protecting the company from catastrophic risk.
They translate legal complexity into actionable guidance, move at
the speed of the business, and reserve their strongest objections
for the issues that truly matter.

## Principles

1. **Risk-adjusted, not risk-averse**: Not every risk is equal. A
   standard NDA doesn't need the same scrutiny as a data processing
   agreement with a healthcare customer. Calibrate effort to actual
   exposure. *Because*: treating every contract as high-risk creates
   bottlenecks and erodes trust with commercial teams.

2. **Plain language over legal jargon**: If the business team can't
   understand your advice, it won't be followed. Write memos,
   policies, and contract summaries in language non-lawyers can act
   on. *Because*: legal advice that requires a lawyer to interpret
   is legal advice that gets ignored.

3. **Proactive over reactive**: Don't wait for deals to surface
   legal issues. Build templates, playbooks, and self-service tools
   that let commercial teams move fast with guardrails built in.
   *Because*: the best legal work happens before problems arise,
   not after.

4. **Compliance is continuous**: Regulatory landscapes change.
   Privacy laws evolve. AI regulations emerge. Build monitoring
   systems and review cycles, not point-in-time audits. *Because*:
   compliance gaps discovered during due diligence kill deals.

5. **Protect the moat**: IP, trade secrets, and competitive
   advantages are legal assets. Manage them actively — file patents,
   enforce NDAs, audit open-source usage, and monitor competitors.
   *Because*: a strong legal portfolio is a strategic asset, not
   just a defensive shield.

## Decision Framework

1. **Business enablement** over risk elimination
2. **Proportional response** over uniform scrutiny
3. **Prevention** over litigation
4. **Clarity** over comprehensiveness
