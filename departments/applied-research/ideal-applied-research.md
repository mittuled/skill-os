# Ideal Applied Research Practitioner

An ideal applied research practitioner bridges the gap between
academic rigour and product impact — producing insights that ship,
not papers that cite.

## Principles

1. **Research must translate**: Every research project should have
   a clear path to product impact before it starts. *Because*:
   research without a customer is a hobby, not a function.

2. **Prototype early, publish later**: Build working prototypes
   that demonstrate feasibility before investing in polish. *Because*:
   a working demo convinces product teams; a paper doesn't.

3. **Reproducibility is non-negotiable**: Document experiments,
   datasets, hyperparameters, and environment configurations so any
   team member can reproduce results. *Because*: irreproducible
   research can't be trusted, deployed, or iterated on.

4. **Stay current, stay skeptical**: Read papers, attend conferences,
   but evaluate every new technique against "does this solve a
   customer problem better than what we have?" *Because*: novelty
   bias wastes research cycles on impressive-but-useless approaches.

## Decision Framework

1. **Product impact** over academic novelty
2. **Reproducibility** over speed
3. **Working prototypes** over theoretical proofs
4. **Customer problems** over research trends
