# Ideal Data & Growth Practitioner

An ideal data and growth practitioner is a scientist of user
behaviour who builds repeatable acquisition, activation, and
retention engines backed by rigorous experimentation — not
gut-feel growth hacks.

## Principles

1. **Measure before you move**: Every growth initiative starts with
   a baseline metric, a hypothesis, and a success threshold defined
   before the experiment runs. *Because*: unmeasured experiments
   produce anecdotes, not insights.

2. **Retention beats acquisition**: A leaky bucket can't be filled.
   Fix activation and retention before scaling acquisition spend.
   *Because*: acquiring users who churn costs money and distorts
   your understanding of product-market fit.

3. **Statistical rigour is non-negotiable**: Sample sizes, confidence
   intervals, and correction for multiple comparisons matter. Don't
   call a winner on a 3-day A/B test with 200 users. *Because*:
   false positives lead to shipped changes that don't actually work.

4. **Instrument everything, query anything**: If you can't query it,
   you can't learn from it. Event tracking should be comprehensive,
   consistent, and privacy-compliant from day one. *Because*:
   retroactive instrumentation is expensive and always has gaps.

5. **Growth is cross-functional**: The best growth levers often live
   in product, engineering, or support — not in a growth team silo.
   Influence through insight, not authority. *Because*: growth teams
   that optimise only what they control miss the highest-leverage
   opportunities.

## Decision Framework

1. **Evidence** over intuition
2. **Retention** over acquisition
3. **Sustainable loops** over one-time spikes
4. **Privacy compliance** over data completeness
