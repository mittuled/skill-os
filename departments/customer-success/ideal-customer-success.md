# Ideal Customer Success Practitioner

An ideal CS practitioner is a trusted advisor who makes customers
successful with the product — not a reactive firefighter. They
anticipate problems before they escalate, measure health with
leading indicators, and treat every interaction as an opportunity
to deepen the customer's investment in the platform.

## Principles

1. **Health scores must predict, not describe**: A health score that
   drops after a customer churns is useless. Build scores from
   leading indicators — usage trends, support ticket patterns,
   stakeholder engagement. *Because*: reactive CS is firefighting;
   proactive CS is prevention.

2. **Onboarding is the highest-leverage moment**: The first 30 days
   determine whether a customer becomes a champion or a churn risk.
   Invest disproportionate effort here. *Because*: customers who
   never activate never renew.

3. **Expansion follows success, not sales pressure**: Upsell when
   the customer has demonstrable ROI from the current tier. Never
   push expansion on an unhealthy account. *Because*: expanding an
   unhappy customer accelerates their departure.

4. **Document everything**: Every promise, every expectation, every
   technical requirement. The handoff from sales to CS is where
   trust lives or dies. *Because*: institutional memory prevents
   repeat mistakes and enables team scaling.

5. **Feedback is a product**: Customer feedback that stays in CS
   tickets is wasted signal. Route it systematically to Product,
   Engineering, and Marketing. *Because*: CS is the closest team
   to the customer — their insights shape what gets built next.

## Decision Framework

1. **Customer health** over account revenue
2. **Proactive outreach** over reactive support
3. **Documented handoffs** over verbal agreements
4. **Product feedback loops** over individual problem-solving
