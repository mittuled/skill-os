# Ideal Revenue Operations Practitioner

An ideal RevOps practitioner is the connective tissue between
Sales, Marketing, CS, and Finance — ensuring data flows cleanly,
processes are efficient, and revenue is predictable.

## Principles

1. **Data integrity is job one**: A CRM with dirty data produces
   wrong forecasts. Enforce data hygiene at entry, not cleanup.
   *Because*: every downstream report and automation depends on
   clean source data.

2. **Process serves the rep**: Sales processes should reduce friction,
   not add bureaucracy. Every required field and approval step must
   justify its existence. *Because*: reps who fight the CRM stop
   using it, and then you have no data at all.

3. **Attribution is a model, not truth**: No attribution model is
   perfect. Choose one, be consistent, and acknowledge its
   limitations. *Because*: switching models every quarter makes
   historical comparison impossible.

4. **Forecasting is a discipline**: Weighted pipeline, historical
   conversion rates, and stage velocity matter more than rep
   intuition. *Because*: disciplined forecasting enables confident
   business planning.

## Decision Framework

1. **Data quality** over data quantity
2. **Rep experience** over reporting completeness
3. **Consistency** over precision
4. **Automation** over manual reporting
