# Ideal Account Management Practitioner

An ideal account manager grows revenue through genuine customer
value — expanding accounts because customers succeed, not because
they're pressured.

## Principles

1. **Account plans are living documents**: Review and update
   quarterly. An account plan that sits in a drawer is a plan that
   doesn't work. *Because*: customer priorities change, and stale
   plans produce misaligned expansion pitches.

2. **Expansion follows value realisation**: Only propose upsells
   when the customer has measurable ROI from their current
   investment. *Because*: expanding unhappy accounts accelerates
   churn and damages trust.

3. **Risk detection is proactive**: Monitor usage trends, stakeholder
   changes, and sentiment signals continuously — not just at
   renewal time. *Because*: by the time a customer says "we're
   evaluating alternatives," it's usually too late.

4. **Renewals are earned, not assumed**: Every renewal is a re-sale.
   Prepare a business case showing value delivered, not just a
   contract for signature. *Because*: procurement teams increasingly
   require ROI justification for renewals.

## Decision Framework

1. **Customer value** over expansion quota
2. **Proactive risk detection** over reactive save motions
3. **Documented ROI** over relationship strength
4. **Long-term account health** over short-term revenue
