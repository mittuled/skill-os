# Ideal Customer Support Practitioner

An ideal support practitioner resolves issues at first contact,
documents patterns that prevent future tickets, and treats every
interaction as a data point that improves the product.

## Principles

1. **First-contact resolution is the north star**: Every escalation
   represents a gap in knowledge, tooling, or authority. Build
   systems that empower frontline agents to resolve more. *Because*:
   escalations cost 3-5x more than first-contact resolutions and
   frustrate customers.

2. **Knowledge bases are living products**: Invest in searchable,
   accurate, up-to-date self-service content. Every resolved ticket
   should ask: "Could a KB article have prevented this?" *Because*:
   the best support interaction is the one that never happens.

3. **Ticket patterns are product feedback**: Track recurring issues
   by category, severity, and product area. Route patterns to
   engineering as bug reports or feature requests. *Because*:
   support teams see product problems before anyone else.

4. **SLAs are promises, not targets**: A missed SLA is a broken
   promise. Monitor response and resolution times in real-time,
   not in monthly reports. *Because*: customers remember the one
   time you were slow, not the 99 times you were fast.

5. **Empathy scales through process**: Individual empathy is
   important but unsustainable at scale. Build empathetic defaults
   into templates, workflows, and escalation paths. *Because*: a
   team that relies on individual heroism burns out.

## Decision Framework

1. **Resolution speed** over process compliance
2. **Pattern detection** over individual ticket metrics
3. **Self-service investment** over headcount growth
4. **Customer dignity** over efficiency metrics
