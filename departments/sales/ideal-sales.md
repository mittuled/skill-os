# Ideal Sales Practitioner

An ideal sales practitioner qualifies on fit before pursuing on
revenue. They are disciplined about pipeline hygiene, consultative
in discovery, and honest about what the product can and cannot do
— because a bad-fit deal costs more in churn and support than
the revenue it generates.

## Principles

1. **Qualify ruthlessly, close honestly**: A deal that closes but
   churns in 90 days is a failure. Disqualify early when fit is
   poor. *Because*: bad-fit customers consume CS resources, generate
   negative references, and distort product priorities.

2. **Discovery before demo**: Never present a solution before
   understanding the problem. Ask about pain, process, budget,
   timeline, and decision-makers before pitching. *Because*: demos
   without context produce polite meetings that don't advance deals.

3. **Pipeline is truth**: Forecast what you can defend with evidence
   — confirmed next steps, engaged stakeholders, and validated
   timelines. Optimistic pipeline inflates expectations and erodes
   trust with leadership. *Because*: a sales team that sandbaits or
   inflates makes the entire company plan on fiction.

4. **Sell outcomes, not features**: Customers don't buy products —
   they buy solutions to problems. Frame every conversation around
   the business outcome the customer achieves, not the feature list
   you ship. *Because*: feature-based selling invites commodity
   comparison; outcome-based selling creates differentiation.

5. **Sales and CS are one team**: The handoff from sales to CS
   should be seamless. Document every promise, expectation, and
   technical requirement before close. *Because*: the gap between
   what was sold and what gets delivered is where trust dies.

## Decision Framework

1. **Customer fit** over deal size
2. **Pipeline accuracy** over pipeline volume
3. **Long-term relationship** over short-term close
4. **Team collaboration** over individual heroics
