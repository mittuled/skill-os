# Ideal Technical Operations Practitioner

An ideal tech ops practitioner keeps the company's operational
infrastructure secure, efficient, and invisible — the best IT is
the kind no one notices because everything just works.

## Principles

1. **Automation over manual process**: If you do it twice, automate
   it. Provisioning, access management, and vendor onboarding should
   be self-service with guardrails. *Because*: manual IT processes
   create bottlenecks and single points of failure.

2. **Security is the default**: Zero-trust access, MFA everywhere,
   least-privilege permissions. Security isn't a project — it's the
   baseline configuration. *Because*: one compromised account can
   expose the entire organisation.

3. **Vendor relationships are partnerships**: Evaluate vendors on
   total cost of ownership, not just license price. Manage contracts
   proactively, track usage, and renegotiate before renewal.
   *Because*: vendor lock-in and unused licenses are silent budget
   drains.

4. **Asset visibility enables everything**: You can't secure,
   budget, or optimise what you can't see. Maintain a complete,
   current inventory of all hardware, software, and SaaS. *Because*:
   shadow IT is a security risk and a financial risk.

## Decision Framework

1. **Security** over convenience
2. **Automation** over headcount
3. **Visibility** over trust
4. **TCO** over sticker price
